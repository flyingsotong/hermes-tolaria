**Aug 20, 2025**
This transcript captures a productive initial coaching call focused on the coachee's journalism business.
**1. Coachee's Intention (What did the coachee want to talk about at the start of the meeting?)**
The coachee,**Karma Paljor**, wanted to discuss the**sustainability and monetization challenges** of his news business,**East Mojo**, which focuses on the neglected Northeast region of India.
- He immediately details the difficult ecosystem: a geographically cornered, small, and difficult-to-monetize market for digital ads.
- The primary challenge is being unable to**sustain the news business solely on journalism** revenue, forcing the team to take on production work (via Atwi Studios) and other sources to cover costs.
- He is**conflicted** about how to proceed, specifically focused on**growing subscriptions** as he has "lost faith in ads," but the current subscription attempts have been slow.
- His most pressing immediate concern is the**venture fund investor** who expects a return that hasn't materialized. Ultimately, he wants to focus only on**premium, in-depth journalism** without worrying about salaries.
**2. Coachee's New Awareness (What did the coachee learn at the end of it?)**
The coachee's new awareness centers on a fundamental**shift in perspective** from a "supply-side" (journalist) mindset to a "demand-side" (entrepreneur) mindset regarding his business.
| | |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Old Belief (Supply-Side) | New Awareness (Demand-Side/Mission-Driven) |
|**Focus:** Sell content (more stories, better investigative work) to get subscriptions. |**Focus:** Sell the _importance_ of the mission—being a credible, vital**beacon** for the Northeast that the mainstream media ignores. |
|**Problem:** Subscriptions are low because of a lack of hard-hitting stories, poor campaigns, and tech issues (payment gateway). |**Core Problem:** The coachee and his team haven't clearly defined or articulated**what people are truly buying** (which is _support_ for the mission and _pride_ in the outlet's existence), not just articles. |
|**Coaching Focus:** How to improve tech, run better campaigns, or find more resources. |**Coaching Focus:** How to validate and communicate the**demand** for their existence. This requires internal clarity first. |
**Key Learnings:**
1.**People are buying the mission, not just the articles:** The true supporters are buying a sense of**pride** and**support** for an important, necessary outlet.
2.**The team lacks clarity on the mission:** The coachee realized that if _he_ can't articulate _what_ they are selling (the mission), his small team is also**confused** and "running around like headless chicken."
3.**Need for internal alignment and transparency:** The coachee realized he needs to reverse his approach: stop focusing solely on _reaching_ subscribers and instead focus on**sitting down with the team** to define and articulate the mission together. He also considered being more**transparent** with the team about the financial challenges.
**3. Future (What could a follow-up conversation look like?)**
The follow-up conversation would likely be a review and accountability check on the first action items, focusing on the**mission-driven approach to fundraising/subscription**.
**Potential Agenda for Next Call (in two weeks, as suggested):**
1.**Mission Articulation Review:**
- What are the core 3-5 phrases or sentences the team is using to articulate the mission of East Mojo?
- How did the coachee integrate this messaging across the website and social media?
1.**Listening to True Fans (Demand Validation):**
- Did the coachee organize a session with their "truest fans" (lifetime subscribers/major supporters)?
- What exactly did the team hear that people are "buying" (their needs)? (e.g., "Educating the rest of India," "Maintaining a credible voice," "Community pride," etc.).
1.**Learning from NGOs (Operationalizing the Mission):**
- Did the coachee connect with Hina Khi/Impulse NGO or another mission-driven organization?
- What specific, practical**fundraising/mission communication tactics** did they learn and how will they apply them (e.g., public financial disclosures, specific "ask" language)?
The conversation would then likely pivot to creating an**actionable, mission-centric subscription campaign** based on the validated demand and the team's new, unified clarity.
