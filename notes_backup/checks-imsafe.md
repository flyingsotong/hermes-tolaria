The**IMSAFE** checklist is a mnemonic used by pilots as a personal health self-assessment before deciding whether they are fit to fly. It helps pilots evaluate their physical and mental condition to ensure they are not impaired in any way that could compromise flight safety.

**I - Illness:** Are you suffering from any illness or symptom, even a minor one like a cold or allergies? Illness can distract you, affect judgment, and impair performance.

**M - Medication:** Are you currently taking any prescription or over-the-counter medications? Many medications have side effects (like drowsiness, dizziness, or cognitive impairment) that are not compatible with flying safely. It's crucial to check if a medication is approved for flight, often by consulting an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME).

**S - Stress:** Are you experiencing unusual psychological stress? This could be from work, family, finances, or other life events. High stress levels can severely impact concentration, decision-making, and situational awareness.

**A - Alcohol:** Have you consumed alcohol recently? Regulations strictly prohibit flying within a certain timeframe after drinking (e.g., 8 hours in many regions) and flying with a blood alcohol content above a certain limit (e.g., 0.02% or 0.04% depending on jurisdiction). Hangovers also impair performance.

**F - Fatigue:** Are you tired or inadequately rested? Fatigue is a major human factor risk, significantly degrading reaction time, judgment, vigilance, and cognitive function. Consider both acute (short-term) and chronic (long-term) fatigue.

**E - Emotion / Eating:**Are you emotionally stable? Upsetting events or strong emotions (like anger, sadness, anxiety, or even extreme excitement) can be distracting and lead to poor decisions. Eating: Have you eaten properly and stayed hydrated? Low blood sugar from skipped meals or dehydration can cause dizziness, weakness, and reduced mental clarity.
