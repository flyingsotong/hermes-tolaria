Last week, I talked about "Context Engineering." This week, I want to talk about why most of us—even the AI-curious—are still stuck in the slow lane.
There is a fascinating paradox happening in software right now. A recent study by METR found that experienced developers using AI actually took**19% longer** to finish tasks. Meanwhile, over at Anthropic, their new coding tool was**90% written by itself.**
How can both be true? How can AI make individuals slower while making systems exponentially faster?
The answer lies in what Nate B. Jones calls the**"Dark Factory."** Most journalists are using AI as a "Spicy Autocomplete." But the real winners are building systems where the lights are turned off and the human only touches the "Spec."
**The 5 Levels of "Vibe Journalism"**
To understand where you are, we can adapt Dan Shapiro’s "Five Levels of Coding" framework for our own industry:
***Level 0: Spicy Autocomplete.** You write the story; AI suggests a better headline or fixes a typo. (Total human labor).
***Level 1: The Intern.** You ask AI to summarize a transcript or draft a sidebar. You review every word.
***Level 2: The Junior Reporter.** The AI drafts the whole story based on your notes. You spend an hour "fixing" its tone.**(This is where 90% of us are stuck, and it’s why we feel slower).**
***Level 3: The Editor-in-Chief.** You don't write. You direct. You give the AI a set of facts and a style guide, and you approve or reject the final product at the "feature" level.
***Level 4: The News Product Manager.** You write a**Specification** (The Spec). You describe the goal, the audience, and the constraints. You come back an hour later to see if the content passed your "Scenarios." You aren't reading for typos; you’re checking for *outcomes*.
***Level 5: The Dark Factory.** You’ve built a black box. Facts go in; verified, formatted, multi-platform news products come out. No human reviews the "copy" because the *system* is designed to be correct.

⠀The J-Curve: Why You’re Grinding Your Gears
If you feel like AI is "more work than it’s worth," you are likely at the bottom of the**J-Curve.** You’re running a Ferrari engine (GPT-5.3/Claude 4.6) on a horse-and-buggy transmission (the traditional reporting workflow). You’re trying to "edit" AI the way you edit a human. That is a low-leverage move.
The breakthrough happens when you stop being the**labor** and start being the**Architect of the Spec.**
**The "Scenario" Secret**
In the "Dark Factory" of software, they don't use traditional tests. They use**Scenarios.** ***Old way:** Check if the AI wrote "the" correctly.
***New way:** A "Scenario" lives outside the AI. It asks: *"Does this article contain the three specific budget figures from the PDF? If no, reject and rebuild."* This is the shift from**Process** to**Outcome.** When you define the *behavior* you want, the machine can iterate a thousand times until it hits it. Your job is no longer to move the pixels or the words—it’s to define the "Scenario" that proves the work is good.

⠀The Golden Opportunity: Revenue per Human
Look at the new breed of AI-native companies. They are generating**$3 million in revenue per employee.** That’s 5x the industry average.
This is the future of the "Micro-Media" empire. A single journalist with a "Dark Factory" mindset can run an information service that used to require a staff of twenty.
* The machine handles the**Implementation** (writing, formatting, distribution).
* The human handles the**Judgment** (what should we build, for whom, and why?).

⠀How to Take the Smallest Step Tomorrow
Don't try to build a factory overnight. Just move one level up the ladder:
1.**Write a "Spec," Not a Prompt:** Tomorrow, don't ask AI to "Write a 500-word post." Write a one-page "Specification." Define the goal, the tone, the 5 things it *must* include, and the 3 things it *must avoid*.
2.**Create a "Holdout Set":** Give the AI a transcript, but keep two "secret facts" to yourself. Ask the AI to write the story. If it doesn't mention the gaps, your "Spec" isn't tight enough.
3.**Invest in "Digital Twins":** Start a folder of your best work. Tell the AI: *"This is the Twin of my voice. Never deviate from this logic."*

⠀The bottleneck in journalism is no longer "writing speed." It is**clarity of thought.** The "Dark Factory" is waiting for your instructions.
What are you going to ask it to build?
