**Don’t assume you know what business you’re in.**

Drucker loved to ask executives “What business are you in?” because they often missed the mark, defining their organization in terms that were too narrow or not properly attuned to customers’ needs.

“That the question is so rarely asked—at least in a clear and sharp form—and so rarely given adequate study and thought, is perhaps the most important single cause of business failure,” Drucker wrote in The Practice of Management.

When you get to the fifth time you ask that question, the answer was that we were in the business of letting you belong anywhere at Airbnb.
