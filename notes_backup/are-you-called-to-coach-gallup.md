- Description: What is a life calling, and how can you find yours -- including whether it involves coaching others? Join Gallup's Dean Jones for insights on this important topic.

- This transcript is from a "Called to Coach" episode featuring Dean Jones discussing the concept of "calling," particularly in relation to coaching. Here are some of the key insights:

1. The origin of "Called to Coach": The term came from early observations that strengths coaches felt a sense of calling or mission in their work.

2. Definition of "calling": A strong urge toward a particular way of life or career, often giving life purpose and meaning.

3. Five attributes to determine if something is your calling:

 - It aligns with your talents

 - It's consistent with your values

 - It contributes to others

 - You have a unique insight into what's missing

 - It's exciting and fulfilling for you

4. Coaching vs. Teaching vs. Consulting: These are often blended in practice, especially in strengths coaching. Pure coaching is about drawing things out of people, while teaching and consulting involve putting information in front of people.

5. Community approach: Focus on your own practice rather than criticizing others' approaches. Embrace diversity within the coaching community.

6. Reflection on purpose: Richard Leider's work suggests that at the end of life, people wish they had been more reflective, more courageous, and had more purpose.

7. Gratitude: The importance of expressing thanks to people for the difference they've made in your life, without waiting until the end.

8. Life changes: Dean's personal accident reminded him that life can change instantly, emphasizing the importance of expressing love and appreciation daily.
