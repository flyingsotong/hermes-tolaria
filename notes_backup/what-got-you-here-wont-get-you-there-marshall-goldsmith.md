- Summary: Marshall Goldsmith's book "What Got You Here Won't Get You There" discusses how the habits and skills that have brought individuals success in the past may not be the ones that will lead to future success. Goldsmith, a coach, helps people identify and change personal habits that annoy coworkers, using 360-degree feedback and teaching them to listen, apologize, and express gratitude. He emphasizes that success can sometimes occur despite certain behaviors, not because of them, and that people often resist changing behaviors they mistakenly believe contribute to their success.

- The book also explores the idea that behavioral issues become more critical at higher levels of an organization, and that people only change when what they value is threatened. Goldsmith advises leaders to focus on what behaviors to stop, rather than new skills to start, and to consider the impact of their comments on others.
