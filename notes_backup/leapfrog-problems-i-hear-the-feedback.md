Leapfrog problems — i hear the feedback July 20, 2015

The Why — and why it’s important

Discussion principles:  - Build a strength-based team and outsource the rest to freelancers/partners

- Manage outcomes, not people

- Org charts are about about management of people, not outcomes

This is what the rundown looks like:

1. Reiterate and test: Mission, values, culture — what do people make of the latest mission. (10 mins)

2. Post Its on Culture: How do we get things done? What’s non-negotiable? (20 mins)

3. Top 3 challenges in my market that run counter to this mission/culture (20 mins)

4. Post Its on Stop, Continue, Build: What should we do? (30 mins)

5. Present: Guiding principles of Lean — history, case studies. Think, Make, Check, Repeat. Repeatable. (30 mins)

6. Value mapping — Where’s the value in the business? Run Revenue vs Scale/Support/Repeatability matrix. What doesn't add value? (30 mins)

7. Talent Discussion: If you were to build an agency startup today, how would you do it? What does the perfect org chart based on outcomes look like? Rebuild your org chart based on outcomes. Now map people to it. If you could build an A-Team for your most valuable client and you could use anyone inside Zeno, how would you structure it? (20 mins)

8. Zappos case study of flat orgs (30 mins)
