## Access note

The OurSG Grants portal (oursggrants.gov.sg/grants/new) is a JavaScript-rendered single-page app — static fetch returns no grant data, and the Chrome extension was unavailable during this automated run. Listings below are sourced from agency websites and web search results. This is a known limitation of the automated check; a manual visit to the portal will show the full current listings.

## What's current (sourced via agency sites and search)

**NAC Presentation & Participation Grant — consolidated & expanded**
Relevance: **Medium**
Effective 16 April 2026, the Production Grant and P&P (Extended Play) Grant have been folded into the P&P General Grant. The cap is now S$100,000 per applicant per financial year (up from S$50,000 for many categories), and applications can be submitted in any of the three General Grant cycles per year instead of once annually. Eligible costs include music EPs, publishing, exhibitions, performances, and festivals via physical and/or digital means. Individuals and non-profits get up to 70% co-funding; for-profit entities up to 50%.
[Apply via OurSG Grants Portal](https://oursggrants.gov.sg/grants/pnp/instruction)

---

**SG Partnerships Fund (SGPF) — Budget 2026 initiative**
Relevance: **Low**
A S$50M fund via the Singapore Government Partnerships Office for ground-up, community-impact projects. Three tiers: Seed (pilot/small-scale), Sprout, and Scale. Open to Singapore Citizens/PRs and Singapore-registered organisations. Key disqualifier: projects that intend to make a profit are not eligible. Makes this a non-starter for Splice, Magenta Debrief, or Dronefly as commercial ventures.
[Apply via OurSG Grants Portal](https://oursggrants.gov.sg/grants/mccysgpfsopn/instruction)

---

**National AI Impact Programme (NAIIP)**
Relevance: **Medium**
IMDA's new programme targeting 10,000 enterprises over three years for AI adoption. This is a broader initiative — not routed through OurSG Grants but through the Business Grants Portal. Complements the Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) and the Champions of AI Programme (joint Enterprise Singapore / Digital Industry Singapore initiative for enterprise-wide AI transformation). Budget 2026 also introduced a 400% tax deduction on AI investments under the Enterprise Innovation Scheme, capped at S$50,000/YA for YA2027–2028.

---

**Budget 2026 enhanced SME grants (now live)**
Relevance: **Medium**
As of 1 April 2026, enhanced support levels are in effect for several existing grants: Market Readiness Assistance (up to 70% for SMEs, for internationalisation); Enterprise Development Grant; Business Adaptation Grant (up to 70% for SMEs, up to 50% for non-SMEs, through October 2027). These run through businessgrants.gov.sg, not OurSG.

## My picks

**NAC P&P Grant** is the one worth a closer look. Magenta Debrief publishes aviation content — the publishing category is explicitly covered, and the cap has just been raised to S$100,000/FY. The key question is whether a for-profit aviation media publication qualifies for the 50% tier. Worth a direct call to NAC to clarify. If eligible, this could fund content production, distribution events, or a print/digital showcase.

**NAIIP / Champions of AI Programme** is relevant for Splice. Splice is an SME, and the Champions of AI programme is designed for companies that want to embed AI across operations — not just buy one tool. Splice's model of AI-augmented consulting and the Splice Nano programme could be a strong fit for the enterprise-wide transformation framing. Check businessgrants.gov.sg for application details.

**Market Readiness Assistance (MRA)** at 70% is worth revisiting for Splice specifically — the internationalisation angle is real given clients in Asia, Australia, and beyond.
