**April 3, 2025**

**What Rachel wanted to achieve**

Rachel expressed feeling panicked and unsure despite having a successful career on paper. She wanted to:

1. Develop better processes and gain confidence in her news judgment and focus, moving away from feeling like she's "buzzing around in a panic"
2. Understand if her feelings stemmed from needing to accept her current abilities or if something else was holding her back from reaching the "next level"
3. Move beyond feeling like she's constantly comparing herself to others and getting stuck in "analysis paralysis"
4. Find a more strategic way to identify and pursue stories, rather than feeling directionless or relying on panic for motivation
5. Determine how to get to "big high level investigations" and understand if her difficulty was due to impatience, needing skill development, or if deeply reported features were more her strength
6. Figure out how to operationalize processes for scoping, picking, and planning stories to avoid feeling "frozen and paralyzed"

---

**Awareness and insights gained**

- The nature of her struggle — she articulated that her issue felt distinct from "classic imposter syndrome," relating more to operational processes, decisiveness, and confidence in her judgment
- The paradox of freedom — she recognized the privilege and daunting nature of having the freedom to choose her stories, contrasting it with the relative ease of being assigned work
- Process vs. panic — she acknowledged that while her current "panic" mode gets results, she desires a more assured, structured process; she recognized her tendency to get stuck in the "scoping phase"
- Defining "good enough" — she grappled with whether her work wasn't meeting her own high standards or if her process itself was hindering her from producing faster or more "revelatory" work
- Seeking mentorship — she realized she probably needs more mentorship beyond supportive friends, although she felt embarrassed to "broadcast" perceived deficiencies
- Editor dynamics — she reflected on needing an editor who actively helps hone ideas, rather than just passively encouraging her to "look into that more"
- Potential tools — she found the ideas of hypothesis-based inquiry and using ChatGPT for structuring thoughts and generating next steps potentially useful for breaking out of indecision

---

**What could have been missed as a coach**

- Deeper dive into "confidence" — Rachel mentioned confidence and imposter syndrome multiple times; exploring the roots of her self-doubt and lack of internal validation versus external success might have been beneficial
- Exploring the editor relationship — Rachel explicitly discussed challenges with her editor; more time could have been spent strategizing how she could manage that relationship differently
- Defining "next level" and "distinctive work" — helping her define what that specifically means for her (beyond comparing herself to others) could have provided a clearer goal
- Actionable steps for process — while hypothesis-based inquiry and AI tools were suggested, Rachel noted getting stuck before the hypothesis stage; more concrete, tailored strategies for moving from the overwhelming "scoping phase" to picking a focus could have been explored
- Addressing the "panic" motivation — exploring the underlying fear or belief driving this pattern might have offered deeper insight

---

**Additional coaching questions to consider**

Confidence & self-perception:

- "You mentioned feeling your struggle is distinct from 'classic imposter syndrome.' Can you elaborate on what makes it feel different for you?"
- "When you look back at work you are proud of, what was different about your internal state or your process then?"
- "What would accepting the 'panic and chaos' as part of your process, rather than fighting it, look like for you?"

Editor relationship:

- "You described wanting an editor who helps 'hone your thoughts.' Can you give me a specific example of what that collaborative process might look or feel like?"
- "What assumptions might you be making about what your editor is willing or able to offer?"

Clarifying goals:

- "If you set aside comparisons to others for a moment, what does achieving that 'next level' mean personally for you and your work?"
- "What specific qualities would a story need to have for you to feel it was 'revelatory enough' or 'good enough'?"

Overcoming paralysis:

- "What specific thoughts tend to run through your mind when you feel stuck in the scoping phase?"
- "What is the smallest possible action you could take to generate some momentum when you feel that paralysis?"

Understanding panic:

- "What purpose do you think the panic has served for you up until now?"
- "When you feel yourself 'vibrating with panic,' what's the core fear or worry underneath that feeling?"
