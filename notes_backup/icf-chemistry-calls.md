Some of the best practices mentioned for preparing for a chemistry call include:

1. Being present and grounded: Having your full presence and being mindful during the call is crucial, just as you would for a regular coaching session.

2. Active listening: Actively listening to the client to understand their goals, concerns, and whether they are genuinely seeking coaching. This will help evaluate their coachability.

3. Sharing insights and observations: Sharing your insights and reflections with the client during the call to help them discover their real issues or areas to work on.

4. Demonstrating coachability: Using your coaching skills to actually coach the client a bit during the call, so they can experience what a coaching conversation feels like, instead of just explaining what coaching is.

5. Being honest: Being honest in judging whether the client is truly seeking coaching or may need a different modality like therapy based on their situation.

6. Preparing with a coaching mindset: Approaching the call with the same preparation as a regular coaching session - being grounded, present and ready to partner with the client in their thinking process.


The transcript mentions the "5 Cs" as an effective framework for conducting a chemistry call:

1. Connect - Establish a genuine connection with the client. Understand their language, way of speaking, share insights and reflections so the client can experience your coaching style.

2. Create - Help the client understand and create clarity around their real issue or goal through coaching. Guide them to explore their thoughts rather than just telling them.

3. Curate - Customize and curate the potential engagement to best fit the client's needs based on their goals, preferences, logistics etc. Explore mutually agreeable alternatives.

4. Cue - Invite the client into the co-creative journey of coaching. Cue them that coaching is a partnership where you will be their thinking partner, not an expert giving advice.

5. Coach - Actually coach the client briefly during the call so they can experience what a real coaching conversation feels like instead of just explaining coaching conceptually.

The 5 Cs are meant to be a cyclical process during the chemistry call - connecting, creating clarity, curating the experience, cueing them about the partnership and coaching them experientially. This allows evaluating compatibility and coachability while providing value.


The transcript provides some valuable insights on overcoming mindsets around pushing for conversion and asking for fees as a coach:

1. Don't make the chemistry call solely about conversion: While getting a new client is the eventual goal, the primary focus of the follow-up after a chemistry call should extend beyond just conversion. It should be about building rapport, providing value, clarifying expectations and demonstrating professionalism.

2. Avoid a "pushy" mindset: Some coaches hesitate to follow up after a chemistry call because they don't want to come across as pushy or pressuring the prospect. This stems from their own past experiences of being pushed. The key is to approach follow-ups from a mindset of adding value.

3. Find ways to add value in follow-ups: Instead of pushy tactics, look for opportunities to continue providing value through follow-ups - by sharing relevant articles, resources, offering additional insights etc. This strengthens the relationship.

4. Examine your money mindset: Hesitation around discussing fees often comes from one's own narratives and feelings about money. Dig deeper to understand if you feel embarrassed, overwhelmed or undervaluing yourself when it comes to monetary exchange for your services.

5. Use coaching on yourself: As a coach, use your coaching skills to overcome your own limiting beliefs and narratives around money and fees. Journal, reflect and get comfortable valuing your services appropriately.

The core message is to approach conversion and fee discussions from a mindset of service, value addition and self-worth instead of desperation or pushiness. Shift the focus to strengthening the overall coaching relationship.
