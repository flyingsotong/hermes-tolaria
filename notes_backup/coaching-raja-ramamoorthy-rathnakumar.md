**February 4, 2025**

**Key insights Raja arrived at**

1. His fixation on "news" and "journalism" was limiting his thinking — stopping the use of those words helped him see his product idea more clearly as an information/community service rather than traditional journalism
2. His hyperlocal idea doesn't need to be framed around traditional journalism metrics (pageviews, subscriptions) but could focus purely on impact and helping people
3. He became more confident about prototyping his idea after acknowledging that tools like ChatGPT could help him overcome some technical limitations
4. His product thinking background is a "superpower" that could help him build something valuable, rather than being in tension with journalism

---

**How to make it more of a coaching call**

1. Structure — while the conversation flowed naturally, establishing more explicit coaching parameters at the start would have helped; Raja came with 5 questions but ended up focusing on one area
2. More questions vs. advice — while reframing away from "news/journalism" was valuable, more probing questions could have helped Raja arrive at these insights himself. For example: "What happens when you think about this project without using the word 'news'?"
3. Action planning — while Raja mentioned wanting to act in "the next six months," more concrete next steps and accountability measures would have helped
4. Deeper exploration — Raja's hesitation about entrepreneurship ("I'm not entrepreneurship material") could have been explored more deeply to uncover underlying beliefs and barriers

The session was effective in helping Raja gain clarity and confidence. The practical insights about product thinking and community needs gave him a new framework — and helped him see beyond traditional journalism paradigms while honoring his desire to create meaningful impact.
