**September 27, 2023** Talha, Aisha Farrukh, Wahid

- started by wanting to know how to turn what seems to be a niche (not really) business focused on politics to a mass one that advertisers would buy into
- took them through their assumptions -- the need to court mass advertisers with a mass product, with metrics that are obscure and unattainable
- explained the BuzzFeed example -- how they suffered as production costs and distribution costs both went up
- talked through the Puma case study -- the importance of understanding first the value you bring to your users
- first understand your value, then build a business around that -- with costs that match the potential
- explained why their survey wasn't helpful -- people don't self-define their news habits
- told them there was 4 hours left, and they were free to deep dive into any of these areas if they wanted to

**Step 01 - Assume**

I assume (this is my problem) -- content is not reaching the masses despite being informative / providing new information

I assume (this is the outcome I want) -- want active subscribers -- increase organic reach / engagement

**Step 02 - Test**

Based on these assumptions, I think (this solution will work) -- increase direct interaction with audience like regular lives / spaces -- engage them in WhatsApp community or channel

For (masses) to achieve (understanding of what's happening around them), they need a way to solution. A potential solution could be (if we start engaging them on all stages of content we are making for them). I know I'm successful if (the engagement increases by %) as an outcome.
