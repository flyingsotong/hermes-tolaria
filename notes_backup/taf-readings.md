`TAF: WSSS 280800Z 1808/1912 03009KT 9999 FEW015 SCT020`

## Basic TAF Structure

- **TAF:** Indicates it's a TAF report.
- **Station Identifier:** A four-letter code identifying the airport (e.g., KLAX for Los Angeles International Airport).
- **Date and Time of Origin:** The date and time the forecast was issued (e.g., 180845Z, meaning the 18th of the month at 08:45 Zulu time).
- **Valid Period:** The time frame the forecast covers (e.g., 1812/1918, meaning valid from the 18th at 1200Z to the 19th at 1800Z).
- **Forecast Meteorological Conditions:** This is the core of the TAF, containing information about:
   - Wind
   - Visibility
   - Weather phenomena (e.g., rain, snow, thunderstorms)
   - Clouds

## Key Elements and Codes

- **Wind:**
   - Expressed as a five-digit group (e.g., 19010KT).
   - The first three digits indicate wind direction in degrees true (190).
   - The last two digits indicate wind speed in knots (10KT).
   - "VRB" indicates variable wind direction.
   - "G" indicates gusts, so 19010G20KT would mean winds from 190 degrees at 10 knots, gusting to 20 knots.
- **Visibility:**
   - Expressed in statute miles (SM) (e.g., 10SM).
   - If less than 1 SM, it's given in fractions or hundreds of feet.
- **Weather Phenomena:**
   - Abbreviations are used to indicate weather (e.g., RA for rain, SN for snow, TS for thunderstorm, FG for fog).
   - Intensity is indicated by:
      - `-` for light
      - `+` for heavy
      - No symbol for moderate
- **Clouds:**
   - Cloud coverage is indicated by:
      - SKC (sky clear)
      - FEW (few)
      - SCT (scattered)
      - BKN (broken)
      - OVC (overcast)
   - Cloud height is given in hundreds of feet (e.g., SCT025 means scattered clouds at 2,500 feet).
   - "CB" indicates Cumulonimbus clouds.

## Changes

- **FM (From):** Indicates a rapid change.
- **BECMG (Becoming):** Indicates a gradual change.
- **TEMPO (Temporary):** Indicates temporary fluctuations.
- **PROB (Probability):** Indicates the probability of certain conditions occurring.

## Important

- TAFs are issued in Zulu time (UTC).
- Pilots must convert Zulu time to local time.
- TAFs are forecasts, not guarantees. Weather can change.
