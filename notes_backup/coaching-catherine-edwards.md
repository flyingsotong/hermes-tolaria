**April 3, 2025**

**What Catherine wanted to achieve**

- Catherine wanted to figure out how to strategically keep her consultancy work going alongside her full-time job at the BBC, especially since her previous client's project ended
- She recognized she would have limited time and needed to be focused on finding the right projects, avoiding her previous tendency to take on too much
- She was interested in learning how to be more strategic in finding work when referrals weren't coming directly to her

---

**Awareness and insights gained**

- She realized the importance of leveraging LinkedIn more effectively, not just for job hunting, but for showcasing her consultancy work and sharing insights
- She understood the value of creating case studies from her work (including potentially her BBC work, pending confirmation) to demonstrate the value she brings to clients
- The coach highlighted the benefit of building a personal brand, potentially with a separate name/identity for her consultancy, to appear more established and build client confidence
- She acknowledged the power of using AI tools to efficiently create content (like posts or a simple website) to market her services within her limited time
- Catherine recognized the need to shift her approach when dealing with resistance to change (like within the BBC), focusing on understanding individual incentives, helping others look good, and proposing small, testable steps rather than just presenting data
- She gained perspective on networking, seeing it less as a chore and more as a way to learn, understand client needs, and show up helpfully for others
- She grasped that having a specific point of view or focus as a consultant is powerful, even if others share similar views, and that optimism can be a strong differentiator
- She was reassured that geographical focus might not be essential, as many core challenges in newsrooms are similar globally

---

**What the coach might have missed**

- More questioning, less advice — while Alan provided concrete advice (LinkedIn, case studies, branding, AI, networking), a coaching approach would involve more open-ended questions to help Catherine generate these solutions herself, potentially leading to deeper ownership
- Exploring "tendency to take on everything" — Alan acknowledged this pattern but didn't deeply explore the underlying reasons or habits behind it; this could have been an area for greater self-awareness
- Defining the "focus" — when Catherine asked how to define her one or two focus areas, Alan stressed having a "point of view" found through conversations, but could have used more coaching questions to help her brainstorm or narrow down possibilities based on her passion, experience, and market needs
- Action planning & accountability — the call ended with Catherine feeling she needed to "get started" and organize her thoughts; more time solidifying concrete next steps and establishing accountability would have helped
