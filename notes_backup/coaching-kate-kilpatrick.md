**Coaching questions (prep)**

- What change do you want to see for yourself a year from now?
- What are you not telling me?
- Apart from your job (if you got laid off, etc), what's changed in your life? What's been changing in the way you think about what you want?
- What do you have to do to put yourself in a position to get what you want this year?
- How would you feel about this decision or indecision 3 months, or 3 years from now?
- What do you need to Start, Stop, Continue?
- What is in your control?
- What is one small thing you can do today to make a big difference for yourself or your family in 10 years time?
- What do you have to give up to get this?

**January 10, 2025**

Kate's pre-session note: "What I want to work on: I want to transition from newsrooms to corporate comms and double my salary. I loved my journalism career but that career has not just stalled but tanked over the 5 years. I'm earning half of what I used to. I'm ready for a major change and want to build my confidence to land an exciting new role where I can learn and grow and easily provide for my child."

This was a career transition coaching session. The session revealed Kate's struggle with declining salaries and job satisfaction over the past 5 years, particularly after having a daughter.

**Key areas for Kate to focus on:**

1. AI integration and skills: create a website using AI tools to gain hands-on experience; explore AI platforms beyond basic familiarity; consider creating a hypothetical PR company website as a learning exercise.
2. Network expansion: branch out beyond journalism circles; attend startup community events; leverage existing communities (NYU alumni events, dance community); build connections in corporate/PR spaces.
3. Professional identity transformation: move beyond the "journalist" label; reframe experience to highlight transferable skills (client management, crisis handling, deadline management); focus on roles requiring "adult in the room" expertise rather than pure content creation; consider account management or client-facing roles.
4. Mindset shifts: address "scarcity mindset" regarding job opportunities; challenge assumptions about work-life balance in higher-paying roles; reframe self-worth separate from declining journalism salaries; set clear salary minimums ($200K+) for job applications.

**What worked:**

- Provided valuable context about AI's impact on the industry
- Shared relevant personal experiences and observations
- Offered practical suggestions based on market reality
- Demonstrated good understanding of the emotional aspects of career transition
- Helped shift perspective from content creation to value-added roles
- Successfully challenged self-limiting beliefs

**Areas for improvement:**

- The session could have benefited from a more structured approach with clear objectives set at the beginning
- A more concrete action plan with timelines could have been established
- Some questions could have been more focused to drive deeper insights
- More use of powerful questions to challenge assumptions could have been beneficial
