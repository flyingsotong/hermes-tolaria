Based on the CAAS document, here is a detailed outline to help you design the two-hour online course for your UA Basic Training Organisation (UABTO).
The course should be approximately**120 minutes** long, including the final quiz. Here's a suggested structure that covers all the required learning outcomes.
**Overall Course Structure**
Your online course must include these four key components:
- **Secure User Registration:** A login system that protects users' personal data in compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA).
- **Courseware:** The actual training material, broken down into the three modules below.
- **Final Quiz:** A 20-question quiz to test the user's knowledge.
- **Certificate of Completion:** A certificate issued to trainees who successfully pass the quiz.
**Detailed Course Outline (120 Minutes)**
Here is a breakdown of the course content and a suggested timeline.
**Introduction (5 minutes)**
- Welcome message and brief introduction to the UABTO.
- Course overview: What the trainee will learn and why it's important for safe drone operation.
- Explanation of how the course and the final quiz work.
**Module 1: Air Law & Legislations (35 minutes)**
The goal of this module is to teach the rules and regulations for flying a drone in Singapore.
- **Topic 1: Types of UA Operations & Permits**
 - Rules for different purposes: recreation, education, and business.
 - What permits are needed for various operations and how to apply for them.
- **Topic 2: Drone & Pilot Requirements**
 - How and why you need to register your drone.
 - Explanation of the Unmanned Aircraft Pilot Licence (UAPL), its different classes, and how to apply.
- **Topic 3: Flying Guidelines & "No-Fly" Zones**
 - The essential "Do's & Don'ts" of flying a drone in Singapore.
 - How to use the**OneMap portal** to identify restricted areas, danger areas, and areas near aerodromes where you can't fly.
- **Topic 4: Penalties**
 - Understanding the consequences of breaking the rules under the Air Navigation Act and ANR-101.
**Module 2: UAS General Knowledge (30 minutes)**
This module aims to provide basic knowledge about how drones work.
- **Topic 1: Types of Drones**
 - Introduction to different categories of UA, such as Aeroplanes (fixed-wing), Rotorcraft (quadcopters, helicopters), and others.
- **Topic 2: How Drones Fly**
 - Simple explanation of the forces acting on a drone (lift, weight, thrust, drag) during flight.
 - Main components of a drone and what they do.
 - How critical systems (like GPS, motors, and flight controller) work together.
- **Topic 3: Controlling Your Drone**
 - Common flight modes (e.g., Position Hold, Attitude Hold, Manual).
 - Different radio-control (RC) modes (Mode 1, 2, 3 & 4).
- **Topic 4: Drone Performance**
 - Understanding key specifications (like flight time, range, and payload capacity) and how they affect the drone's performance.
 - Where to find this information for your specific drone.
**Module 3: UAS Safety & Operations (35 minutes)**
This module focuses on teaching the practical skills and awareness needed for safe drone operations.
- **Topic 1: Flight Checks & Best Practices**
 - How to conduct proper**pre-flight, in-flight, and post-flight checks**.
- **Topic 2: Hazard & Risk Management**
 - Identifying and mitigating potential risks, such as bad weather, obstacles, and environmental factors.
- **Topic 3: Handling Emergencies**
 - What to do in common emergency situations like low battery, loss of GPS signal, or loss of control link.
- **Topic 4: Maintenance & Non-Commercial Drones**
 - Importance of periodic maintenance and best practices for inspections and repairs.
 - Safety issues to consider with non-commercial or self-built drones.
- **Topic 5: The Human Factor**
 - How physiological factors like fatigue, sickness, or alcohol can affect your ability to fly safely.
 - How psychological factors like inexperience or carelessness can impact performance.
 - Proper scanning techniques to maintain awareness while flying.
 - Understanding visual illusions and sun blindness and how to prevent them.
**Final Quiz (15 minutes)**
The quiz is the final step to complete the training. Here are the rules for designing it:
- **Question Bank:** You must create a bank of at least**50 questions** covering all three modules.
- **Quiz Format:** The quiz will randomly pull**20 questions** from the bank for each user.
- **Passing Requirement:** The trainee must answer**all 20 questions correctly** to pass.
- **Incorrect Answers:** If a user gets a question wrong, the system must generate a**different question** from the same module. The system should use all available questions before repeating one.
