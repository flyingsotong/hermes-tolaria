**February 17, 2025**

At the start, Franz began somewhat hesitantly, expressing that after 13 years at Bloomberg and now 5 years (2 as editor) at the Bureau of Investigative Journalism, he was thinking about "what's next." He framed his situation primarily through the lens of journalism, focusing on his investigative journalism background and expertise in covering corruption, particularly in Africa. He mentioned having conversations with major news organizations like FT and NYT, but finding himself "one step away" from getting hired, possibly due to his somewhat unorthodox career path focused on African coverage.

By the end, Franz had a significant shift in perspective. He realized he had been underselling himself by focusing solely on his journalism credentials while overlooking his substantial leadership experience managing a team of 30 people, handling HR, fundraising, and overall organizational management. The conversation helped him recognize that he could position himself as a business leader who happens to have deep journalism expertise, rather than just a journalist.

**Areas for deeper exploration:**

1. When Franz mentioned his work in Africa, could have explored what specific leadership challenges he faced there and how those experiences shaped his management style.
2. When he brought up his involvement in fundraising, would have been valuable to dig deeper into his experience with financial management and stakeholder relations.
3. Could have asked more about his specific achievements in transforming the Bureau during his tenure as editor — what measurable improvements did he bring?
4. When discussing his team of 30 people, could have explored his approach to building and developing teams.
5. Could have asked more about what energizes him in his current role — what parts of his job give him the most satisfaction and why?
6. When he mentioned being "open-minded" about his direction, could have explored what that meant specifically — what kinds of roles or industries intrigue him?
7. The conversation could have benefited from more exploration of his values and what he wants his long-term impact to be, beyond just the next career move.

The conversation successfully helped Franz reframe his perspective, but could have gone deeper into specific examples and experiences that would help him build his new narrative as a business leader.
