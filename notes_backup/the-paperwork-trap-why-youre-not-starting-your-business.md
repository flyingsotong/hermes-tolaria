The first-time founder always asks the same questions: _Sole prop or Private Limited? Physical office or a virtual one?_
You think you’re seeking legal clarity. You’re not. You’re seeking**certainty**—a false sense of security that if the paperwork is perfect, the business will be, too. This obsession with administrative detail is a powerful form of**analysis paralysis** that keeps you from the only thing that matters: getting a paying customer.
You don't procrastinate tasks; you procrastinate feelings. In this case, the feeling is the fear of being judged, of messing up, or of realizing your idea isn't good enough.
Stop. The market doesn't care about your LLC. It only cares if you solve a problem.
**The Entrepreneur’s First Principle: Sell First, Paper Later**
The fastest path to failure is spending time and money on legal structure before you have a single sale. This is the**Distribution First, Product Second** principle.
1.**Validate, Don't Incorporate.** Your first step isn't choosing a company type; it's**validation**. Build the smallest possible version of your product—a**Minimum Viable Product (MVP)**—and prove people will pay. Use a simple landing page, a personal PayPal account, or a Google Sheet.
2.**Embrace the Mess.** You are trying to avoid problems that need to be solved ASAP. Mess it up now, worry about it later. The successful founder is the one who struggles and keeps going.
3.**Focus on the Offer.** You need two things to start: an offer and a way to take payment. Everything else—the LLC, the EIN, the business bank account—comes _after_ you have traction.
**The Coach’s Mandate: Action Over Perfection**
Your job is to move from**mental masturbation** (thinking, talking, visualizing) to**doing the work**.
-**Start Small.** The antidote to paralysis is making**small promises** to yourself. What is the smallest step you can take today to get closer to a sale?
-**Find Your Partner.** The journey is too hard for one person. Find a**work partner**—a co-conspirator—who can help you grind and keep you accountable.
-**Trust Your Momentum.** Momentum is oxygen. Each small action builds upon the last, creating a**compound effect** that transforms tiny habits into extraordinary results.
The paperwork is a distraction. The real work is finding out if anyone cares enough to pay. Go sell. The rest is just admin.
