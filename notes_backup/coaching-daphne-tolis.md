**Daphne's situation**

- Extensive experience working with major media organizations (BBC, ABC, Vice, National Geographic)
- Cyclical work pattern — very busy during crises (refugee crisis, economic crisis, natural disasters) followed by quiet periods
- Experienced burnout from covering traumatic events, particularly during the refugee crisis
- Struggles with the "fixer" label which doesn't fully capture her responsibilities (field producing, location management, permissions, character finding)
- Finding more work in reality TV/documentary shows than traditional journalism lately
- Faces common freelancer issues: late/no payments, undervaluation of skills

---

**Main advice given**

- Rebrand herself as a company rather than an individual freelancer
- Move away from the "freelancer" or "fixer" title which invites poor treatment
- Create a professional website with a company name and clear service offerings
- Use her downtime (the 50% when she's not working) to develop this business identity
- Study how other successful companies in her field market themselves

---

**What worked well**

1. Identifying the core problem — quickly recognized that her "freelancer/fixer" identity was limiting her professional opportunities and compensation
2. Practical solution — offered a concrete, actionable solution (creating a company identity) rather than vague encouragement
3. Empathy and understanding — acknowledged the challenges freelancers face with late payments and poor treatment
4. Building on her strengths — recognized her skills in finding characters and stories were marketable services

---

**Areas for improvement**

1. Addressing her burnout/trauma — while acknowledged, there wasn't much discussion about sustainable work practices going forward
2. Exploring her actual interests — the conversation focused on rebranding but less on what type of work she actually wants to pursue
3. Concrete next steps — while the general direction (create a company) was given, more specific initial steps would have helped
4. Balancing practicality with passion — the solution was business-focused but didn't address her evident passion for human-centered storytelling

---

**Missed opportunities**

- Client acquisition strategies — no exploration of specific methods for finding new clients beyond rebranding
- Work feedback mechanisms — no discussion about how she might get constructive feedback to continue improving
- Pricing strategy — limited discussion on how to price services appropriately as a company rather than a freelancer
- Portfolio development — no exploration of how to showcase her work effectively to potential clients
