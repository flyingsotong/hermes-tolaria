# Human Factors Part 1 (RAAus Practice Exam)

**Source:** Pilot Practice Exams - RAAus Human Factors Part 1
**URL:** https://www.pilotpracticeexams.com/exams/human-performance-and-limits/
**Type:** Multiple choice, 23 questions
**License:** Free practice exam

1. **Pilots who smoke will generally experience hypoxia:**
   - at higher altitudes than non-smoking pilots (all other factors being equal)
   - at the same altitudes as non-smoking pilots (all other factors being equal)
   - none of these
   - **at lower altitudes than non-smoking pilots (all other factors being equal)** ✓

2. **Pilots are more prone to disorientation when the aircraft is:**
   - **doing manoeuvres well above the ground** ✓
   - landing
   - taking off
   - taxiing

3. **Illusions associated with angular and linear acceleration include:**
   - Coriolis
   - graveyard spin/spiral
   - climbing illusion
   - both the graveyard spin/spiral and the climbing illusion
   - **Coriolis, graveyard spin/spiral and climbing illusion** ✓

4. **Generally, the time from spotting a possible collision to control response is considered to be:**
   - **12.5 seconds** ✓
   - 14.5 seconds
   - 2 seconds
   - 10 seconds

5. **The best type of sunglasses for pilots are:**
   - **non polarised** ✓
   - polarised

6. **You have an elevated likelihood of having a mid air collision:**
   - close to a non-towered aerodrome
   - **all of these** ✓
   - when an aircraft overtakes another
   - VFR during daylight on the weekend

7. **Two major factors for avoiding mid-air collisions are:**
   - pilot attitude and pilot peripheral vision
   - pilot skill and peripheral vision
   - **situational awareness and visual scanning** ✓
   - situational awareness and pilot skill

8. **A medical condition where oxygen starvation occurs is known as:**
   - hyper-oxyia
   - hypo-oxia
   - **hypoxia** ✓
   - hyperoxia

9. **In flight, an aircraft that appears stationary in the windscreen is considered:**
   - none of these
   - very low risk
   - **high risk** ✓
   - low risk

10. **Human performance factors that can affect a pilot's ability to fly include:**
    - alcohol and drugs
    - vision, hearing, inner ear
    - **both of these** ✓
    - none of these

11. **Smoke, fog, dust, haze and clouds highlight the need for:**
    - lots of training away from water
    - **an artificial horizon** ✓
    - lots of training at night
    - none of these

12. **Early symptoms of oxygen depletion or starvation are similar to symptoms of alcohol intake.**
    - **True** ✓
    - False

13. **CASA can conduct a BAC or drug test anytime either airside or not, even on recreational pilots.**
    - **True** ✓
    - False

14. **Hypoxia and its onset is only affected by altitude. Health factors do not change when hypoxia occurs.**
    - **False** ✓
    - True

15. **Flying with a cold or flu is not advisable due to:**
    - none of these
    - sudden onset of hypoxia without warning
    - **sinus and middle ear effects** ✓
    - both of these

16. **The three basic stages of collision avoidance, in order of occurrence are:**
    - decision, detection, evasive action
    - none of these
    - **detection, decision, evasive action** ✓
    - decision, detection, invasive action

17. **Other factors that may reduce mid-air collisions include:**
    - planning ahead and adhering to operational procedures
    - avoiding crowded airspace where possible and wear good sunglasses
    - make S bends during climbing to see past the nose and use anti-collision lights even during daylight
    - **all of these** ✓

18. **When gases expand in your blood and cause pain, injury or discomfort, it is known as:**
    - barometer
    - none of these
    - baro-effect
    - **barotrauma** ✓

19. **CASA policy on Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) is:**
    - 12 hours since last drink and 0.00% BAC
    - **0.00% BAC and 8 hours since last drink** ✓
    - 10 hours since last drink and 0.02% BAC
    - 0.02% BAC and 8 hours since last drink

20. **Flying without enough elapsed time after scuba diving can cause injury or adverse effects on the pilot. What is the minimum (not safe) recommended wait time after multiple dives in a day that do not total more than 4 hours or require decompression?**
    - 24 hours
    - **12 hours** ✓
    - 18 hours
    - There is no minimum

21. **A situation where a pilot cannot sense his/her attitude correctly is known as:**
    - none of these
    - **spatial disorientation** ✓
    - altitude loss of awareness
    - attitudinal loss

22. **Distant aircraft that are not on a collision path with you will:**
    - remain stationary in your windscreen
    - move up or down across your windscreen
    - **track across your windscreen, or move up or down across your windscreen** ✓
    - track across your windscreen

23. **Pilot skill has little bearing on the likelihood of a mid-air collision; it is primarily vision and other factors.**
    - **False** ✓
    - True
