## 3. France Becomes First EU Country to Ban Social Media for Under-15s

**Moment:** July 21, 2026 — French parliament approved a bill banning social media access for children under 15 (279-81 vote). President Macron championed it. New accounts blocked from September 2026; existing accounts from January 2027. Exceptions for educational platforms and encyclopedias.

**Source:** https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/jul/21/france-ban-social-media-access-under-15s (Verified 200)

**Key quotes from the source:**

> "France is leading the way in Europe when it comes to protecting our children and teenagers," Macron said, hailing "a major step forward."

> "The Constitutional Council must now rule on it, and then it will be time to take action to make this measure a reality and protect our children online."

> The digital minister Anne Le Hénanff said: "For four months, all of us in France will have to prove our age. If someone is under 15, the account will be closed."

**Rupture:** First major EU country to implement full age-gating for social media. If this works in France, it becomes a template the EU Commission scales. For media operators: age verification becomes a distribution requirement — and the cost of compliance pushes toward platforms that already have identity infrastructure (Apple, Google, Meta) rather than independent publishers who'd have to build or buy age verification.

**Theme:** Platform power

**Angle:** The distribution equation changes when the audience is age-gated at the platform level. Publishers who built youth audiences on TikTok/Instagram face a regulatory wall. The winners aren't the publishers with great content — they're the platforms with authentication infrastructure. The losers are anyone dependent on social discovery for under-25 audiences.
