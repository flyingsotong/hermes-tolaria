**Moment:** June 2026 — UK news industry backing the Automated Online Software (Access and Transparency) Bill, following New York's Stealth Crawler Preservation Act. **Source:** https://pressgazette.co.uk/news/uk-news-industry-backs-law-to-stop-deceptive-ai-scraping/
**Verified:** 200 OK

**Rupture:** The news industry is shifting from individual lawsuits to collective regulatory action — and winning. While AI labs talk about partnerships and licensing, there's a $1bn shadow scraping economy that publishers are targeting through law, not negotiation. The "and yet": the most effective publisher response to AI scraping isn't a deal — it's a bill.

**Theme:** Platform power

**Key quotes (from Press Gazette):**
- "A proposed UK law is being drafted to stop companies deploying AI bots from using deceptive tactics to scrape websites"
- "The move follows New York state passing the Stealth Crawler Preservation Act"
- "Both seek to address the issue of bots that hide or do not disclose their intention — such as search indexing or AI training — and who is behind them"
- "Media consultant Matthew Scott Goldstein, citing data from a company called Mordor, has suggested the network of third-party scrapers and brokers together comprise a $1bn industry"
- "Some scrapers pretend to be humans while others may falsely label themselves, for example as a Google bot"
- "This makes it difficult for website owners to understand how their content is being used, stops them from being able to block bots they don't want to access their content, and ultimately makes it harder to negotiate with AI firms"
- The New York bill "will make it an offence to 'damage, impair or burden the operation of a covered news site or otherwise cause a news site economic harm'"

**Angle for Alan:** Publishers are learning that regulatory action at scale beats individual deals. The playbook being built in New York and the UK could reshape the AI-training data economy faster than most operators realize. This is the Swiss cheese model applied to content protection — multiple layers of defense (technical, legal, regulatory) rather than relying on any single one.

**Alan signal needed:** A conversation with someone in policy or regulation. Noticing the parallel between aviation safety regulation (multiple layers of defense, the Swiss cheese model) and what publishers are now building. Or watching a regulatory development and recognizing the pattern from his KAS/ICFJ workshops.

**Closing provocation:** "You can sue one AI company at a time. Or you can change the law for all of them. Which one scales?"
