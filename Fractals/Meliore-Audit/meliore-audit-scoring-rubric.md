# Meliore Audit — Scoring Rubric (1-5 scale)

*Use for every org. Score within 24 hours of each conversation.*

---

## Hunger — Is the leader and team genuinely hungry, or performing it for the funder?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Skin in the game (own salary deferred, personal financial risk). Has turned down other opportunities to do this. Talks about revenue with specificity, not aspiration. |
| 4 | Clear appetite. Has made tangible moves toward commercial thinking (engaged consultants, hired revenue person, ran a pricing experiment). Willing to discuss pricing. |
| 3 | Says the right things but evidence is thin. Commercial language is borrowed (from funder or consultant). Revenue talk stays in future tense. |
| 2 | Performing hunger for the room. Uses funder-facing language. No personal stake visible. Deflects when asked about personal tradeoffs. |
| 1 | Actively defensive about commercial thinking. Treats it as a distraction from mission. No evidence of wanting to change. |

---

## Offer — Is there something a paying customer would actually buy in the next 90 days?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Identified buyer with a specific price point. Product exists or is buildable from existing assets in weeks, not months. |
| 4 | Clear latent asset — data, dense audience, proprietary methodology, identified buyer hypothesis. Needs packaging but the core exists. |
| 3 | Some latent IP or audience but unclear who would pay. Potential buyers are a category, not a name. |
| 2 | No identifiable product. Revenue plan is generic (ads, events, donations). No one has signaled willingness to pay. |
| 1 | Active structural barrier to having an offer (legal, constitutional, or self-imposed). |

---

## Permission — Can the org legally and structurally take commercial revenue?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | For-profit or hybrid structure. Clear commercial vehicle. Board aligned. No constitutional limits. Revenue welcome. |
| 4 | Nonprofit with a clear path to commercial vehicle (subsidiary, fiscal sponsor, trading arm). Board openness. |
| 3 | Legal structure allows it but cultural resistance exists. Firewall language suggests revenue is seen as threat to mission. |
| 2 | Constitutional, legal, or governance blocks. Board would need to approve changes. No conversation started. |
| 1 | Org cannot legally take commercial revenue without fundamental restructuring. No appetite to do so. |

---

## Culture — Can the team behind the leader carry this?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Multiple people one layer down who could build and own a revenue stream. Team has shipped commercial products before. |
| 4 | One strong person who could carry commercial work. Team open to it. Prior commercial attempts visible (even if they failed). |
| 3 | Leader is the only commercial thinker. Team is mission-aligned but commercial-naive. Failure stories are absent — not because nothing failed, but because nothing was tried. |
| 2 | Team actively resistant. Values conflict: "that's not what we do." No one below the leader could own commercial work. |
| 1 | Team is the leader. No one else exists to carry anything. |

---

## Cadence — Can the org ship at commercial speed?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Ships editorial AND commercial. Decision speed under 48h for non-editorial. Short approval chain. Shipping muscle visible. |
| 4 | Strong editorial cadence. Some commercial experiments visible. Decision-making is reasonably fast. |
| 3 | Ships editorially but not commercially. Approval chain is long. Leader is a bottleneck. Ideas are discussed more than tested. |
| 2 | Slow on everything. Committee-driven. Analysis paralysis on non-editorial decisions. |
| 1 | Cannot ship without leader's sign-off. Leader is overwhelmed. Nothing has been shipped in months. |

---

## Clock — How much runway remains, and is urgency visible to the whole team?

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Runway is known and finite. Urgency is visible to the whole org, not just the ED. Timeline is driving decisions. |
| 4 | Runway is known. Leader feels urgency. Team may or may not see it. |
| 3 | Runway exists but is vague. "A few years" or "adequate." No visible urgency driving behavior. |
| 2 | Runway is unknown or a forbidden topic. Leader protects team from financial reality. |
| 1 | No concept of a clock at all. Infinite philanthropic horizon assumed. |
