**Marc Lourdes memorial -- opening speech**

I don't really know what to say. I don't have Marc's gift of words so I had to write this down.

I thought it would be nice to celebrate the man who's brought all of us together, even in under these circumstances.

I'm recording this for Darsh, who can't join us today. But I think this would be something special for her to see how much we all loved the man she married.

For this service, please mute your mics, until you want to speak.

Share your memories of Marc. Or leave a comment in the chat box. Or hold him silently in your memory. He would appreciate all of this either way.

Feel free to turn off your cameras as well, if that helps.

If you'd like to make a donation, click the link. We'll send that over to Darsh, who said she would put it to something that Marc cares deeply about.

On that site, you'll also see a link to a shared Google Photo gallery. Feel free to add wonderful photos of Marc being Marc, so that we all have this memory of him.

We're doing this at 4 pm because it straddles a whole bunch of time zones. So expect people to come and go. Stay as long as you can, and if you have to run off, please do.

Finally, I don't know what an online memorial service needs to be. This is something I've never had to do.

In trying to prepare for this service today, I've been looking through the WhatsApp chats that Marc and I used to have.

The one thing I've realized is that talking to Marc is much like watching a Seinfeld episode. It's rambling conversation about nothing, and often layered with highly inappropriate comments that wouldn't survive today's cancel culture.

But talking shit with Marc, and the dirty jokes we shared, gave both of us tremendous joy.

In the end, it was never about the conversation per se -- but how he made you feel.

Marc had a way of making you feel appreciated, respected, and loved. And he did this by listening, by being present, and giving no judgement.

I didn't know this until I saw it mentioned in the write up by CNN today. This was something Marc had in his email signature -- and I found it in the very first email exchange between us on August 16, 2010.

My candle burns at both ends It will not last the night But ah my foes and oh my friends It gives a lovely light

So thank you Marc for leaving behind this void. I miss you, that filthy mind of yours, but most importantly, the light and the love that you gave all of us.

I'd like to call on Rueben, one of Marc's closest friends, to take this foward.

Rueben, I'm sorry for your loss.
