**Group Mentor Coaching Session 5 — 12:30pm with Bryan Goh and Adrian Lim**

Key lessons learned:

1. The importance of creating a safe space for open and honest conversation. Bryan provided a non-judgmental environment for Adrian to share his thoughts and feelings freely, which allowed him to get issues "off his chest."
2. Focusing on feelings over just facts. Rather than solely dissecting the situations where clients requested coach changes, Bryan guided Adrian to explore and process the emotions (curiosity, self-doubt, defensiveness) he was experiencing. This helped Adrian gain more self-awareness.
3. Avoiding the temptation to solve the problem. Bryan resisted the urge to try to "solve" Adrian's issue about why some clients requested coach changes. Instead, he stayed focused on Adrian's feelings, which was more productive.
4. The value of supervision and mentoring for coaches. The conversation highlighted how helpful coaching supervision can be for coaches to unpack the emotions that arise during client interactions.
5. Importance of confidence and managing self-doubt. A key takeaway for the next session was building Adrian's confidence back up after experiencing the setbacks of clients wanting coach changes.
6. Being cognizant of different coaching styles. The discussion touched on how a more questioning, pure coaching style may not resonate with all clients, who may prefer more direct input from the coach at times.

In summary, key lessons centered on creating psychological safety, exploring emotions, avoiding problem-solving tendencies, the benefits of supervision, building coaching confidence, and respecting differences in preferred coaching approaches.
