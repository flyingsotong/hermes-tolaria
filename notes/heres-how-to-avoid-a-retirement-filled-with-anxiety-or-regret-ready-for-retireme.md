# Here's How to Avoid a Retirement Filled with Anxiety or Regret (Ready For Retirement podcast)

Thursday 2 January 2025


[Here's How to Avoid a Retirement Filled with Anxiety or Regret - Ready For Retirement](https://pca.st/sbdrfnyq)

This episode of "Ready for Retirement" with James Canole and Ari discusses the delicate balance between two major retirement regrets:

1. The regret of running out of money

- This is the most common fear among retirees

- People often become overly frugal due to this fear

- Through proper planning and analysis, this fear can be managed by understanding realistic withdrawal rates and scenarios

2. The regret of underspending and missing out on life

- Many retirees are so focused on not running out of money that they miss opportunities to:

 - Travel while they have good health and energy

 - Retire earlier to spend more time with family

 - Invest in their health

 - Give more to family or causes they care about

 - Pursue meaningful experiences

“How do we just go into retirement, eyes wide open, that there's not just one regret, which is running out of money. We're all acutely aware of that.**There's the other regret of not doing what you could have done because you were either too fearful of doing it or whatever the case might be**, but you look back and are upset that that's the case.”

The hosts emphasize that the key to minimizing both types of regret is finding the right balance through:

- Intentional planning that ensures financial security while enabling meaningful spending

- Understanding that compound interest continues working in retirement

- Recognizing that extreme frugality can lead to significant missed opportunities

- Focusing on spending on things that truly matter to you

They shared a poignant example about Charlie Munger, who despite dying with billions of dollars, expressed regret about never catching a 200-pound tuna - highlighting how having money but not health or time can lead to regrets.
