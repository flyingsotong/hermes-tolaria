- Summary: Paul Miller, CEO of Questex, emphasizes the importance of delegate revenue as an indicator of an event's value, suggesting that people's willingness to pay to attend an event makes it compelling. He believes that if an event can monetize its audience effectively, the sponsorship revenue will naturally follow. Miller also discusses the significance of data collection before and during events. Before events, targeted marketing is used based on community interests, such as promoting non-alcoholic beverage content to those reading about it. At events, a wealth of data is collected, including attendee movements and session attendance, using technologies like AI and badges. This data helps to understand attendee behavior and preferences, which can be reported back to sponsors. However, Miller notes that there can be too much data, and it's essential to manage it responsibly. The balance between sponsorship revenue and delegate revenue depends on the event and its market.
