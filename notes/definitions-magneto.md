# Definitions Magneto

The magneto is an essential electrical component in piston engines, particularly in aircraft engines and some older vehicles. It performs two key functions:

1.**It generates electrical power independently of any battery or external power source.** The magneto contains permanent magnets that rotate with the engine's crankshaft, creating electrical current through electromagnetic induction.

2.**It provides the high-voltage electrical spark needed to ignite the fuel-air mixture in the engine's cylinders at precisely the right moment in the combustion cycle.** The magneto amplifies the generated current from about 12V to around 20,000V which is needed to create the spark at the spark plug.

What makes magnetos particularly valuable in aircraft engines is their**self-contained nature - they don't require a battery to operate, making them very reliable.**

Most aircraft engines actually use dual magnetos for redundancy, so if one fails, the other can still provide ignition.
