# Meeting Bridgette See – Journalism Training Programs

Tue, May 7th, 2024

- 9:00am met with Bridgette See

 1. Exploring ways to involve and collaborate with social media influencers and creators in journalism programs, recognizing their growing influence and role in shaping opinions and narratives.

 2. The importance of reaching out to and upskilling freelance journalists, who often lack access to training resources compared to those in traditional newsrooms.

 3. Considering regional and Asia-based organizations, experts, and resources for investigative journalism programs, rather than primarily relying on Western counterparts.

 4. Investigating the possibility of developing a program specifically tailored for influencers and creators, to provide them with journalistic principles, fact-checking skills, and ethical guidelines.

 5. Conducting research or a "listening session" with a diverse group of influencers to understand their perspectives, challenges, and needs, which could inform the development of future programs or initiatives.

 6. Exploring potential collaborations with organizations like Probe in the Philippines, which have experience working with influencers on fact-checking and information verification.

 7. Leveraging existing communities and networks, such as Planet Splice, to connect with relevant stakeholders and tap into existing resources and expertise.

 8. Breaking down larger events or conferences into smaller, more focused components that could be sponsored or supported independently, such as funding travel costs for specific attendees or organizing side events.

- Overall, the conversation centered around exploring innovative ways to engage with emerging information ecosystems, leveraging regional resources, and fostering collaborations between traditional journalism and the influencer/creator space.
