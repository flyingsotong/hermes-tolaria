# Becoming an SSG-registered training provider

<https://www.tpgateway.gov.sg/plan-courses/organisation-registration-for-first-time-training-provider/apply-for-organisation-registration>
** Opportunity Summary: Building a Training Provider in Singapore**
** Strategic Opportunity**
Becoming an SSG-registered training provider opens doors to:
- **Access government funding** for courses, making them more attractive to learners.
- **Tap into national upskilling initiatives** like SkillsFuture and WSQ frameworks.
- **Build credibility and visibility** through official recognition and listing on TPGateway.
- **Serve diverse markets**—corporate clients, public learners, and in-house staff.
This is especially powerful if you're building a brand around innovation, tech, or creative industries—areas where Singapore is investing heavily in workforce development.
** What You Could Build**
**1. Training Academy or Brand**
A modern, design-forward training brand focused on:
- **Digital skills** (UI/UX, branding, web development, drone operations)
- **Creative disciplines** (design thinking, visual storytelling, animation)
- **Entrepreneurship & innovation** (startup strategy, product-market fit, marketing copywriting)
You could position it as a hybrid model: public-facing and in-house training for partner companies.
**2. Course PortfolioTo qualify, you’ll need a one-year track record of training. You could start with:**
- Workshops for SMEs or creators (e.g. “Logo Design for Startups” or “Drone Safety & Regulations”)
- Online masterclasses with e-attendance tracking
- Community-led sessions (leveraging your aviation and design networks)
**3. Training Infrastructure**
You’ll need:
- A legal entity registered with ACRA or ROS
- A physical or virtual training space (not a P.O. Box)
- Policies and operations manuals covering course admin, quality, and outcomes
- A website or brochure with full course details and trainer bios
** Key Requirements to Prepare**
To apply, you’ll need:
- Legal registration and proper naming conventions
- Financial health proof (IRAS Notice of Assessment)
- One-year training track record (quarterly sessions minimum)
- Sample course materials, contracts, and marketing assets
- Defined management roles and training premises documentation
** Entrepreneurial Angle**
You could differentiate by:
- **Designing emotionally resonant courses** (e.g. preserving family artwork through digital design)
- **Offering modular, stackable micro-courses** with strong branding
- **Building a community hub** around drones, design, and innovation
- **Leveraging your branding expertise** to help other trainers polish their course identity
