**September 24, 2025**

---

**Marco's insights and learning**

Marco gained significant insights into his professional and personal identity:

- Understanding the conflict between passion and profit — Marco realized the core of his frustration wasn't a lack of passion for journalism or content creation, but the conflict between doing it for personal fulfillment versus doing it to make money. A project can be a fulfilling hobby without needing to be a profitable business.
- The problem with the "freelancer" label — the word diminishes his professional value and the perception of his skills. By positioning himself as a specialist — a documentary filmmaker, video editor, or content strategist — he can command more respect and attract higher-quality clients.
- The value of his diverse skill set — initially, Marco saw his dual paths in journalism and digital marketing as a liability. Through questioning, he realized his marketing skills are a unique strength that differentiates him in the media industry.
- The "different hats" concept — he moved from a binary, all-or-nothing mindset to managing multiple distinct professional identities. Different websites for each specialization (filmmaker, editor, audience strategist) helped him conceptualize how to market each skill without creating confusion.

---

**Coaching improvement areas**

**Moving from telling to evoking awareness (ICF 7)**

Direct advice and solutions were offered frequently ("You need to stop calling yourself a freelancer"). A more powerful approach would lead Marco to these conclusions himself:

- Instead of "You need to stop calling yourself a freelancer" — ask "What does the word 'freelancer' mean to you, and how do you think potential clients perceive it? What might be a different way to describe your work?"
- Instead of suggesting three different websites — ask "Given what you've learned about the power of specificity, how could you present your diverse skill set without causing confusion?" This lets him arrive at the solution himself.

**Partnering with the client (ICF 3 & 8)**

The coaching started with Marco expressing confusion, and Alan immediately stepped in to "give a point of view" rather than helping him structure his own thoughts. A stronger opening: "What would a successful outcome from this session look like for you?" This sets a clear agenda focused on his desired outcome.

**Maintaining a coaching mindset (ICF 2 & 5)**

The conversation moved rapidly toward solutions rather than staying in a state of not knowing. When Marco said he felt "empty" in his digital marketing profession, a deeper question could have been: "Tell me more about that feeling of 'empty.' What was missing for you in that work?"

---

**Summary**

Strong rapport and genuine support throughout. Practical advice was well-received and clearly helpful. To elevate from mentoring to coaching, the goal is to shift from being the source of answers to being a facilitator of the client's self-discovery — building his capacity to solve similar challenges in the future.
