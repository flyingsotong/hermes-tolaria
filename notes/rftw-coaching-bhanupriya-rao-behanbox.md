# RFTW coaching Bhanupriya Rao (BehanBox)

_Our sessions have charted Bhanu's strategic pivot from running a journalism platform to leading a "business of change," first by unearthing her "Change Labs" concept as the core business plan. This led to a focus on hiring a senior leader to execute this new vision and, most recently, to developing a "Value Assessment" survey to understand what her audience truly values, which will define her future products and team structure._
**November 5, 2025 (Session 5)**
Bhanupriya's organization is at a pivotal moment. Having successfully aligned her team around the "business of change" and moved away from a reporter-centric model, her goal is to build a new strategy based on user needs.
She came to the call seeking feedback on a comprehensive "Needs Assessment" survey she had drafted. Her intention was to use this survey to understand what her different user groups want, which would then define the new "products" they should build and the new, non-traditional roles they need to hire for.
**The Big "Aha" Moment**
The big "aha" moment was the fundamental shift from conducting a "Needs Assessment" to conducting a**"Value Assessment"**.
You challenged the premise that users "know" what they need, pointing out that many of her questions were framed with industry jargon (like "journalism") and focused on the _product_ (e.g., "what flavor of ice cream do you want?") rather than the _benefit_.
The breakthrough came when you reframed the core question she should be asking: not "What do you need from us?" but**"Tell me how we are valuable to you"**.
Bhanupriya immediately grasped this, realizing that her organization's value isn't just its _topic_ ("gender," which some users feel they "already know") but its _function_: helping people connect the dots, making the invisible visible, and creating a space for crucial conversations. Understanding this _value_ is the true foundation for all future product development.
**How to Structure Your Next Conversation**
Your immediate next step is to provide feedback on her revised survey. The next full conversation will be to analyze the results and move forward on the Change Labs strategy.
-**Your Immediate Task (Asynchronous):**
 - Bhanupriya is reworking the survey questions _tonight_ based on this "value assessment" framework.
 - She will send the new draft to you for a quick review.
 - Your feedback should focus on ensuring the survey is short (promising it will only take 1-2 minutes), avoids jargon, and is edited down to the few essential questions that will reveal the _value_ her audience receives.
-**Your Next Call's Agenda:**
 1.**Analyze the Survey Data:** Once the survey is complete, your session will be a debrief. You will help her analyze the qualitative and quantitative data to identify the core "value" her users are articulating.
 2.**Connect Value to Products:** Using that data, you can brainstorm what products or services would "triple down" on delivering that specific value.
 3.**Define the Roles:** With the value and potential products clarified, you can return to her original goal: finalizing the job descriptions for the "Change Labs" and hiring the right people to build this new future.
