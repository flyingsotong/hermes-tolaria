**Friday, June 20, 2025**

**What Chatwan wanted to learn on this call**

Chatwan primarily wanted to discuss his new local news project for Bangkok, "Soy Society," and specifically sought advice on the revenue side of things. He mentioned he felt comfortable with reporting, content, and audience development, but was "not really sure how I should approach the revenue side." He also seemed to be looking for validation on his audience survey findings and the general concept of his project.

**What he eventually got out of it**

Chatwan received a significant shift in perspective regarding his project's core focus and monetization strategy. You guided him away from thinking about "news" or "journalism" as the primary product and instead emphasized an **audience-first, problem-solving approach**.

Specifically, he learned:
- The limitation of "news" surveys — people don't know what "news" means when asked if they'd pay for it, and they compare it to traditional media.
- The importance of identifying and solving specific problems — repeatedly stressed as "who is this for and why do they need it?" and "what is the problem you're fixing for them?" This directly addresses revenue concerns by linking value creation to monetization.
- The need for hyper-specificity — moving away from broad "underreported topics" to specific problems (e.g., property developers needing city planning info, migrants needing visa and schooling info).
- The evolving landscape of information consumption — insights on AI's role in information retrieval challenged his initial newsletter-first approach, suggesting data sets or other direct service models.
- Validation of the audience and market — actionable advice on how to validate his hypothesis for specific niches (e.g., using Instagram or LinkedIn ads to find 50, 100, or 1,000 people with a shared problem).
- Critique of old media models — why past English-language local news outlets in Bangkok failed (advertising models, not service models, website dependency) provides context and reinforces the need for a new approach.

**What you could have done better as his coach**

- More explicit action items at the end — excellent conceptual shifts and validation strategies, but a clearer summary of the immediate next steps Chatwan should take could have been beneficial. For example: "1. Refine your survey questions to focus on daily problems. 2. Consider running a small Instagram ad campaign targeting X audience with Y problem. 3. Explore Reddit for specific pain points related to Z."
- Addressing his initial "newsletter" idea more directly earlier — Chatwan mentioned it quite early as his planned launch vehicle. Acknowledging it more directly before diving into the broader problem-solving framework might have made the subsequent advice even more impactful.
- Asking about his definition of "journalism" — a brief question like "When you say 'journalism,' what does that mean to you in the context of this project?" could have revealed his internal biases and provided another angle for self-reflection before you presented your alternative framework.

Overall, your coaching provided Chatwan with a powerful reframing of his project, moving him from a traditional media mindset to a more entrepreneurial, problem-solving, and audience-centric approach.
