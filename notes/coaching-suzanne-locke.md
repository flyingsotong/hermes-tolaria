# Coaching - Suzanne Locke

**March 5, 2025**

**Suzanne's insights**

1. Reframing AI — she realized the potential of AI as a tool to enhance her work rather than just a threat to her career
2. Breaking out of the content box — she recognized the need to diversify her skills beyond content creation and explore new areas like DEI and content design
3. The value of her DEI experience — she learned to view her DEI experience as a potential asset for future opportunities, even if she doesn't feel like an expert yet
4. Overcoming imposter syndrome — she realized she doesn't need to be an absolute expert to teach or consult in a particular area, especially in emerging fields like AI
5. The importance of imagination — with AI taking over many tasks, the pressure is now on imagination and exploring new possibilities, rather than just acquiring knowledge

---

**Areas for improvement**

1. Deeper dive into Suzanne's goals — while her interest in DEI and the possibility of a PhD were touched upon, her motivations and aspirations could have been explored in more depth; understanding her values and long-term goals would have allowed for a more tailored conversation
2. More targeted questions — could have asked more targeted questions to help Suzanne explore specific challenges and opportunities, for example about the biggest obstacles she faces in her current role at Aurora 50 or what excites her most about DEI work
3. Action planning — the conversation ended with a general discussion about imagination and exploring new possibilities; a more concrete action plan for next steps would have helped (specific AI tools to learn, networking opportunities to pursue)
4. Addressing concerns about the PhD program — Suzanne expressed concerns about the financial implications of the PhD and how it might affect her plans to move to Norway; potential solutions weren't explored, such as scholarships or part-time work opportunities
5. Exploring the possibility of coaching — Suzanne asked about coaching and seemed interested in learning more; this could have been explored further
