**April 28, 2025**

**Regine's key takeaways**

- The need for a niche audience — she acknowledged the importance of shifting from a broad "general audience" approach to identifying and serving a specific, high-value niche that would find her work on disinformation in the Philippines truly useful and be willing to pay for it (e.g. diplomats, academics, researchers, NGOs, or organizations concerned with brand/reputation management)
- Utility drives monetization — for content to be monetizable, it must be perceived as valuable, and that value is directly tied to its utility for the target audience
- The book as a long-term goal — writing a book on the history of propaganda and disinformation in the Philippines remains a central long-term objective
- Newsletter as a foundational step — a newsletter is a practical initial step to build content, keep tabs on the disinformation landscape, and potentially serve as an archive for her book project
- Consultancy as a viable income stream — active consultancy opportunities (e.g. with the ICC) leverage her expertise and represent a clear path to income
- Concerns about the "creator" path — the general creator route (YouTube, TikTok) brings challenges: difficulty of sustainable monetization, pressure of constant content creation, and risk of online harassment
- Personal calling vs. burnout — she feels a strong obligation to cover disinformation but is aware of the emotional toll and need to manage potential burnout

**Regine's planned actions**

- Complete master's dissertation by September
- Continue developing her book on disinformation in the Philippines, aiming for publication before the 2028 election, and seek a literary agent
- Further develop her "Disinformation Nation" project, likely starting with a newsletter after clearly defining the target audience and value proposition
- Conduct audience research — speak with potential users (embassy contacts, researchers) to understand their pain points and what they'd pay for
- Continue with existing consultancy projects (ICC, Berlin Media) and look for similar opportunities
- Consider structuring content in "seasons" to manage commitment and allow re-evaluation
- Approach the Puma Podcast pitch cautiously, given the challenges of monetization and audience building

---

**Alan's coaching insights & advice**

- Focus on the "who pays?" question — consistently pushed Regine to identify who would specifically pay for her work, as this is the cornerstone of a sustainable model
- Value utility over general interest — content must be useful to a defined audience to command a price; likened general interest content to "art on a wall" — nice, but not necessarily something people will pay for
- Niche down for sustainability — targeting a "general audience" makes monetization difficult; finding a very specific niche is more viable
- Understand the cost of misinformation to specific entities — exploring the tangible impact and cost of misinformation for specific organizations (e.g. a bank like BPI, the ICC) uncovers clear use cases and value propositions
- Structured data as a product — creating structured databases about disinformation networks (e.g. mapping paid creators) as a distinct, potentially high-value product for researchers or PR companies
- The "1,000 true fans" principle — aim for a smaller number of dedicated, paying customers rather than chasing mass views
- Creator path vs. entrepreneurial path — different mindsets: mass-reach creator (chasing algorithms, high volume output, public scrutiny) vs. entrepreneur serving a niche (focused on specific client needs and value)
- Permission to evolve and end projects — working in "chapters" or "seasons," giving oneself permission to end projects if they are no longer serving their purpose
- Consider the personal cost — repeatedly reminded Regine to consider the emotional and mental toll of engaging with disinformation and online harassment, and to factor in the kind of life she wants to lead
