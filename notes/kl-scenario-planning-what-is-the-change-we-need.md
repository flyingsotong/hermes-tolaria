**What are we doing?**

- Scenario planning — identify critical uncertainties and develop plausible scenarios and responses to changes coming to media in the next year.

- Where will we be this time next year? What do we need to thrive?

**What is the outcome we want?**

- An understanding of what needs to change in the funding landscape to meet new opportunities — not to fix old problems

**What is the output we want?**

- A theory of change statement and report with specific steps for the donor community to get behind

**How we got here**

- Timeline on the wall — what were some of the biggest changes to the industry in the past 30 years?

- Mark the start of Yahoo (1994), Craigslist (1995), Malaysiakini (1999), Facebook (2004), etc.

- Get people to fill the timeline from then till today (Post-It notes)

- Intended outcome

 - Ask: What can we learn from this timeline? What are the trends you see?

 - Ask: What has changed? What hasn’t changed?

 - First, an understanding that the biggest changes started way back then

 - Second, tech disruption isn't new — the problem is that our definitions of the work we do hasn't changed when everything else has (tech, audience habits, government policy)

- Timeline has two layers — tech change, political changes

**Future state — where do we need to go?**

- Language change — from now on, we talk only about the future and not the problems of the past or present

- Ask: What are the biggest changes you see in the next two years?

- Ask: What will that do to your business?

- Ask: What has to change for you? What will happen if you don't change?

- Ask: What do you need to make this change happen? What’s holding you back?

- Intended outcome: An articulation of the change that needs to happen and a commitment to own that change (not your donor’s problem)

1. Definitions: What is journalism? You can't sell what you don't know. We occupy a very small space in the information ecosystem. We compete with anyone with expertise and a following

2. Creators: Partner with creators — identify their needs and support their work. They can't monetize beyond brand deals and are married to the platforms. Find opportunity in that.

3. Costs: What can you do to cut costs now

4. Build micro teams. Companies over-hire.


**Trends**

- Information ecosystem is much bigger than the media ecosystem — politicians have ignored media for a reason

 - Modi, who was elected to power in 2014, has not appeared in a single press conference in India since.

 - Marcos election

- People with specific expertise are in competition with professional media. This is the information space. The media company of the future will not collaborate with sector experts and fill their slimmer staffs with people obsessed with their topic areas. The days of the generalist are mostly over. — Brian Morrissey

- There is a learned helplessness due to a generation of being buffeted by external forces over which publishers had little control. We are all prone to fear and sensing danger around us. This can lead to rejecting new approaches altogether because that’s “not how it works.” — Brian Morrissey

Mass media is the empty calories of the information ecosystem.
