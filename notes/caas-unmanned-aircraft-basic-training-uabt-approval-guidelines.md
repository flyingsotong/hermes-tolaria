# CAAS Unmanned Aircraft Basic Training (UABT) Approval Guidelines

**1. Introduction**

**1.1 Training Requirements**

- Pursuant to ANR-101, a person must not provide UA basic training (UABT) unless they hold a UABT approval.

**1.2 UA Basic Training (UABT) Purpose**

- UABT is mandatory for UA users operating a UA with a total mass exceeding 1.5kg but not exceeding 7kg for recreation or education purposes.

- UABT provides the UA user with the relevant knowledge to operate their UA safely and responsibly.

**2. Components of UA Basic Training (UABT)**

**2.1 UABT Administration**

- The UABT should be administered as an online course with a duration of approximately 2 hours, including the quiz.

- The UA Basic Training Organisation (UABTO) should ensure that online UABT includes, but is not limited to, the following components:

 - (a) Means to secure user registration and login that is compliant with the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) (refer to Appendix 1).

 - (b) Courseware (refer to Appendix 2).

 - (c) A randomly generated 20 questions picked from a question bank that consists of at least 50 questions and corresponds with the module referenced in Appendix 2, to be administered.

 - (d) The quiz will only be considered completed when all 20 questions are correctly answered:

 - (i) If a user answers a question incorrectly, a different question from the corresponding module in the question bank is generated.

 - (ii) All questions from the question bank must be exhausted before recycling the same question.

 - (e) Means to provide trainees with a Certificate of Completion after successful completion of the quiz. The certificate should include the trainee’s full name as per the NRIC (or other equivalent ID such as the passport), course title, date of completion of the UABT, name of UABTO, UABTO’s Accountable Manager’s signature, and reference/serial number.

**3. Application for UABT Approval**

**3.1 Application Process**

- An application for a UABT approval should be made via <https://esoms.caas.gov.sg>, and accompanied by the relevant application fee (refer to Appendix 4).

- Complete applications with all required documents and fees are typically processed within 2 months.

- Incomplete submissions or complex applications may require additional processing time.

- Applicants are encouraged to submit their applications well in advance of their planned training start date to allow for processing time.

**3.2 Required Information for Application**

- The applicant is required to submit the following information as part of the application:

 - (a) ACRA BizFile / Certificate of incorporation

 - (b) CV of Accountable Manager (AM)

 - (c) A manual including:

 - (i) Description of organisational structure, including the roles and responsibilities of personnel involved in activities relating to the approval.

 - (ii) Description of the UABT programme such as the training syllabus, modality of quiz, estimated duration for completion, modality of certificate issuance, intended training fee, etc.

 - (iii) Description of the record keeping system such as the list of records to be retained, storage/retrieval/disposal procedures, etc.

 - (iv) Description of the online system implementation such as platform compatibility, user process flow, quiz administration, payment details, etc.

 - (v) Description of the quality of the online system, including a declaration of compliance to IM8 and Personal Data Protection Act.

 - (d) E-learning script detailing the programme outline, on-screen graphics and text, voice over (if any), user interactions, etc.

 - (e) Question bank that consists of a minimum of 50 questions.

**3.3 Accountable Manager (AM) Role**

- The AM is normally the chief executive officer (CEO) who by virtue of position has overall (including financial) responsibility to run the organisation.

- If the AM is not the CEO, he/she should have the overall responsibility to run the organisation and have direct access to the CEO.

**3.4 Accountable Manager (AM) Responsibilities**

- The AM is responsible to:

 - (a) Ensure that UABTO complies with regulatory requirements set by CAAS.

 - (b) Ensure that sufficient funding is available to conduct activities to the approved standard.

**4. Records**

**4.1 Record Retention**

- The UABTO must maintain the following records for the specified retention period:

| Records | Minimum Retention Period |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Trainee’s name, date of birth, contact address, contact number, email address and last 4 alphanumeric characters of the NRIC/FIN/Passport | Five years after the trainee has completed UABT |
| Date and time of which trainee completed the training | Five years after the trainee has completed UABT |
| Reference/serial number of the certificate issued | Five years after the trainee has completed UABT |

**4.2 Record Integrity**

- The UABTO should ensure that:

 - (a) The integrity of records is maintained.

 - (b) The records are not deleted or altered.

 - (c) There is a backup system of the records to provide continuity.

**4.3 Record Provision to CAAS**

- The UABTO should provide CAAS with records monthly or when required by CAAS.

**5. Validity of UABT Approval**

**5.1 Approval Duration**

- The UABT approval will be valid for up to 1 year.

**6. Renewal of UABT Approval**

**6.1 Renewal Application Process**

- An application for renewal of approval should be made via <https://esoms.caas.gov.sg>, and accompanied by the prescribed application fee (refer to Appendix 4).

- Complete applications with all required documents and fees are typically processed within 2 months.

- Incomplete submissions may require additional processing time.

- Applicants are encouraged to submit their applications at least 2 months before the expiry of the approval to allow for processing time.

**6.2 Required Information for Renewal Application**

- The applicant is required to submit the following information as part of the application:

 - (a) Latest revision of the organisation manual if there are changes since the last issuance of the approval.

 - (b) Latest revision of the e-learning script and question bank if there are changes since the last issuance of the approval.

 - (c) Access credentials to the UABT system for CAAS’ review and verification.

**7. Variation of UABT Approval**

**7.1 Notification of Changes**

- The approved UABTO must notify CAAS if there are changes to the courseware or question bank.

- If there are any changes to the online system, key personnel, or ownership of the UABTO, the approved UABTO should also notify CAAS.

**7.2 Variation Application Process**

- An application for variation of approval should be made via <https://esoms.caas.gov.sg>, and accompanied by the prescribed application fee (refer to Appendix 4).

- Complete applications with all required documents and relevant fees are typically processed within 1 month.

- Incomplete submissions or complex applications may require additional processing time.

- As approval by CAAS is required before the organisation can proceed with implementation of the change, applicants are encouraged to submit their applications early to allow for processing time.

**8. Transferability**

**8.1 Approval Transferability**

- A UABT approval granted to an organisation is not transferable except as a result of a change in ownership.

- A new application is required in the event of a unique entity number (UEN) change.
