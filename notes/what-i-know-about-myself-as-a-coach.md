# What I know about myself as a coach

**What I do well**
I build frameworks that stick. Every engagement produces a "sticky concept" the client takes away — The Oxygen Metaphor, The Uncomfortable Truth, The Launching Muscle, The Steward Narrative. Most coaches ask good questions; I give clients new language for their own experience.
I excavate the real problem. I consistently hear past the presenting issue to the underlying one — credential-seeking as a confidence crisis, business questions as grief, tactical questions as identity anxiety.
I create safety through self-disclosure. Sharing my own struggles consistently unlocks client vulnerability. The risk is that I use it too early and shift the focus to myself.

---

**My clearest gap**
I fall into the consulting trap at exactly the wrong moment — when the client opens their heart, I respond with strategy.
The pattern: client says something vulnerable ("I feel like a failure," "I don't know what the point is") and I reach for frameworks, analogies, market logic. I follow them into their head — the safer, more intellectual space — when they've just cracked open emotionally.
My media expertise makes this worse. It's too easy to reach for.
**The fix:** When a client says something that sounds like defeat or doubt — pause. Don't explain the market. Explain them.
> *"I want to stop there. You said [x]. Tell me more about that."*

---
**What I sometimes miss**
**Making clients do the work in-session.** I name the insight for them rather than waiting for them to arrive at it. Better question: *"Based on what we've been talking about — what do you think the uncomfortable truth is?"*
**Anchoring the close.** Clients leave inspired but without a stated, self-directed next step. Before ending every session: *"What is the one commitment you're making to yourself before we speak again?"*
**Acknowledging courage before moving forward.** When a client has done something hard between sessions — a difficult conversation, a decision, a letter written — name it before moving on. That action is the work. Don't skip it.
---
**What I know about myself**
- I can second-guess a client's desire for change and hold back
- I can hold back on confrontation
- I know when I'm doing it — there's a signal. Pay attention to it.
**The question to ask myself mid-session:** *Am I coaching the person, or am I consulting the problem?*
