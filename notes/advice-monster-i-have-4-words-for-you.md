Do you know why feeding that advice monster is so rewarding? Because in society, we are praised for having answers. Doesn’t have to be the best answer, just having an answer is usually enough to receive that instant gratification.

The first thing I ask my coaches to do is to stop jumping in to solve their clients problems.

**When we try to solve for our clients, we are employing more than just an advice monster, we’re inviting the judgement monster too!**

Here is where I will challenge you:

What makes you believe you can solve someones problems?

What sort of mindset do you think you are activating by viewing the clients situation and a problem to be solved?

What makes you believe that your client cannot be the hero of their own story?

I will add one last thing that I think you may need to hear: Just because you did not provide advice, does not mean you are incompetent and don’t know how to provide value.

Okay, now that we’ve gained some new perspectives, I will provide you with 3 potential steps to slow down (if not altogether stop) that monster when it comes up to eat:

When you’re giving advice, it becomes about you, rather than your client. Bring the spotlight on your client. Do this through questioning.

When you catch yourself giving advice, stop yourself. Right there and then. Again, shift the focus back to your client.

Sit back in your chair, literally.**Create a physical movement to remind yourself that you are not in the drivers seat, your client is.**
