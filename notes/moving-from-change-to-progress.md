# Moving from 'change' to 'progress'

She explained: “We shouldn’t use the word ‘change’. When you tell people to ‘change’, they hear ‘What I’m doing right now is not good enough’. And with that, they are already in defense.”

Instead of talking about change, we should be talking about progress. Everyone wants to progress. It is a concept people naturally gravitate towards because it suggests improvement and forward movement.

What I saw in nature as change, isn’t change. It’s progress, moving forward through natural cycles, progressing.

Progress isn’t always pretty. It can be awfully painful. It can mean we leave behind what doesn’t work anymore, to make space for new things. In nature, for example, a heavy branch falls during a storm to bring the tree back in balance, or a forest fire clears undergrowth to make breathing room for the larger trees.

When we talk about change, we implicitly talk about the need to succeed. Change needs to be successful. That is a big ask for everyone involved. In our collective minds we’re thinking: Will we be able to succeed? And with heaps of effort, some will, and some won’t, leaving us with that sense of failure.

Yet, progress is just about progressing from where we are today. Moving. Any progress, no matter how small or large, is progress. It makes us ask different questions. Instead of ‘will we succeed’, we now ask ‘what does progress need from me?’.
