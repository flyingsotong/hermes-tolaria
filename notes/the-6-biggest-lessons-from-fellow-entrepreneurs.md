# The 6 biggest lessons from fellow entrepreneurs

<https://www.reddit.com/r/Entrepreneur/comments/1oqxcxy/what_is_the_biggest_lesson_being_an_entrepreneur/>

I recently came across a great discussion where entrepreneurs shared their single biggest lesson. The insights were too good not to share.

Here are the key takeaways:

**1. Action beats everything**

The most common lesson: "Execution beats ideas every single time." Stop perfecting plans and just start. Progress comes from doing and iterating, not just imagining. As another user put it, "Progress, not perfection."

_"If you had to launch 'Version 0.1' of your project tomorrow, what would you ship?"_

**2. Resilience is your superpower**

Entrepreneurship is tough. The key is to "keep going," even "when you're going through hell." The consensus is that there is no real failure: "either you win, or you learn."

_"How can you reframe a current challenge from 'a problem I'm facing' to 'a lesson I'm learning'?"_

**3. Consistency compounds**

Success isn't built in a day. "The small, boring things you do daily" —the follow-ups, the emails, the small tweaks— "end up being what actually builds momentum."

_"What 'boring' but essential task do you procrastinate on most, and how could you reframe it as your most important 'momentum-builder'?"_

**4. It's all about people**

A few strong lessons on people:

-**Hiring:** "Hire Slow, Fire Fast."

-**Networking:** "Networking is everything." Be genuine, friendly, and listen for people's needs.

-**Sales:** You must learn to "sell. Sell to clients. Sell to partners and investors. Sell to your team. Sell to your own self."

-**Reality Check:** "No employee will care as much as you do."

_"When assessing talent, how do you distinguish between skills (which can be taught) and values (which are fundamental)?"_

**5. The market is the only judge**

You can't fool the market. It is the "ultimate truth-teller." You must "look for demand" and learn to "view life through their eyes, not yours" to understand what customers truly need.

_"What strongly-held belief do you have about your customers that you haven't actually validated in the last 30 days?"_

**6. Get out of your own way**

Ultimately, you are the biggest factor. "Nobody cares about the good, or the bad, as much as you do." You have to trust your gut, show up for yourself, and not get stuck in the small details that prevent you from launching.

_"If you were coaching someone in your exact situation, what 'blind spot' would you tell them to pay attention to?"_
