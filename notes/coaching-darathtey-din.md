**September 13, 2023**

- A catch up chat of sorts — she almost died from dengue climbing up Kota Kinabalu
- Maybe that made her reflect on things differently
- She really took the time and energy to let me know that these sessions have been really helpful and that this matters greatly to her
- This call was more about her expressing gratitude than wanting a way forward
- My insight: this too is coaching — perhaps one of the best outcomes of what coaching could be

**July 17, 2023**

- "I'm burnt out" — she started the call talking about her stress. Says it's the first time she's been able to articulate all of this
- Tells me the big stone, small sand thinking has really helped her understand her stress and priorities
- Talked about pain, depression, suicide — these are real for her
- Her insights: ...I didn't ask and I don't know why
- My insights: sometimes people just want to talk about how they feel — not about being coached in any particular direction; I need to set aside time to read and learn more about these topics

**June 23, 2023**

- Wanted to understand about pricing, time management, energy management
- Her insights: my pricing is not my value, I can add 30% margin, and what's the worst that could happen
- My insights: I'm still learning that balance of giving advice and drawing it out from them. I felt I was giving advice 70% of the time on this call

**May 15, 2023**

- Wants to start a system (maybe Notion) to organize all her work — but won't let herself
- Perfectionist — worried that she'll get sucked into tweaking it
- Procrastinates — pointed out that it's a factor of her need to create perfect work but also her worry that she can't do it right — ie. failure and disappointment
- "Why do you procrastinate?"... "No one has ever asked me that before."

**April 27, 2023**

- Perfectionist — cynical about the world, her company, NGOs, development work
- Idealist — wants to be around people who think and work the way she does — doesn't feel like there's anyone around her who does that
- Needs to find ways to identify success on her own terms — doesn't give herself permission to congratulate herself
- Grew up poor — worried that all of this could end, and that creates anxiety for her
- Tells herself that she's not good enough — instead, she should tell herself what she's great at: listening, asking questions, being curious. Two parts to the same coin
