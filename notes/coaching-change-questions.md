**Key coaching objectives**
1. Separate their identity as a journalist from their career choices
2. Identify transferable skills and unique combinations — and gaps
3. Set clear decision criteria for when and how to pivot
4. Build confidence in exploring alternatives

---

**Identity and values**
- Reflect on your best days in journalism — what specific elements made them fulfilling?
- If money weren't a concern, what parts of journalism would you still want to keep in your life?
- Which aspects of being a journalist are core to your identity, and which are just part of the job?

**Skills translation**
- Beyond writing and reporting, what skills have you developed that energize you most?
- Looking at your policy background and data expertise, which industries or roles have caught your attention?
- When you think about your strongest stories, what unique approach or skills did you bring that others didn't?

**Financial reality**
- What's your minimum viable income need, and what would be your ideal target?
- How long can you sustain your current situation while exploring options?
- What financial milestones would make you feel secure enough to stay in journalism?

**Market assessment**
- Which organizations are doing innovative work at the intersection of journalism, policy, and data?
- What trends are you seeing in how journalism skills are valued in adjacent industries?
- Where do you see your combination of skills being better compensated?

**Risk and transition**
- What's the worst that could happen if you tried a potential pivot direction and it didn't work out?
- What would a gradual transition look like versus a complete pivot?
- What's holding you back from exploring alternatives more actively?

**Personal network**
- Who in your network has made a successful transition from journalism?
- Which of your connections work in areas you're curious about?
- How are you currently positioning your journey to your professional network?

**Future vision**
- When you imagine yourself five years from now, what kind of work are you most excited about?
- What skills or experiences do you want to develop, regardless of your career path?
- How do you want to stay connected to journalism even if it's not your primary income source?

**Experimentation**
- What small experiments could you run now to test different career directions?
- How might you use your current freelance work to explore potential pivot areas?
- What side projects could help you build credibility in new areas?

**Reflection and deepening**
- How does the answer to that question change the way you look at this?
- What doors could it open for you?

> "That's an intellectual question, but let's look at the leadership one: Why is that specific answer important to you right now? What are you hoping that data will give you permission to do?"
