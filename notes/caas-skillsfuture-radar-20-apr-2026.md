## CAAS schemes

**Aviation Outreach Fund (AOF)**
Relevance: Medium

Previously known as the Youth Outreach Seed Fund, the AOF funds schools, industry associations, societies, and student groups to develop customised aviation activities — curriculum development, enrichment programmes, competitions, and aviation teaching facilities. CAAS provides up to S$40,000 per initiative. Direct fit for Dronefly.sg if structured as an outreach programme with an educational partner, or for Magenta Debrief if it formalises aviation education content with a school or aeroclub as co-applicant. The fund is oriented toward youth-facing activity, so the pitch would need to frame the content through that lens. Status: Open (ongoing). Last updated 21 March 2024.

Source: https://www.caas.gov.sg/public-passengers/learn-about-singapore-aviation/aviation-outreach-fund

---

**SAA Fellowships (Singapore Aviation Academy)**
Relevance: Low

CAAS runs several international aviation training fellowships — Singapore Cooperation Programme (SCP), Singapore-ICAO Developing Countries Training Programme (DCTP), AFCAC, ACAO, LACAC, and Asia Pacific Training Awards. Over 11,000 fellowships and scholarships awarded to date. These are designed for aviation professionals from overseas attending SAA courses in Singapore. Not applicable to Alan as a Singapore-based founder. Status: Ongoing. Last updated 20 February 2026.

Source: https://www.caas.gov.sg/saa/fellowships

**SkillsFuture for Digital Workplace**
Relevance: Medium

Described on the SkillsFuture individuals page as "Equipping Singaporean adults with digital literacy skills for the digital economy." This programme funds structured digital skills courses — including AI tools, content production, and workflow automation. Directly applicable to Splice Nano and Splice Media's training offerings. If Splice qualifies as a training provider (requires SSG approval), courses built around AI-augmented workflows could be listed as subsidised. Status: Ongoing.

Source: https://www.skillsfuture.gov.sg/initiatives/individuals

## My picks

**1. Aviation Outreach Fund — Dronefly.sg**
The strongest actionable lead. At up to S$40,000, the AOF could fund structured content development for Dronefly.sg if framed as a youth aviation education initiative. The key move: find a school or youth aviation group (e.g., Air Scouts) willing to co-apply. CAAS has funded curriculum development and aviation teaching facilities before — Dronefly's regulatory content is exactly that. Application worth scoping now.

**2. SkillsFuture for Digital Workplace — Splice Nano**
If Splice can get SSG-registered as a course provider, AI-augmented workflow training (the core of Splice Nano) could qualify for SkillsFuture Credit subsidies. This is a longer play — SSG registration is a process — but the payoff is recurring subsidy for learners, which lowers Splice Nano's price barrier significantly. First step: check SSG course provider registration requirements at tpgateway.gov.sg.
