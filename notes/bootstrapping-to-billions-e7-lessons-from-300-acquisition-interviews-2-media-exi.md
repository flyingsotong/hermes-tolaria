This interview with Lexi Grant, founder of _They Got Acquired_, offers crucial insights for bootstrapped founders considering an exit. The core message is to be proactive, not reactive, about selling your business. Key takeaways include selling before you burn out, understanding what makes a business attractive to buyers, and preparing for the often-underestimated due diligence process.
**Key Learnings**
**Why Smaller Exits Are Overlooked**
Many founders of smaller, bootstrapped businesses feel lost when it comes time to sell. This is because the M\&A market is incentivized to focus on larger, multi-million dollar deals, as they require a similar amount of work as smaller ones but yield higher commissions for advisors. Lexi Grant started _They Got Acquired_ to fill this information gap, providing stories and resources for founders navigating exits under $50 million.
**When and How to Sell Your Business**
-**Sell Before Burnout**: A common reason founders sell is burnout. However, the selling process itself is a "marathon." It's crucial to**start exploring an exit when you first feel the inkling of burnout**, not when you're completely exhausted, so you have the energy to see the process through.
-**Sell While You're Growing**: Buyers pay a premium for businesses with a clear growth trajectory. It's better to**sell while the business is still on an upward curve** rather than waiting for it to plateau or decline. This indicates future potential and leads to better valuation multiples.
-**Finding a Buyer**: Data from over 300 founder interviews shows that buyers are found in three main ways, each accounting for about a third of deals:
 1. Using a**broker or advisor**.
 2. Receiving**inbound interest** directly from a buyer.
 3. Finding a buyer through your**personal network**.
**Getting Your Business "Exit Ready"**
Being "exit ready" from day one is not just about selling; it's about building a healthier, more sustainable business for yourself.
-**Focus on Profit (EBITDA)**: While revenue is important, buyers are primarily concerned with your**bottom line (EBITDA)**. Increasing profitability benefits you immediately with more take-home cash and makes the business more valuable at exit.
-**Reduce Founder Dependency**: A business that heavily relies on its founder is difficult to sell. By implementing systems, processes, and a capable team, you make the business a**transferable asset**. This also makes it easier for you to run, reducing stress and workload.
-**Build Recurring Revenue**: Businesses with predictable, recurring revenue (like SaaS companies) command higher multiples. Even if you're not a SaaS company, incorporating recurring revenue models into an agency, e-commerce, or media business can significantly increase its value.
**The Biggest Challenge: Due Diligence**
The most underestimated part of the acquisition process is**due diligence**. It's often a full-time job in itself, and founders must continue running their business effectively during this period to prevent performance from declining. Proactively keeping your business financials and documentation clean and organized ("keeping your room clean") can make this stage much smoother.
