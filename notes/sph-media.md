# SPH Media

This is now our chance to get the media we want

CLT is just a**cool legal vehicle** to do this in a way that the money generated from the business goes back into the business.

**This isn't a business model.**It doesn't mean that this is suddenly a hot amazing news product that people want. It's just a corporate legal structure. So the work still has to be done.

Let's not forget how we got to this place anyway. SPH's media assets are not viable because despite the signs of change everywhere,**the company has failed in its media transformation**, in part because it saw itself as a newspaper company -- and therefore the need to save print at all costs.

On the other side of the equation, Singaporeans want to see critical reporting political issues. ST**doesn't have the credibility**to stand up when it comes to local politics.

And that's where the comparison with the Guardian fails. The Guardian is a profitable company today not because it's set up through a trust, but that it's supported by subscribers who believe in its mission of public interest journalism.

**Singapore needs to decide what kind of media it wants.**It is in our interest to have a credible media organization that reports freely and fairly. Fundamental importance in the fight against misinformation. And now that public funds are planned, public accountability is needed.

_The move would make Singapore the only advanced industrial country in the non-communist world with no major profit-driven news organisation. -- Cherian_
