**Pitch for airspeed:**
Changing the pitch angle of the aircraft (nose up or nose down) primarily affects its airspeed. Raising the nose (increasing pitch) will decrease airspeed, while lowering the nose (decreasing pitch) will increase airspeed, assuming constant power.
**Power for altitude:**
Adjusting the engine's power output primarily affects the aircraft's altitude. Increasing power will cause the aircraft to climb, while decreasing power will cause it to descend, assuming a constant pitch.
**Think of it this way:**
Pitch determines how the aircraft cuts through the air (speed) \
Power determines how much force is available to overcome gravity (altitude)
**Climb PAT → APT**
**Descent PAT → PAT**
