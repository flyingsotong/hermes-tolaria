**Setting the stage**

- Opening: What would you like to get out of this conversation today? What's your biggest challenge or area of focus?
- Goal setting: What do you want to achieve? Why is this important to you? How will you know when you've been successful?
- Reflection: What's working well right now? What could be improved? What have you learned so far?

---

**Exploring and expanding**

- Awareness: What are your thoughts and feelings about this situation? What are your strengths and weaknesses in this area?
- Challenge assumptions: How does that serve you? What are the other possibilities? What evidence is there for your perspective?
- Generating options: What are some creative solutions you can think of? What would someone else in your shoes do?

---

**Taking action**

- Accountability: What specific steps will you take next? When will you do them? How will you measure your progress?
- Overcoming obstacles: What challenges might you face? How will you handle them? What support do you need?
- Celebration: What did you learn from this experience? What are you proud of yourself for? How will you celebrate your accomplishments?

---

**Remember**

- Ask open-ended questions that encourage exploration and reflection
- Listen actively and pay attention to both verbal and nonverbal cues
- Be curious and non-judgmental
- Focus on empowering the person to find their own answers
- Adapt your questions to the specific context and individual
