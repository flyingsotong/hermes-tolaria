# Greenhouse Narrative report, July 2024

**Final Narrative Report: Splice MediaBOOMERANG NETWORK (THAILAND)GREENHOUSE PROJECTJuly 2024Greenhouse Project grant**

** **

**Overview**

In the first six months of the [Greenhouse](https://greenhousecowork.com/?fbclid=IwAR2R_10Kwh_3buVJ-Q9v6pw0EcXGixpcBZCy-iFgIr4vGoskA4auI2dzdUI) project, a venue was found and renovated to establish a unique co-working space and training venue in Chiang Mai, Thailand. The business was developed and launched successfully to the public in January 2023 and over the next 18 months we focused on growing the business and making it sustainable. The funds from the partners were used to support these activities, including purchase of equipment and furniture and hiring of key personnel.

The Greenhouse concept – a co-working space with bookable training rooms and event spaces, as well as a Myanmar tea house offering – is showing signs of strong support from the broader sector of media and development organisations with bookings and memberships taking place in the first few months of operation.

We are continuing to focus on attracting individuals – freelancers, journalists, members of the general public – to the space, running networking and other events to attract media workers, and consolidating the tea shop business, Gatone’s, into a profitable venture.

Over the past year, we have progressively increased our revenue, particularly from Gatone’s and hosting events and trainings, while more recently also reducing our expenditure to ensure the business is sustainable.

**Operations**

The first step was to identify and lease the physical space. Detailed plans were developed for renovation and contractors engaged to carry out the renovations. The space launched to the public on 30 January 2023 successfully. Key staff were put in place to operate the business, including events management, bookings and catering.

After the launch, the next phase of development was to focus on attracting individuals – freelancers, journalists, members of the general public – to the space; running networking and other events to attract media workers; and consolidating the tea shop business into a profitable venture.

Greenhouse has already become an important part of the media landscape in Chiang Mai. The training spaces for NGOs and others have been well booked, and the adjacent café offering has also opened and is trading well. The Gatone’s teashop has performed much better than expected and helped to bring in more foot traffic, which improves awareness of the co-working and training spaces. It will take some time to fully develop the businesses and increase public awareness of the service offering but for now the indications are very promising.

Greenhouse has not only given the Frontier team a base from which to operate, but also welcomed many people, both those working in the media industry and those from other fields. Among the organisations to have used the space are International Centre for Journalists, WAN-IFRA, Exile Hub, AFP, Deutsche Welle, Amnesty International, TedX and Beam Education. Since launching in January 2023, we have hosted nine commercial events, five in-house events, one in-house class that ran for two months, and 22 training/workshops/meetings. Altogether these events and trainings hosted more than 1,500 participants/attendees.

As of June 2024, Greenhouse had almost 100 members, who collectively booked 2,500 hours of co-working space over the preceding six months. We have also allowed 30 Myanmar people living in exile in Chiang Mai use the space for free, including five photographers for exhibition space, five journalists for the podcast recording room, and 20 students for basic English lessons.

Over the past year we also focused on building up the teashop business, Gatone’s. When we first opened, we were ordering food from an outside provider, which meant that we had limited menu options and often sold out or had food leftover that went to waste. In the middle of 2023 we hired our own team to prepare the food and launched an expanded all-day Burmese breakfast menu. As a result, we have been able to keep costs down and increase our profit margins. Having our own team also enabled us to sell food and drinks through Grab delivery, which has caused in a significant increase in sales, and also helped to improve awareness about Gatone’s and Greenhouse. More recently, we introduced a better system for inventory control that results in less waste.

From late 2023, we started using Greenhouse for Frontier events, something we had previously been reluctant to do for security reasons. After conducting a more thorough security assessment we felt the risks could be managed. In December 2023, we hosted our first “Frontier Fridays” event, a monthly informal meet-up for Frontier readers (members and non-members), attracting around 80 people. Through 2024 we are expecting to hold around one Frontier event a month at Greenhouse (we have so far held events in January, February and June, and two in March). This helps to grow Frontier’s membership programme by offering benefits to members thus incentivising new signups and retention; to increase awareness about Greenhouse and Gatone’s; and to directly increase Greenhouse and Gatone’s revenues, through venue rental (as Frontier pays to use the space) and food and beverage sales.

**Financial sustainability**

We expect Greenhouse will contribute to Frontier’s financial sustainability in the long term through revenue from co-working fees, training spaces, events and other offerings. The launch was hugely successful and Greenhouse’s training spaces for NGOs and others have been well booked. The adjacent café offering has also opened and is trading well. It will take some time to fully develop the businesses and increase public awareness of the service offering but for now the indications are very promising.

In the six months of operations (January-June 2023), revenue was above US$20,000. In the second half of 2023 (July-December), Greenhouse revenue (including Gatone’s) was US$30,465. While revenue was slightly lower in the first half of 2024 at about $24,000, we managed to decrease our operating costs by 27% to $42,000.

Based on anticipated ongoing running costs, Greenhouse is not far off break-even levels, although more promotional work will be needed to further grow our revenue from memberships and training/event bookings.

**Challenges**

The launch of such a major initiative in a relatively small period of time was not without its challenges, however nothing preventing the further success of the project at this stage.

·         Delays in construction / contractors occurred in late 2022, partly due to delays in securing additional funds. These were resolved but did delay the launch of the venture by some weeks;

·         Staffing the venture has been challenging at times, particularly finding people with the right skills. However, the business currently has the right team and is working well.

·         Reaching the target markets of individual co-working space users, media development NGOs, media workers and teashop customers is an ongoing challenge, with different strategies in place for different markets. Some groups (e.g. NGOs) have responded very quickly and positively.

·         There were no significant changes to the original project. However, membership sales (for use of the co-working space) have not been as strong as expected due to the high level of competition within Chiang Mai.

·         There are also some challenges using the same space for co-working and training/meetings, as organisations often want to book the whole space for privacy or confidentiality reasons. As a result, memberships have become less of a focus for Greenhouse.

·         The weather has a significant effect on business. Because of the smoky season from March to May, we are not able to hold many outdoor events at that time. Similarly, sales at Gatone’s are lower because some people leave Chiang Mai temporarily.

**Financial report**

A financial report of the first and second years of grant expenditure is attached. Expenditure was significantly higher than expected in the first year. However, this can nearly all be attributed to two areas. Renovation costs were about $30,000 higher than budgeted, primarily due to the cost of labour. Equipment purchases were about $15,000 higher than expected, as we had underestimated the cost of furniture and equipment for the tea house (particularly the coffee machine). These were all one-off expenses. Given our ongoing costs have been lower than anticipated, we were able to remain within the budget by the end of the second year.

**Evaluation**

Overall, we believe that Greenhouse has been a success in several respects. It has been a safe space in Chiang Mai for journalists and media organisations, including the Frontier team. We have hosted many trainings and in-house (Frontier) events, the latter helping to build awareness about Greenhouse and increase foot traffic. Cultural events have also helped to engage a wider audience. Gatone’s teashop has been very successful and is already turning a profit, contributing to the overall sustainability of the project.

Some facts and figures to demonstrate the success of the Greenhouse project so far:

-          Hosted nine client events, including six Frontier events

-          Organised and hosted five in-house events

-          Booked 22 trainings/workshops/meetings covering around 50 days in total

-          Attracted well over 1,500 attendees in total to these events

-          Sold about 100 memberships to the co-working space

-          Managed on ground activities for Splice Beta in 2022 and 2023

-          Arranged one in-house speaking class, which ran for two months

-          Hosted our first hybrid panel event in March 2024

-          Held a range of cultural events, including three photo exhibitions and a Burmese food night
