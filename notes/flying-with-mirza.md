Picking a runway in accordance to the current wind conditions at the aerodrome:

1. Winds reported on Metar/TAF indicates where the winds are coming FROM. Example if winds are reported at 27010 - winds are coming FROM the direction 270 degrees at 10 knots and NOT blowing towards the direction of 270 degrees at 10 knots

2. Under normal circumstances, aircraft will takeoff into the wind (upwind).

3. To determine which runway to use, pilots NEED to know what runways are available at the airport they're taking off and landing from. From that knowledge, we pick the runway to use that has the Maximum headwind and minimal crosswind.

This is how you decode a METAR. I would expect each one of you to be able to determine and justify your choice of runway usage prior to the commencement of every sim session.
