# Drone Guidance systems

**Summary of Key Differences**
-**LiDAR** and**Vision-based** systems are best for creating**detailed maps** and understanding the drone's environment in 3D, with LiDAR providing superior, highly accurate depth information regardless of object color or texture.
-**Infrared** is unique in its ability to**"see" in the dark** or through smoke by sensing heat, making it invaluable for specific industrial and public safety applications.
-**Ultrasound** is the simplest and most**cost-effective** for**very short-range** distance checks, primarily used for tasks like maintaining a constant altitude close to the ground (ground tracking) and basic low-speed obstacle detection.
| | | | | |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Feature | Infrared (IR) | Vision-based (Visual) | Ultrasound (Sonar) | LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) |
|**Working Principle** | Detects**heat signatures** (thermal radiation) emitted by objects.**Thermal imaging** translates temperature differences into visible images. | Uses standard**cameras** and**computer vision** algorithms (like image processing, object recognition, and SLAM) to map and interpret the environment. | Emits high-frequency**sound waves** and measures the**Time-of-Flight (ToF)** of the echo to calculate distance. | Emits rapid**laser pulses** and measures the**Time-of-Flight (ToF)** of the reflected light to create a 3D**point cloud** map. |
|**Range** | Variable, typically good for**medium-to-long range** applications like surveillance and search and rescue. |**Short to Long** range, highly dependent on camera resolution and processing power. | Very**Short range** (typically < 10 meters, often < 5 meters). |**Medium to Long range** (can be hundreds of meters). |
|**Environmental Reliability** | Excellent in**low light**,**darkness**, or through obscurants like**smoke** or**light fog**, as it sees heat, not visible light. | Highly reliant on**light conditions** (poor in low light/darkness) and scene features. Can be affected by glare/occlusions. | Unaffected by light, color, smoke, or dust. Performance can be affected by**wind** or large**temperature gradients** in the air. | Generally robust. Not significantly affected by light, but can be degraded by**heavy rain** or**dense fog**. |
|**Data Output** | 2D thermal image showing**temperature distribution** (heat map). | 2D image/video. Used to derive depth, distance, object position, and mapping features. |**1D distance measurement** to the nearest solid object in the direction of the sensor. | Highly accurate, dense**3D point cloud** (3D map) of the environment. |
|**Common Use on Drones** |**Search and Rescue** (locating people/animals),**Inspections** (identifying overheating components or heat loss). |**Navigation** (Visual Odometry),**Obstacle Avoidance**,**Localization**,**Surveillance**,**Mapping** (Photogrammetry). |**Altitude holding** (low-altitude),**Precision Landing**,**Short-range obstacle avoidance** (especially in simple, near-field scenarios). |**High-precision Mapping** and Surveying (topographic),**Complex Obstacle Avoidance** for autonomous navigation. |
|**Cost** |**High** (Thermal cameras are expensive). |**Low to Moderate** (Standard cameras are cheap; high-end systems with powerful processing are more expensive). |**Very Low** (Simple sensor). |**High** (Complex, rotating mechanical components or high-density solid-state arrays). |
