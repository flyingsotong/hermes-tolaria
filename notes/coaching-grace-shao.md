**February 17, 2025**

**What Grace wanted to achieve:**

- Guidance on how to grow her newly established business combining a newsletter focused on China tech/AI insights with consulting work
- Clarity on how to make her business more "legitimate" beyond being a small boutique operation
- Understanding of how to position herself in the evolving media landscape
- Looking to connect with others in similar situations and expand her network

**Key insights and awareness gained:**

1. Reframing her business definition: shifted from seeing herself as "in media" to understanding she's providing valuable insights that help people make investment decisions; recognized that her newsletter isn't just content but serves as brand-building and credibility establishment.
2. Product-market fit clarity: understood the importance of deeply understanding her users' needs rather than focusing on output formats; realized she should engage more with her readers to understand how they use her insights; learned that scale isn't necessarily about reaching massive numbers but could mean serving a smaller, high-value audience well.
3. Business model understanding: gained clarity that her consulting work is her primary revenue stream while her newsletter serves as a marketing/credibility tool; understood that she doesn't need to monetize her newsletter through subscriptions if it serves a different strategic purpose.

**Areas for improvement:**

1. Earlier structure: could have established clearer coaching parameters at the start — Grace mentioned she wasn't sure how to use the call.
2. More explicit action items: could have helped Grace articulate specific next steps more clearly before moving to discussing Splice.
3. Time management: the conversation shifted significantly to discussing Splice's business model in the latter part. While this was valuable context, it took time away from focusing on Grace's immediate needs.

**What worked:**

- Challenged her assumptions about what constitutes "media" and "journalism"
- Helped her reframe her thinking about scale and success
- Provided practical examples and analogies
- Offered concrete networking opportunities and follow-up support
- Created a safe space for her to discuss her concerns about operating in Hong Kong's complex environment

The session was particularly effective because you balanced providing direct advice (which she sought) with helping her develop new perspectives on her business.
