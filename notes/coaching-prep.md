**Objective: What is the one insight that your client needs today?**

Start, Stop, Continue. How to plan a regret-free future

**A coach offers**

1. Awareness
2. Choice
3. Responsibility

The main role of the life coach is to enable and empower the client. This is achieved using the "power of commitment" as leverage. Once clients agree to an activity they are committed to do it. This commitment is powerfully linked with the client's identity. The life coach taps into this power.

**It is your job as the coach to help to identify the interference and remove it. It has nothing to do with giving advice or imposing knowledge on your clients. Coaching is about removing the obstructions and not adding any new ones.**

Concentrate on balancing all the areas of your clients' lives. Believe that your clients have all the resources they need to solve their own problems and that it is your job to help them remove the obstacles that prevent this happening.

**Principles**

- Coaching is about raising **awareness** — not necessarily to solve a problem
- "The process of helping others to have their own insight"
- **You don't have to be right** — you just need to have the process
- Coach the Person, Not the Problem
- Are you coaching the problem, or are you coaching the person?
- **Assume the client has the answers** — offer them as many opportunities as possible to make their own decisions
- Did I raise **awareness**?
- Did I offer **choice**?
- Did I provide **responsibility**?
- Did I build trust?
- Understand the difference between **Goal** and **goal**. Do not take the goal that the client brings at face value.
- **Partnership**: We're doing this together
- **Permission**: Am I on the right track? Is this where you want to go?
- **Perception**: What am I seeing that you're not? What are you seeing?
- Bring energy and give it
- Awareness leads to choice... Choice leads to responsibility. **What exactly does the coachee need in terms of insight?**
- Do not take away someone's responsibility to come to their own solution
- Leaders are humans, like all coachees — focus on the person, not the box, nor their title. **If you're going in with a solution in mind, you're coaching the box**

**Contracting**

- Make sure that the organization's goal and the coachee's goal is transparent and aligned
- Priority is with the coachee — that's the trust priority
- Trust is the First Principle — protect that at all costs!
- Have you been coached before? What are your expectations?

**Sessions**

**Rules of engagement**

- Confidential — no one will know the contents of what we say here
- I'll keep notes for myself

**Starting questions**

- What do you want to work on today? (Puts the onus on coachee)
- What do you need for yourself today?
- **What is the one thing you want to get out of today? What is the insight you're looking for?**
- What can we work on together today? (Shared path)
- How can I serve you today? (I don't need your story — just tell me what you need)
- Why is this important to you right now?
- Play your entire movie, not just the clip that led to this belief. What do you see?
- How does (the "problem") come up in your thoughts?
- How do you notice that thought coming up? When does it get triggered?
- Create degrees of separation and focus on your thoughts which are in your control.
- *Stop Self-Sabotage*, Dr. Judy Ho, PhD
- Antecedent > Behavior > Consequences
- Ask in the middle: are we on the right track?

**If you see something, say something**

- Call out the inherent **beliefs, values, attitudes**
- Name the connections that the client is making in their heads
- Get comfortable with sharing an observation
- Who benefits from you playing this role (victim, rescuer, enabler)? Why?

**The topic is not the agenda. Go deeper**

- What's the problem behind the problem?
- Why is this important to you?
- Why are they telling me this?
- What are they not telling me?
- Why is this a problem for the person?
- **What would achieving xxx do for you?**
- **What's stopping you?**
- What's the upside to doing this? What's the downside?
- **But what happens if you don't?**

**Work on the beliefs**

- Identify the belief that is driving you
- Challenge its validity. Is it true? How do you know that?
- How is this belief connected to your other beliefs? Is it part of an old pattern?
- What are the feelings associated with this belief?
- Practice self-empathy. Try not to judge yourself for having this belief. Be compassionate towards yourself.
- Begin to reframe the belief
- **Explore how they see themselves as entrepreneurs — What is true about you as a startup founder?**
- What do you think top xxx have as true?
- What's limiting you?
- What are you saying to yourself to sabotage your plan?
- What would happen if that belief were to go away?
- **What is the smallest step you can take in this journey?**
- What is a small reward you can give yourself for this?
- What are you normalizing that shouldn't be?
- **What is the hardest question you're not asking yourself?**

**Take them into the future/hypothetical**

- Have them describe what success would look like 3, 6, 9 months from now
- Would the next 6 months look different? If not, why?
- What does freedom FROM look like?
- What does freedom TO look like?
- What does it look like 3 months from now? What would the ideal look like?
- Write a letter to your future self. What would you say?
- What if you had xxx years to work on this?
- **If you could ask your mentor/expert, what would they say?**
- **If you had unlimited resources, what would you do?**

**Closing: Build a growth pathway**

*These questions can help summarize the conversation and ensure that all important points have been covered. They can also help identify any areas that need further discussion or clarification. By asking these questions, you can help ensure that the call or session ends on a positive note and that everyone is clear on what needs to be done next.*

- Before we close off, what is the one insight you got out of this call?
- **Right now, what do you think is the best decision for you?**
- **What do we need to do next?**
- Let's talk about what you're learning so far...
- What's the one thing you want to say before we end this session?
- What's clearer to you now?
- Is there anything else you need before we wrap?
- How can I support you?
- What is the question you want me to ask you at this juncture?
- Would this be a good point to end this conversation?

**4. Cultivates Trust and Safety:** Coach shows genuine concern, support and respect for the client and is attuned to client's beliefs, perceptions, learning style, and personal being at a basic level.

- I can appreciate that this is important for you
- Let's talk a bit about why this matters to you
- I can see that this is upsetting for you

**5. Maintains Presence:** Coach demonstrates curiosity about the client and the client's agenda and is responsive to the information the client offers throughout the session.

- Let me see if I'm understanding you right...
- You're saying that...
- Would it be helpful to explore...

**6. Listens Actively:** Coach listens to what the client communicates in relation to the client's agenda, responds to what the client offers to ensure clarity of understanding, and integrates what the client has communicated to support the client in achieving their agenda.

- Let me see if I'm understanding you right...
- You're saying that...

**7. Evokes Awareness:** Coach uses inquiry, exploration, silence and other techniques to support the client in achieving new or deeper learning and awareness.

- What's behind this narrative you have?

**8. Facilitates Client Growth:** Coach supports the client in exploring how to apply the client's learning and awareness to post-session actions that are related to the client's stated agenda and have the potential to move the client forward in their thinking, learning, or growth.

- Based on what you've just learned about yourself, what will you need to move into that?
- What have you learned today? What would you like to work on in the next session?
