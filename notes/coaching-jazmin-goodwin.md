# Coaching - Jazmin Goodwin

---

**February 7, 2025**

**What Jazmin needed**
- Insights on transitioning from full-time employment to entrepreneurship
- Guidance on navigating challenges as a media entrepreneur
- Clarity on her business strategy and timeline
- Validation of her approach and business model

**Key insights she gained**

Freelancing challenges:
- Can be energy-draining and financially unstable
- Takes significant time for pitching and chasing payments
- May detract from building her own business

Revenue realities:
- Subscription and newsletter revenue takes 2–3 years to build
- Need for faster revenue generation strategies
- Importance of having multiple revenue streams early on

Value creation:
- Need to move beyond just content creation
- Importance of identifying specific pain points
- Focus on solutions people will pay for

Alternative strategies:
- Consider merchandise and events for immediate revenue
- Look into information products and workshops
- Leverage existing communities rather than building from scratch
- Use AI for market research and content curation

**What I could have done better as coach**

Questioning approach:
- Instead of explaining freelancing challenges: "What has your experience been with freelancing in the past? How did it impact your energy and focus?"
- Rather than suggesting revenue streams: "When you imagine your ideal revenue mix, what does that look like? What types of offerings excite you most?"
- Instead of giving advice about content: "What makes your audience choose to pay for certain services or products? What problems keep them up at night?"

Emotional exploration:
- When she mentioned fear of going broke: "What does financial security mean to you? What would help you feel more secure during this transition?"
- To understand her motivations deeper: "What excites you most about this venture? What scares you the most?"

Goal setting:
- To clarify success metrics: "How will you know if this venture is successful? What does success look like at 3 months? 6 months? A year?"
- To understand priorities: "If you could only focus on one aspect of the business in the first month, what would it be and why?"

Action planning:
- For immediate next steps: "What's the smallest action you could take tomorrow to move this forward?"
- To establish priorities: "Of all the ideas we've discussed, which one energizes you the most?"
- To create accountability: "What support do you need to make these actions happen? How can you hold yourself accountable?"
