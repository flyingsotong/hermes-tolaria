# Show up as a “transition architect,”not an AI trainer

Your pitch is not “I teach AI tools.” It is:
**“I help newsrooms rebuild their operating system for the judgment era — with a safe sandbox, clear specs, and proof of impact.”**

That framing does three things at once:

* It legitimizes fear as a *systems problem*(not a mindset problem)
* It moves the conversation from “AI ethics”to “workflow and accountability”
* It positions you as an operator who ships,not a commentator

⠀A clean pitch you can say in 20seconds


**“AI makes production cheap. That means journalism’s value shifts to judgment: choosing the right mission, specifying what ‘good’ lookslike, and proving impact. I run short, practicalsandboxes with teams to convert fear into aworking operating model — one workflow at atime, with guardrails and measurement.”**
## The fear → sandbox move (your corepromise)
Newsrooms are afraid because they arepicturing AI as:

* Replacement
* Cheating
* Unaccountable automation

⠀Your move is to reframe AI as:

* **A controllable workflow component**
* **Only as good as the specification**
* **Auditable when you design it that way**

⠀So you sell the *container*, not the tool:

* **Time-boxed**
* **Scoped**
* **Measured**
* **Reversible**

⠀Line: **“We are not rolling out AI. We are runningcontrolled experiments with accountability.”**
## The “sandbox offer” (concrete, non-scary)
Call it something like:
**The 2-week newsroom sandbox: from fear to a spec**
What you do:
1) Pick **one routine workflow** that is alreadypainful (something low-drama)
2) Write a **testable spec** for success (accuracy,time saved, user impact)
3) Build **context infrastructure** (inputs,definitions, exclusions, checklists)
4) Run the workflow with AI + humans
5) Produce a **before/after**: what changed, whatfailed, what to standardize
What they get:

* A working workflow
* A reusable template (spec + checklist +evaluation)
* A shared language that reduces anxietybecause it makes the work legible

⠀The credibility wedge that is uniquelyyou
You have range across:

* Editorial judgment and newsroom ops
* Product thinking
* Coaching (how people actually changeunder uncertainty)
* Proof you ship solo systems, not just slidedecks

⠀So you can say, plainly:
**“I’m not asking you to trust AI. I’m asking you totrust a process that makes AI inspectable.”**
