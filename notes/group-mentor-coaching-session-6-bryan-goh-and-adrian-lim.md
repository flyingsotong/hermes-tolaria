- 11:00am met with Bryan Goh, Adrian Lim for Group Mentor Coaching Session 6

 - Based on the transcript, here are some key lessons learned from the coaching discussion:

 1. The importance of setting clear expectations and framing for coaching sessions, especially when coaching friends or acquaintances. Casual terms like "catch up" can undermine the professional nature of the interaction.

 2. The value of "spot contracting" - asking for permission to shift the conversation when new topics or directions emerge during a session.

 3. The distinction between focusing on what's "developing" (moving towards a goal) versus what's "unfolding" (emerging in the present moment) during coaching conversations.

 4.**The analogy of coaches as "midwives" helping to birth new insights or realizations in clients.**

 5. The challenge of balancing professionalism and charging for services with the desire to help friends/colleagues, especially when trying to accumulate coaching hours.

 6. The importance of listening deeply and noticing subtle cues or "inflection points" in the client's responses.

 7. Recognizing that every business problem can be a coaching opportunity, as it often stems from underlying beliefs or perspectives.

 8. The value of being mindful of the broader impact of coaching, including effects on society and future generations.

 9. The need to build confidence as new coaches, similar to newly graduated medical professionals who must trust their training.

 10. The benefit of ongoing supervision and reflection to continue developing coaching skills.

 - Overall, the discussion emphasized the importance of intentionality in framing coaching relationships, deep listening, and balancing professionalism with empathy in coaching practice.
