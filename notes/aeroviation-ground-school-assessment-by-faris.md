# Aeroviation Ground school assessment by Faris

**Feb 14 2025**

Based on the assessment transcript, there were several areas where you faced challenges in explaining concepts clearly. Here are the main ones:

1. Stall Explanation - You understood that a stall occurs when lift is lost, but had difficulty articulating the process of airflow separation and how it leads to a stall. Faris helped guide you to explain how at a critical angle of attack, the airflow separates from the wing's surface, disrupting the smooth laminar flow needed for lift.

2. Lift Equation - While you knew components like surface area and pressure, you had trouble recalling and explaining the complete lift equation. Specifically:

- You missed the coefficient of lift (CL) initially

- Had difficulty explaining how the factors interact in the equation

- Needed prompting to connect angle of attack to the coefficient of lift

3. Left Turning Tendencies - You struggled to recall and explain the four factors that cause left-turning tendencies in single-engine aircraft. While you eventually understood concepts like torque effect and gyroscopic precession after Faris's explanations, you weren't able to list them independently.

4. Barotrauma - When asked about barotrauma, you initially couldn't explain it, though you later demonstrated understanding once Faris mentioned trapped gases.

5. Purpose of Oil - You could only recall two of the four purposes of oil in an engine (lubrication and cooling), missing its roles in sealing and dealing with contaminants.

The pattern seems to be that while you often had the basic understanding of concepts, you found it challenging to:

- Articulate technical explanations without prompting

- Recall specific numerical details (like equations)

- Connect theoretical knowledge to practical applications without guidance

Faris noted that this is normal for a student pilot, and praised your responsible attitude toward learning and safety. He suggested that hands-on simulator training would help reinforce these theoretical concepts, as you seem to learn better through practical application rather than pure theory.
