# Coaching - Arundhati Saha

**February 14, 2025**

What Arun was looking for:

1. Clarity about her next career move after spending over two decades in communications and PR
2. Permission and validation to make a change, as she felt stuck between wanting something different while being afraid of losing relevance and financial stability
3. Understanding of whether she should pursue one of three paths: an in-house role at a tech company, an agency that allows more innovative work, or starting her own consultancy

**Key insights she left with:**

1. Recognition that she already has everything she needs to make a change — supportive spouse, financial stability, housing security, and transferable skills.
2. Realization that she mainly needs permission from herself, not external validation.
3. Understanding that framing it as a one-year experiment makes the change more manageable and less daunting.
4. Clarity that her children would support and be inspired by her taking this risk.
5. Recognition that holding onto traditional career definitions may be limiting her growth.

**Areas where additional questions could have been valuable:**

1. Deeper exploration of what success metrics would look like for her consultancy — beyond just "working at my own pace."
2. More specific discussion about her home decor business idea and what initial steps she could take while still employed.
3. Investigation into what specific skills or knowledge she feels she needs to acquire to feel confident in making the transition.
4. Exploration of her relationship with money and financial security beyond just acknowledging it as a concern.
5. Discussion of specific next steps or commitments she could make within the next 30 days.

**What worked:**

1. Maintained presence — stayed deeply focused on Arun's narrative while picking up subtle cues; demonstrated comfort with silence and allowed space for reflection.
2. Listened actively — noticed patterns in how Arun talked about herself and her fears; picked up on important metaphors and language choices (eg. her use of words like "hustle" and "grind").
3. Evoked awareness — asked powerful questions that helped Arun see beyond her current thinking; used effective metaphors (like "holding labels loosely vs tightly"); challenged her assumptions about career definitions.
4. Cultivated trust and safety — created an environment where Arun felt safe to be vulnerable; shared relevant personal experiences (like your pilot training) thoughtfully.
5. Partnership — let Arun set the direction for the conversation; helped her distill complex thoughts into clearer insights.

A particularly strong moment was when you helped Arun reframe her year-long exploration from a risky venture to an experiment that she could learn from regardless of outcome. One especially effective approach was how you consistently turned questions back to Arun's own wisdom and knowing, helping her discover her own answers rather than providing them.
