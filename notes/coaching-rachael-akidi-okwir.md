**May 7, 2025**

Rachel gained clarity and a structured approach to her consultancy work, particularly in the realm of AI adoption in African media.

**Key takeaways for Rachel:**

- Confidence building as a core offering: Rachel reaffirmed that her primary role is not to force transformation but to give media leaders the confidence to explore and adopt AI.
- Understanding resistance to AI: she gained insights into why newsrooms might be hesitant to adopt AI, including ethical concerns, fear of the unknown, waiting for larger organizations to lead, lack of clear internal policies, and a narrow understanding of AI's potential beyond content generation.
- The need for local champions: Rachel recognized the importance of cultivating AI champions within African newsrooms rather than solely relying on examples from Western media.
- Framework for engagement: she developed a clearer framework for how to approach media houses, starting with understanding their specific challenges and offering tailored solutions.
- Leveraging her upcoming presentation: Rachel saw an opportunity to use her upcoming presentation at the African Media Convention to showcase her unique framework for ethical AI adoption and attract potential clients.

**Rachel's planned actions:**

- Develop an AI-powered newsletter focused on media industry updates, innovation, AI, and tech in Africa
- Create introductory workshops/masterclasses (90 minutes to 2 hours) as "AI 101" sessions
- Offer process audits for organizations wanting a deeper adoption strategy
- Develop internal AI guidelines and policies as a service
- Build a "Champions Program" — identify and train AI champions within newsrooms through an intensive cohort program
- Run pilot programs with smaller media houses to build case studies
- Focus on LinkedIn for visibility with decision-makers
- Present "The Rachel Framework" for ethical AI in journalism at the African Media Convention in Morocco
- Share presentation slides on LinkedIn after the convention

**Key coaching insights provided:**

- Reinforced her core strength: giving people the confidence to adopt AI, rather than forcing transformation
- Helped identify the real barriers to AI adoption — lack of ownership, narrow focus on content generation, resistance to change
- Guided her to concentrate efforts on those who are "AI curious" rather than trying to persuade staunch resisters
- Developed a product funnel: regular workshops → newsletter as lead magnet → process audits as paid service → policy development service
- Introduced the "Champions Program" concept: train AI champions within newsrooms to create local heroes and a cohort of advocates
- Framed her convention talk as "The Rachel Framework" with a clear call to action and pitch for pilot newsrooms
- Advised starting with smaller media houses for case studies, with a clear "theory of change"

**January 22, 2025**

**Background and current situation:**

- Rachel spent 21 years at BBC, rising from radio journalist to head of journalism, leading operations in East Africa and later Africa
- Left BBC 18 months ago through voluntary redundancy and started ReboConsult, a consultancy based in Nairobi
- Currently does independent consulting work for organizations like BBC Media Action and Aga Khan University
- Recently selected for AI journalism leadership cohort at Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism
- Looking to grow her consultancy in 2024 and establish more consistent income

**Key challenges and goals:**

- Wants to transform and support media organizations navigating industry changes, particularly around digital transformation and AI adoption
- Needs to develop business development and outreach strategies (currently relies mainly on referrals)
- Looking to establish herself as a thought leader in media transformation
- Transitioning from public media mindset to entrepreneurial/consulting mindset
- Wants to help organizations gain confidence in embracing change, particularly around AI adoption

**Key insights and advice from the coach:**

1. Differentiation strategy: focus on being a confidence builder for organizations facing change; develop a clear theory of change and point of view to stand out in a crowded consulting space.
2. Thought leadership development: create content around helping organizations confront fears about change; consider presenting at Africa Media Festival; build thought leadership through LinkedIn content and potentially podcasts.
3. Business development approaches: be prepared for long sales cycles with legacy media organizations; focus on building a strong brand and point of view to attract clients; consider how to package and price services effectively.

**Key insights Rachel gained:**

1. Reframing her value proposition: started thinking about herself as a "confidence builder" rather than just a transformation expert; realized her expertise isn't just about media/journalism, but about leadership and change management more broadly.
2. Content strategy direction: shifted from thinking about general media consulting to focusing on addressing fear and confidence in transformation; got concrete ideas for thought leadership content.
3. Business perspective: better understood the importance of having a distinct point of view in a crowded market; gained insight into the long-term nature of sales cycles with legacy media organizations.

**Areas for improvement:**

1. Deeper exploration: could have dug deeper into her specific experiences leading change at BBC — there seemed to be rich stories that could inform her consulting approach; might have explored more about what made her successful in previous transformation projects.
2. Practical next steps: could have helped her outline more concrete steps for developing her thought leadership platform; might have explored specific pricing or packaging strategies.

The coach noted Rachel's positive, forward-leaning attitude toward change and encouraged her to leverage this as part of her brand.
