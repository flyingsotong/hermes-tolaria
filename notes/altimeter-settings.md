**Atmospheric pressure decreases with altitude**, so if the altimeter setting is not adjusted, the altimeter will read higher than the actual altitude. This can be dangerous, especially when flying in mountainous terrain or near obstacles.

The altimeter setting in an airplane is used to adjust the altimeter reading to reflect the local atmospheric pressure. The altimeter setting is usually given in inches of mercury (in Hg) or millibars (mb).

The altimeter setting is important because it ensures that the altimeter reading is accurate.\
To adjust the altimeter setting, the pilot simply enters the current altimeter setting into the altimeter. The altimeter setting can be obtained from a flight service station, an air traffic controller, or an automated weather observation system (AWOS).

**Here is an analogy to help you understand the altimeter setting:**

Imagine that you are hiking in the mountains. You have a map that shows the elevation of different points along the trail. However, the map was made using a different baseline elevation than the one you are currently at. As a result, the elevations on the map are all inaccurate.

To correct the elevations on the map, you need to know the difference between the baseline elevation used to make the map and your current elevation. This difference is like the altimeter setting.

Once you know the altimeter setting, you can adjust the elevations on the map so that they are accurate. This is like adjusting the altimeter reading in an airplane.
