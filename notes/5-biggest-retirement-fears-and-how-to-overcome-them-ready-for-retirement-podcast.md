[Ready For Retirement - Pocket Casts](https://play.pocketcasts.com/podcasts/27de6bb0-2f89-0139-32f8-0acc26574db2)


James Canole discusses the five major fears people have about retirement and how to address them:

1. Fear of Outliving Savings

- Can be addressed through optimal Social Security timing strategy

- Requires proper savings planning while working

- Recommends doing a "test run" of retirement spending

- Emphasizes importance of sustainable withdrawal strategies

2. Fear of Losing Purpose

- Work provides identity, routine, and connections

- Suggests identifying what aspects of work you value

- Recommends finding alternative sources of structure and meaning through:

 - Social clubs and activities

 - Volunteering

 - Creating new routines

 - Setting personal projects

3. Fear of Inadequate Healthcare Coverage

- Emphasizes importance of understanding Medicare options (Parts A, B, D, and Medigap)

- Recommends consulting with healthcare coverage experts

- Suggests utilizing Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)

- Explains how medical expense tax deductions can help (expenses over 7.5% of adjusted gross income)

4. Fear of Loneliness

- About one-third of retirees report feeling lonely

- Recommends building and maintaining social connections before retirement

- Suggests careful consideration of retirement location

- Emphasizes being intentional about creating new social circles

5. Fear of Long-Term Care Costs

- Common concern about becoming a burden to family

- Suggests considering long-term care insurance, though notes it can be expensive

- Recommends evaluating whether existing assets could cover care costs

- Emphasizes importance of having a specific plan for long-term care needs
