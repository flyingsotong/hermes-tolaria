# Coaching - Sharon Tshipa (Nano)


**Tuesday, May 6, 2025**

**Product idea:**

Initially, Sharon envisioned creating a website that would highlight the role of women in development. This platform would use AI tools to aggregate, rewrite, and summarize news about women, focusing on their contributions and inspirational stories. The goal was to address the underrepresentation of women in news and media. She considered monetizing it through advertising, primarily to cover operational costs rather than generating profit.

**Key learnings from the conversation:**

- **Rethinking the "content-first" approach:** The conversation challenged Sharon to think beyond content as the primary offering. It was highlighted that starting with content can be difficult to monetize and compete with the vast amount of existing online content.
- **Focusing on the core mission and audience:** Sharon realized the importance of identifying who the product is for and what value it offers them. Understanding the audience's needs is crucial for monetization.
- **Exploring alternative avenues:** Instead of solely focusing on a content website, ideas like hosting events (e.g., special dinners, conferences), or creating awards to recognize women's achievements and those who cover them were discussed. These alternatives could be more monetizable through sponsorships and partnerships.
- **The power of AI tools for rapid prototyping:** Sharon learned about AI website builders (like TypeDream and Trickle) and other AI tools (like Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT) that can help create a website, generate content, and even develop business plans quickly and often at no initial cost. This allows for rapid experimentation and launching.
- **The "product bit" is key:** She understood that defining the core product/service ("the product bit") and its value proposition should come before the technical aspects of building a website.

**Moving forward:**

- **Re-evaluate the core offering:** Sharon plans to step back and rethink her core product, moving away from a purely content-focused idea. She will consider alternatives like events or awards that align with her mission of highlighting women's contributions.
- **Define the "product" first, then build:** The immediate next step is to solidify the product idea and strategy. She recognizes that this "product bit" will require more work but is crucial for success.
- **Leverage AI tools for implementation:** Once the product vision is clear, she intends to use the AI tools discussed to quickly build a website and potentially other necessary assets.
- **Iterative approach:** Sharon sees that establishing the core product/service will make the subsequent content creation and website setup easier. She plans to work on defining what she wants to offer first and then proceed to the technical implementation.
