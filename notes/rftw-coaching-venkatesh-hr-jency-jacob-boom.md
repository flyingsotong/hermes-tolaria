# RFTW coaching Venkatesh HR + Jency Jacob (Boom)

Venkatesh and Jency from Boom sought guidance on developing new, sustainable revenue streams. They feel they are at a**crossroads**, anticipating a potential disruption in their primary revenue from Meta's fact-checking program. Their main goal was to explore how to become more self-dependent and move beyond a reliance on platform grants, having found previous B2C efforts like memberships and paywalls to be very difficult. They came to the call looking for a path toward financial stability.
**What They Learned**
The conversation with you helped them crystallize several key ideas and provided a new strategic direction:
-**Embrace B2B:** They received validation that focusing on their B2B strengths is a smarter path than struggling with B2C. You pointed out that B2B is an easier game because the customers are identifiable and they aren't chasing massive, expensive-to-acquire audiences.
-**Move Beyond "Media":** A major takeaway was the need to stop thinking of themselves solely as a media or content company. You encouraged them to reframe their identity around their core skill: the**methodology and process of fact-checking**.
-**Repackage Skills for New Markets:** They learned to apply their fact-checking expertise to new industries. You specifically highlighted how their skills are valuable to**banks, financial services, and telcos** who are fighting scams and need credible partners to educate the public.
-**Content is Dead, Data is the Future:** They found your advice—and the validation from their competitor, DataLeads—that "content is dead" to be clarifying. The conversation shifted their focus from creating articles to building a valuable, proprietary**data set**, such as a comprehensive scam database.
-**A Tangible Product Idea:** The call gave them a concrete product concept: a**scam verification tool** (e.g., an app or chatbot) powered by a custom-trained LLM. This tool could provide a risk score for suspicious messages, a service they could sell or get sponsored by corporations.
-**A New Outreach Strategy:** They learned a new way to approach potential corporate clients. Instead of asking for sponsorship for content, they can now propose a partnership to solve a mutual problem (scams), offer to build a data tool, or organize an**industry event** to build relationships with key decision-makers.
**Potential Next Steps for the Next Call**
For your next session, you can support them by helping them operationalize these new ideas. Here are potential outcomes to work toward:
1.**Develop the Scam Database Prototype:** They mentioned they had already started collecting scam messages. You can help them structure this data.
-**Support needed:** Guide them on**categorizing the data**, defining the necessary fields, and creating a simple dashboard or prototype to show potential clients.
1.**Flesh Out the B2B Service Offering:** Help them define exactly what they would sell to a bank or a telco.
-**Support needed:** Brainstorm a**"product sheet"** that outlines the service (e.g., LLM verification tool, employee training, public education content creation, event partnership). This will clarify their pitch.
1.**Map Out an Outreach Plan:** Jency and Govind have institutional access but are hesitant. You can help them build a confident, strategic approach.
-**Support needed:** Role-play the initial conversation with a bank executive. Help them script an email or call that frames the discussion around solving the bank's problem, not asking for a handout. You could also help them outline a small, high-impact**offline event** to attract the right people.
