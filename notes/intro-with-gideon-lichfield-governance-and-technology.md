- 9:00am met with Gideon Lichfield

This was an introductory call between Gideon and Alan, discussing Gideon's ideas for a project and Alan's experience running a media-focused community and events business called Splice. Key points from their conversation:

1\. Gideon is interested in exploring topics related to governance in the 21st century, including technology, infrastructure, copyright law, free speech, privacy, and disinformation.

2\. Gideon's initial plan is to create content around these topics, with the possibility of convening people and organizing events in the future.

3\. Alan shared his experience with Splice, which has evolved over 10 years from a content-focused business to one centered on community building and hospitality.

4\. Splice focuses on serving media startups and creators, offering various formats for engagement including Slack and Telegram groups, newsletters, and an annual in-person event.

5\. Alan emphasized the importance of identifying specific personas and needs within the community, and creating spaces where people feel seen, heard, and connected.

6\. Splice's business model includes community building, consulting work, training, and events.

7\. Alan advised Gideon to focus deeply on one specific category or track within his broad area of interest, as this could help define the audience and potential impact.

8\. Alan shared that Splice has about 7,000 people in its wider community, but operates on a principle of cultivating 100 "true fans" who are closely engaged with their work.

The call provided Gideon with insights and advice from Alan's experience in building a community-focused business in the media space, which could inform Gideon's approach to his own project.
