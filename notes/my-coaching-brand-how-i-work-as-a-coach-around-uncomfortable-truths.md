# My coaching brand — how I work as a coach around uncomfortable truths

Here’s how you can build a simple, compelling description of what you do when you help clients figure out their uncomfortable truths.


**1. The Core Philosophy (Your "Why")**

This is the thought leadership piece you can use to explain the concept. It sets the stage for why this process is so important.

> "In my experience coaching mid-career professionals, I've found that the state of being 'stuck' is rarely due to a lack of talent or effort. It’s almost always caused by an**'uncomfortable truth'**—a reality about their industry, their role, or themselves that they know deep down is true, but have been avoiding.
>
> My core belief is that you cannot build a new future on a foundation of denial. Real progress doesn't come from layering on new tactics; it comes from bravely confronting that one truth. I specialize in [What I've learned as a coach.md](My%20coaching%20brand%20%E2%80%94%20how%20I%20work%20as%20a%20coach%20around%20uncomfortable%20truths/What%20I've%20learned%20as%20a%20coach.md) to find the 'handbrake' a client doesn't realize is on. Once we name it and accept it, the path forward, while not always easy, becomes incredibly clear."

**2. The Simple Pitch (Your "What")**

These are short, memorable descriptions you can use in conversation, in a bio, or as a headline.

**Option A (Problem-Focused):**

> "You know how talented professionals can feel stuck, putting in endless effort without seeing real progress? I help them identify the one 'uncomfortable truth' that's holding them back, so they can finally move forward with clarity and purpose."

**Option B (Direct & Metaphorical):**

> "I specialize in helping leaders and creators find the handbrake they don't realize is on. We work together to identify the single uncomfortable truth that is keeping their career from evolving. Once we release it, they can accelerate with intention."

**Option C (The Specialized Bridge):**

> "I serve as a specialized bridge for journalists and media leaders in crisis. I help you [Coaching Reframing journalism.md](My%20coaching%20brand%20%E2%80%94%20how%20I%20work%20as%20a%20coach%20around%20uncomfortable%20truths/Coaching%20Reframing%20journalism.md) by confronting the uncomfortable reality of a shifting industry, turning that source of friction into the catalyst for your next chapter."

**3. The Process Description (Your "How")**

This is for your website, proposals, or a detailed LinkedIn post. It breaks down your system, showing that it's a structured and safe process, which builds trust and credibility.

>**Navigating Your Uncomfortable Truth: My 3-Step Process**
>
> Helping you confront a core truth requires a process built on trust, safety, and directness. Here’s how I guide my clients through it:
>
>**Step 1: The Diagnostic - Naming the Truth** Through focused, empathetic questioning, we create a safe container to explore the friction you're feeling. I use my [Clifton Strengths Personality Report.md](My%20coaching%20brand%20%E2%80%94%20how%20I%20work%20as%20a%20coach%20around%20uncomfortable%20truths/Clifton%20Strengths%20Personality%20Report.md) to listen for the patterns, the hesitations, and the things left unsaid to help you articulate the truth that has been lingering in the back of your mind. The goal is simple: to give it a name.
>
>**Step 2: The Analysis - Understanding the Resistance** An uncomfortable truth is "uncomfortable" for a reason. It's often protected by fear, old habits, or a story you've been telling yourself for years. We gently unpack that resistance using a**"Compassionate Challenge"** style—where I share my own vulnerabilities to make it safe for you to face yours. Understanding the "why" dissolves its power.
>
>**Step 3: The Crossroads - Choosing Your Path** With the truth named and its resistance understood, you arrive at a powerful moment of choice. We map out the two paths ahead: the compounding cost of continuing to ignore the truth, and the incredible opportunities that open up when you decide to accept it and act. This isn't about me telling you what to do; it's about me showing you the clear consequences of your decision, empowering you to build your**"launching muscle"** and move forward with conviction.
