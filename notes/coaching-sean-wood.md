# Coaching - Sean Wood

**Analysis of coaching call with Sean Wood**

---

**1. Sean's intent**

Sean's primary intent was to explore and resolve an internal conflict stemming from:

- Desire for work-life balance — wanting to work less, dedicate more time to his young family and personal interests, after 15 years of intense focus at Positive News
- Ambition for the organization — feeling the potential and expectation (from the board, audience, and himself) to grow Positive News significantly
- Reconciling opposing needs — struggling with how to achieve greater success for the organization while simultaneously reducing his workload and overwhelm. He explicitly asked: "How do I have it all? How do I work less and achieve more?"
- Seeking external perspective — given his long tenure within a single organization, he wanted insights from others in the field

---

**2. Sean's awareness**

Through the conversation, Sean gained several key insights:

- Feasibility of transition — he moved from seeing a leadership transition (empowering his COO, Pauline) as daunting to recognizing it as a tangible, step-by-step possibility. He realized he had put up a mental boundary instead of thinking through logical steps.
- Importance of open communication — he acknowledged the value of initiating an open conversation with Pauline about his thoughts on the future, despite initial uncertainty
- Validation of personal feelings — hearing Alan's perspective on career transitions and letting go of titles helped validate his own feelings about wanting change
- Shifting focus — he resonated with the idea of defining value by "what we can do for people going forward" rather than past achievements or titles
- Actionable first step — the conversation crystallized a clear next step: initiating the conversation with Pauline about a potential transition plan

---

**3. Coaching improvement areas**

**Maintains presence / listens actively (ICF 5 & 6)**

While Alan clearly listened and reflected Sean's core conflict, there were moments where personal experiences and perspectives were offered quite readily. Sharing can build rapport, but it can also shift focus away from the client's exploration. The lengthy sharing towards the end, while validating for Sean, took time that could have been used for deeper exploration of his specific next steps.

**Evokes awareness (ICF 7)**

When Sean presented his core dilemma ("How do I work less and achieve more?"), the immediate pivot was toward the practical solution (transition plan with Pauline) rather than exploring underlying assumptions more deeply first. Questions that might have surfaced deeper awareness before moving to solutions:

- "What does 'success' look like for you personally, separate from the organization?"
- "What fears arise when you think about not making Positive News massive?"
- "What assumptions are you holding about what's required to grow the business?"

**Facilitates client growth (ICF 8)**

The session concluded with a clear action step (talk to Pauline). To further solidify commitment, these questions could have helped:

- "What specific support would help you prepare for that conversation with Pauline?"
- "What might a first, small step look like in broaching this topic?"
- "By when might you aim to have this initial conversation?"
- "How will you know you've successfully opened this dialogue in the way you intend?"
