# What I'd Learn Instead of Automation in 2026 (Nick Saraev)

<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YIl-awY250k>
This video argues that the AI revolution is making surface-level technical skills obsolete. To build a successful business now, you need to shift your focus from _how_ to build automations to _what_ problems need solving and _how_ to communicate those problems to AI.
Here is a summary of the key takeaways and how you can apply them to your business strategy.
**1. The Big Shift: From Technical Skills to Business Acumen**
The core argument is that technical skills, like mastering specific automation tools (e.g., make.com, Zapier) or knowing API endpoints, are rapidly losing value. Just as the sewing machine made complex hand-stitching techniques less valuable, AI is beginning to automate the technical work of building systems.
-**The Problem:** AI will soon be able to build entire automation systems based on plain English descriptions. The "doing" of the technical work is becoming a commodity.
-**The Opportunity:** The real value is moving up to a higher level of abstraction. Your competitive advantage will not be your ability to use the tools, but your ability to understand a business's problems and goals.
**_How to think about it:_** Stop focusing on memorizing the features of every new AI tool. Instead,**focus on learning the fundamentals of how businesses operate and where their biggest pain points are.**The most valuable people will be those who can identify problems worth solving, not just those who can implement the solutions.
**2. The New High-Leverage Skill: Communicating with AI**
The most critical skill in the near future will be your ability to translate business needs into clear, logical instructions for an AI. The speaker predicts that within**12-24 months, natural language will be the primary way we build complex business systems like CRMs, sales pipelines, and inventory management.**
To do this effectively, the video introduces the**CLEAR framework** for prompting AI:
-**C - Clarity:** Be extremely precise. Instead of "build me a lead gen system," specify the exact outcomes, target audience, and constraints.
 - _Example:_ "Create a lead qualification process for companies with 50+ employees in the manufacturing sector who have shown interest in automation in the last 90 days."
-**L - Logic:** Break down the problem into sequential steps and clear decision points that the AI can follow.
-**E - Examples:** Provide specific scenarios, edge cases, and desired outcomes.
 - _Example:_ "If a lead scores over 80, route it to a senior sales rep. If it's below 50, put it in a nurture sequence."
-**A - Adaptation:** Treat prompting as a conversation. Refine and improve the AI's output through iterative feedback. Don't expect a perfect result on the first try.
-**R - Results:** Define how you will measure success and validate that the final output solves the business problem and provides a return on investment (ROI).
**_How to think about it:_ Your main job is becoming that of a "systems architect" who uses AI as the builder.**Your value is in the quality of your blueprint (the prompt). Start practicing the CLEAR framework to move beyond simple prompts and toward building robust, reliable systems.
**3. The Ultimate Skill: Systems Thinking**
Beyond just communicating with AI, the most valuable and timeless skill is**systems thinking**. This means understanding the fundamental "shape" of a business, which is transferable across any industry or technology.
Every service business, whether it's a marketing agency or an AI consultancy, follows a similar pattern:
Marketing Sales Onboarding Delivery Retention/Reactivation
If you understand the principles behind each of these stages, you can build a successful business regardless of the specific service you're selling. The technology is just a tool you pour into this proven container.
**_How to think about it:_** Don't just learn "AI automation." Learn the shape of a successful service business. Master client acquisition, project management, and team structure. This foundational knowledge will allow you to adapt and thrive as technology continues to change. Your business isn't about the AI; it's about solving a problem for a customer. AI is simply the new, incredibly powerful tool you get to use to do it.
