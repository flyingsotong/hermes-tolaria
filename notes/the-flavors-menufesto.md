# The Flavors Menufesto

"What would you do for a country on the brink of the internet?"

What we've seen... and know to be true

- Modern media is user-focused, demand-driven, and interest-based

- Journalism is just one flavor of information — the information ecosystem is far larger than just news

- Diminished role of traditional information gatekeepers — eg. newsrooms

- Rise of digital-first media startups thanks to lower barriers to entry

- Proliferation of creators and influencers amplified by platforms and/or funded by marketers/PR

Our beliefs and approach

- A news/media/journalism-only lens to understanding the shifts in the information ecosystem is too limited and doesn't sufficiently address both threats and opportunities

- We are past peak content. AI, with its ability to create indefinite wall-to-wall content in all formats, and at scale, will flood the information space

- To surviveinfo producers need to se and to stand out, info publishers need to serve a need

- Support a user-first mindset: What problems/irritations need to be solved? For whom? How can I validate that? How can I make money from solving that?

- Don't fight the megatrends — instead, create opportunities to support media startups, creators, influencers

- The best way to support legacy media companies is to show the path forward led by digital information producers

- Create a program to co-opt creators and influencers so they can assimilate the most valuable principles of journalism, including fact-based reporting, accuracy, and fairness

We recommend

- Research: Who are the players in the information ecosystem in Timor — journalists, politicians, creators, influencers, Timor-Leste Press Council. What are their needs? How do we address those from an industry perspective?

- Workshops: Scenario planning — set expectations for 1, 2, 3 years. What are the high-probability, high-impact prospects? How do we plan for that? Where are the gaps? Start with baseline as described in the State of Media report

- Knowledge-sharing: Present these processes and lessons on stage at Splice Beta — benefits NGOs, funders, journalists
