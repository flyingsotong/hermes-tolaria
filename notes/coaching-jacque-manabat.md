**February 17, 2025**

**What Jacque wanted**

Jacque was seeking clarity about her career transition after leaving ABS-CBN, where she had spent 17 years. She was struggling with multiple opportunities but felt uncertain about which direction to take. She specifically wanted to understand how to build a sustainable independent news operation while dealing with industry feedback and validation. She was also grappling with questions about maintaining journalistic integrity while exploring new business models.

---

**Key awareness**

The most significant breakthrough came when Jacque realized that her attachment to traditional definitions of "journalism" was holding her back. Through the discussion, she recognized that:

1. She was seeking validation from industry veterans who may not understand the new media landscape
2. Her desire to conform to traditional journalism standards was limiting her ability to innovate and create sustainable business models
3. She had already begun creating valuable content but was undervaluing it because it didn't fit the traditional journalism mold
4. Her concerns about brand deals and monetization were rooted in outdated industry norms rather than actual ethical considerations

---

**Coaching improvement areas**

1. Fear and identity — while we touched on her fears about leaving traditional journalism, I could have explored more deeply how her identity as a journalist was affecting her decision-making
2. Practical next steps — we discussed conceptual shifts but could have developed more concrete actions. What specific steps could she take in the next week?
3. Support system — we discussed industry veterans' validation but didn't fully explore how she could build a new support network of creators and entrepreneurs
4. Business model development — we touched on monetization through brand deals but could have explored other revenue streams more comprehensively
5. Audience understanding — we could have dived deeper into understanding her current audience and how to better serve them without the constraints of traditional journalism

The conversation was productive in helping Jacque recognize her self-imposed limitations, but more actionable outcomes could have made the insights more immediately applicable.
