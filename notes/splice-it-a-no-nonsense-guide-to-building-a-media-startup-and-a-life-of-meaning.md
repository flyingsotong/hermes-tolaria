# Splice It A no nonsense guide to building a media startup -- and a life of meaning around it

**How to think about pricing**

We get this question a lot, and to be honest, there's no secret formula to it. Sorry.

You could start with your costs -- your time, software subscriptions, internet, coffee -- and slap on a 50% margin on top of that. That's how many small retailers do it.

But you could also ask a different question: If I could solve this problem for you, what are the benefits and outcomes that you'd get from that? For example, if you helped me avoid an expensive knee surgery costing $20,000, that becomes the top end of that pricing.

Simply put, the more valuable the outcome, the more you can charge for it.

But keep in mind that pricing is both inclusive and exclusive at the same time. Sure, you can drop your subscription fee to $5 a month so you capture a bigger audience, but that also keeps out a segment that assumes $5 a month doesn't create the $1,000 in value they're expecting. It works both ways.

Also, think about how your offering compares with others in the market. Tip: If you managed to find a niche that made you the only fish in a small pond, you're in a better position to name your price because no benchmark exists.

So think about the value you're creating for your user and price yourself accordingly. People will spend $50 to save them $1,000. But you'll have to help them see that. And that's what marketing does.

**How to market yourself**

If you're niche (there's that word again), this will be easier for you; you already have a good idea of who your reader is.

Splice It: A no nonsense guide to building a media startup -- and a life of meaning around it

Your problem is our opportunity. If you get nothing else out of this book, we hope you'll remember this golden rule with business: people will spend money to solve a problem. Understand that and you're halfway there.

Every business, big and small, is built around problems. Need your clothes washed? Need your kitchen sink fixed? Need help with your tax filing? Need help getting a bigger job?

These are all jobs to be done, and that's where you'll find entrepreneurs.

But what about media? When was the last time you heard someone say, "I need help with understanding the world?"

Even before digital, media was always paid for by someone else. Newspapers were funded by advertisers wanting to sell you their homes, used cars, or clothes. TV made its way into your living room because someone wanted you to buy their soap.

And over decades, we've assumed that people bought "news" because they wanted journalism. Newspapers didn't make money from journalism; it was simply the only place where you could find a room to rent, or a second-hand car.

As media practitioners, we've lost sight of understanding people's needs and what they needed to know -- who to pick in the next election, which schools to send the kids to, how to invest $100, or something as simple as do I take an umbrella today.

These are needs, or as we like to think of it, problems to be solved. And problems are opportunities for you to build a media company around.

The goal of this book is to help you figure out how to identify that problem and how to build a life and a business around solving that for someone.

Obsess about what people need. And if you get that right, they will not only pay you for it, and that will help you live a life defined only on your terms.
