**Lessons from organizing Beta**

1.**Onboarding**(begins the moment someone decides they want to register until arrival at event)

2. show me what I'll learn

3. who I'll meet

4. how I can help

5. how can I get updates

6.**Enrichment**(gathering and enriching data to create tremendous value to the participant)

7. who's coming

8. what do they want to get out of Beta

9. what can they share

10. what can they learn

11.**Engagement**(build organic and structured connections before, during, and after the event)

12. no one's a stranger

13. how can I share what I know

14. how can I learn from you

15.**Experience**(create a spectacular intellectual and emotional connection)

16. how do we delight you

17. how do we help you create memories from Beta

What people took away from Beta: "I'm not alone" "I should go try" "Don't leave your audience depressed and helpless"

Email confirmation upon registration

Can we register into Squarespace members?

Assign clinic rooms directly into the booking itself

It's about the CRM. The attendee is the atom. Get that right and you can scale the program around them. Get that wrong and you'll end up with a hundred moving parts and constant emailing.

Mail merge when possible. But to do that quickly and accurately, you need to get the CRM right. See the first point.

Always ask for first and last names. It's hard to mail merge when you keep having to separate full names.

Find a way to automate a form so it fills out a Google Doc or Sheet for people to request an official letter of invitation.

Set aside space for people to self-organize sessions -- help them get the word out

Or just set aside an unconference as a third day

Set aside space for people to have private conversations

Pick an identifiable "Meeting Point" spot

Session: Mental health for creators -- dealing with abuse and trolls

Ask better survey questions around the use of private data

Buy event insurance
