**Manifesto: The Beauty of Small**

**On Micro, Niche, and Nano Companies**

---

**We believe in the power of small.**

Not small as in insignificant. Small as in specific. Small as in intentional. Small as in human-scaled and sustainable.

**The Problem with "Scale"**

For too long, we've been told that success means scaling. More content. More employees. More offices. More, more, more. The advice comes relentlessly: "You need to scale!" — as if the only path forward is to become a factory, churning out widgets (or hot dogs, as the case may be) at increasing velocity.

But what if scale is the wrong metric entirely?

**What if the goal isn't to serve millions, but to serve *one* — perfectly — and then do it again, and again?**

**The Internet of One**

Modern media is user-centered, demand-driven, and interest-based. This isn't just a business model — it's a philosophy of service. 

Just as Pringles makes 50 flavors and Colgate makes 34 varieties, not because people are small, but because *people are specific*. Everyone has different needs. Some want salt in their toothpaste. Some need sensitive teeth protection. Some crave clove flavor.

**Niche is not about being small. It's about being very, very specific.**

A language spoken by a million people in a corner of India isn't niche — it's an audience of a million who've been underserved by "mainstream" solutions. Your micro-company can serve them better than any mass-market giant ever could.

**The Two-Person Revolution**

There is profound beauty in a company that needs only to pay two salaries. A company that can take December off. A company where the founders wear Uniqlo green and hand out name tags at their own festival because they *want* to, not because they can't afford staff.

This isn't a stepping stone to something bigger.**This *is* the thing.**

When you ask yourself "how much do I need to make per month?" and you and your co-founder coincidentally have the same number — that's not a limitation. That's liberation. That's a company shaped to fit your life, not a life bent to serve your company's growth ambitions.

**What We're Building**

We're building:
- Communities, not audiences
- Solutions, not content factories  
- Relationships, not reach
- Sustainability, not scale
- Intention, not inevitability

We're asking harder questions:
- Who *specifically* needs this?
- Why do *they* need it?
- What can *they* do with it?
- How will *they* get it?

We're obsessed with serving the audience of one. And then the next one. And the next.

**The Future Is Nano**

While Media 4.0 brings wall-to-wall AI-generated content, the opportunity isn't in making *more*. It's in making something that matters to *someone*. 

The nano company — lean, agile, deeply human — can:
- Pivot in an afternoon
- Test assumptions in a week  
- Build genuine community connections
- Offer what AI cannot: personal knowledge, lived experience, human certification, actual relationships

**"Made by machine, guaranteed by human"** — this will be your competitive advantage.

**Permission to Stay Small**

You don't need:
- A 10-person team
- An office
- Investors demanding 10x returns
- To "look official" before you start
- To apologize for not wanting to be huge

You need:
- A clear problem you're solving
- People who need it solved
- A way to deliver value
- The courage to start

**Just start.** Sell first. Paperwork later. Prove the need exists before you rent the office or print the business cards.

**In Defense of the Human Scale**

There's a reason Splice Beta feels different from traditional media conferences. It's tuktuk chic, not black-tie formality. It's 55% female presenters, not rows of white men in suits. It's Burmese journalists running cafes out of former garment factories. It's a festival, not a conference.

**We believe that a healthy viable media ecosystem requires a broader definition of media.**

We believe communities of creators, YouTubers, Substackers, podcasters — all are part of the ecosystem. The walls between "real journalism" and "mere content" serve no one but gatekeepers.

We believe in putting users at the center, not content.  
We believe in demand-driven work, not supply-push publishing.  
We believe in interest-based service, not mass-market mediocrity.

**The Work Ahead**

Modern entrepreneurship is about obsessing over what your user needs solved. It's about asking, listening, and acting — over and over again.

Ask: What business do you think I'm in?  
Listen: What value do I create for you?  
Act: Build that. Not what you *think* they need. What they actually need.

**Fail fast. Fail cheap.** Better to spend $100 testing an assumption than $100,000 building something nobody wants.

**A Final Word**

When people gather under fairy lights at a cultural center in Chiang Mai, when exile journalists build co-working spaces in former dormitories, when two founders in Uniqlo green hand out name tags because they can't let go of the welcome table even with volunteers around — that's not lack of scale.

**That's presence. That's intention. That's love made visible.**

The future belongs not to those who grow biggest, but to those who serve best. To those who understand that a healthy ecosystem needs diversity — different voices, different approaches, different scales of operation.

There is beauty in small, micro, niche, nano companies.  
There is power in specificity.  
There is freedom in sustainability.  
There is meaning in human-scaled work.

**We are the tuktuk revolution.**  
Pink, loud, a little jerry-rigged.  
But we're taking people where they need to go.

*This is the way.*
