A stall is the reduction in lift that occurs **when the critical angle of attack is exceeded**, causing the airflow to separate from the wing's upper surface. This separation results in a significant decrease in lift and an increase in drag.

Symptoms: Mushy controls, reduced yoke control, reduced aileron control, stall horn, buffeting.

**Power-on stall:** Occurs when the aircraft's angle of attack exceeds the critical angle while the engine is producing power. This often happens during maneuvers like attempting to climb too steeply or during a rapid acceleration.

P**ower-off stall:** Occurs when the angle of attack exceeds the critical angle with the engine at or near idle power. This typically happens during approaches to landing or when practicing glide approaches. The key difference is the engine power setting and the situations in which they occur.
