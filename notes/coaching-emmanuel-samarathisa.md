# Coaching - Emmanuel Samarathisa

---

**January 14, 2025**

Emmanuel is a journalist of 12+ years who recently transitioned to running his own paid newsletter full-time.

**Current status**
- Has 330 paid subscribers (started with 300)
- Targeting 1,000 subscribers for full sustainability, though 600 would be workable
- Pricing: $60/year founding members, $50/year regular, $5/month
- Has taken steps to reduce costs (moved to mother-in-law's house)

**Main concerns**
- Financial stability as sole breadwinner, particularly regarding his daughter's education and swimming activities
- Fear of failing both practically and personally
- Legal risks (police reports, lawsuits) without corporate protection
- Product development and value proposition
- Loneliness in the entrepreneurial journey

**Key advice given**
- Clear definition of success and failure metrics
- Focus on understanding current subscribers rather than rushing to new products
- Emphasis on community building with core supporters
- Caution against premature diversification (e.g., second newsletter, advertising)
- Importance of setting clear financial boundaries

**Areas for potential improvement in the coaching**
1. Structure — the conversation could have benefited from more structured goal-setting. Could have helped Emmanuel create a more detailed action plan with specific milestones.
2. Time management — the deep focus on fears and concerns was valuable, but more time could have been spent on practical next steps and specific growth strategies.
3. Metrics deep dive — given Emmanuel's interest in metrics (daily P&L tracking), could have explored more specific KPIs to track beyond just subscriber numbers.
4. Support network — while addressing the loneliness of entrepreneurship, could have suggested specific ways for him to build a support network of other newsletter entrepreneurs or join relevant communities.
5. Follow-up framework — the session could have ended with a clearer framework for follow-up or specific homework to work on before the next conversation.

Your coaching showed strong empathy and provided valuable perspective, particularly in helping Emmanuel focus on what matters (understanding his current subscribers) rather than getting distracted by additional products or revenue streams. The discussion about defining success and failure on his own terms was especially powerful.

**Questions to try next time**
- "When you think about 'failure,' what specific scenario are you most worried about? Let's explore what that might actually look like and what options you'd have."
- "What would success look like for you in 6 months? What's the smallest version of success that would still feel meaningful?"
- "How is the pressure of being a breadwinner affecting your creative decisions? What would you do differently if that pressure wasn't there?"
- "What would make you feel more secure in taking these creative risks? What support systems or safety nets could we help put in place?"
