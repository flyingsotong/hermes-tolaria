Poon King Wang is the Chief Strategy and Design AI Officer at the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) and concurrently serves as Director of the Lee Kuan Yew Centre for Innovative Cities (LKYCIC). His work focuses on the human dimensions of smart cities, digital transformation, and the future of work, education, and healthcare.

**Introduction**

This presentation highlights the shift in perspective regarding AI, moving beyond it as just a tool to considering it a teammate. The speaker, with a background in policy, banking, analytics, and university transformation, emphasizes translating research into practice.

**SUTD: A Design University**

- SUTD (Singapore University of Technology and Design) is a young, small university with approximately 500 students, jokingly referred to as the "SME of universities."

- It has pivoted to become the world's first design-led university.

- A Design AI degree was started in 2019, with a focus on people's needs.

**Key Research Conclusion**

- AI is no longer just a tool; it's also a teammate.

- Users can learn to create from the bottom up without technical training.

- This approach is seen as increasing job opportunities, especially for SMEs, by enabling internal team building and cost reduction, potentially leading to future collaborations with larger entities.

**Why "Stop Using AI Wrong"?**

**1. Noise vs. Signal**

- The AI landscape is noisy, with many self-proclaimed experts emerging quickly.

- Research helps to separate the signal from the noise.

**2. Misinformation from Right Sources**

- Even reputable sources can provide misleading information.

-**Example:** Reports of 95% of GenAI pilots failing were later clarified to reveal that professionals preferred consumer LLM interfaces due to trust and better answers, highlighting a challenge for enterprise AI tools.

**3. Geopolitical Influence**

- Perceptions of AI optimism differ significantly between countries (e.g., China vs. America).

- This can influence how individuals interpret and approach AI, potentially leading to pessimism if primarily exposed to Western perspectives.

**Three Ways We Are Using AI Wrong**

**1. Underusing Underpowered AI**

- Many users are not utilizing the full capabilities of AI models (e.g., reasoning models in ChatGPT).

-**Example:** Only a small percentage of users (1% free, 7% paid before automatic selection; 7% free, 24% paid after) were using reasoning models, missing out on more powerful AI.

- Analogy: Choosing a less capable financial advisor or intern when a better option is available for the same price.

**2. Limited Multimodal Usage**

- Most users (65%) are only using text-based AI.

- Only 25% are using multimodal features (graphs, image generation, code, calculations).

- Only 10% are creating agents.

- Analogy: Hiring an intern who can only write well versus one who can also do mathematics, Excel, PowerPoint, coding, and presentations.

**3. Organizational Level: Enterprise vs. Consumer Interfaces**

- Enterprise AI tools are often underperforming compared to consumer LLM interfaces.

- SMEs need to demand better from enterprise vendors.

- Enterprise providers must improve their offerings to compete with consumer-grade AI.

**Solutions: Three Ways to Get AI Right**

**1. Work with AI as a Teammate (100% Utilization)**

-**Co-forecasting:** Using multiple language models (open-source and proprietary) to forecast global trends quickly and accurately, offering advantages over traditional, time-consuming methods.

-**Digital Triplets:** Exploring the creation of digital replicas to multiply productivity, as demonstrated by research on the speaker's own performance with AI models.

 - Straightforward (yes/no) answers: AI can achieve 90%+ accuracy.

 - Complex answers: AI achieves \~45% accuracy.

 - Different models excel in different areas (e.g., Western models for yes/no, Chinese models for complex learning).

-**Human-AI Collaboration:** AI disrupts tasks and skills, not entire jobs. Humans can focus on increasing value in remaining tasks.

-**Practical Application:** An example of an individual using an "arsenal of AI" tools (at least 7 steps) to create a speculative iPhone 17 Pro concept, demonstrating that AI is a tool for augmentation, not replacement.

**2. Empower Everyone to Use AI (100% Capability)**

-**Training Initiatives:** SUTD has trained 70% of its staff in one year, with 90% expressing a desire to learn more after a two-hour course.

-**Lifelong Learning:** The training fosters enthusiasm for continued learning, unlike traditional RPA courses.

-**Versatile Training:** Programs cater to various needs, including teachers, focusing on hands-on building to generate excitement and self-driven learning.

-**Student Innovation:** Students with no prior coding background are able to cut prototyping time from days to hours, accelerating feedback cycles.

 - Hallucinations are viewed as a design problem, not a technical one.

 - Students are encouraged to build and create, demonstrating "democratized creation."

-**Examples of Student Projects:**

 - Grading GPT: Critiques and grades images based on design and architectural principles.

 - SUTD Habits: An internal resource for policies, built by students and staff in two weekends, managing 200 documents.

 - Visualization and graphics tools.

-**Master's Class in Smart Cities:** International students with no coding background built an interview companion using voice interaction, adopted by the National Library's Oral History Centre.

**3. Organizational Leapfrogging**

-**SGTech Leadership:** SGTech is taking the lead in promoting AI adoption, with a conservative estimate of 30% of SMEs already engaged.

-**Productivity Outcomes:** A trial for use cases showed that 75% of submitted cases achieved 5-10% or more productivity gains, with some reaching up to 30%.

 - This demonstrates that SMEs can achieve significant productivity improvements, surpassing national ITM sector growth averages.

-**Badging Process:** SGTech is developing a badging process to help SMEs justify grant applications and support by demonstrating achieved outcomes and productivity gains.

-**Ecosystem Collaboration:** SUTD offers organizational laboratories, industry training, and customer training, fostering a collaborative ecosystem for AI adoption.

**Call to Action**

- Submit use cases to demonstrate collective progress and confidence to agencies.

- Leverage the expertise and resources available to leapfrog to enterprise solutions and enhance the ecosystem. Show that SMEs are well on their way to AI adoption. This can be achieved with minimal time investment (estimated 6.5 hours).

**Conclusion**

By embracing AI as a teammate, utilizing its full capabilities, and fostering an environment of continuous learning and innovation, individuals and organizations can unlock significant value and drive progress. The research and practical examples from SUTD demonstrate that democratized creation with AI is achievable and highly beneficial. The goal is to enable individuals to perform effectively while enjoying a better work-life balance, as envisioned by the speaker's hope of "sipping a pina colada by the beach" while AI assists in academic publishing. The focus is on making AI work for people, not the other way around. The speaker encourages the audience to step up and embrace the right way of doing AI. The speaker is confident in their ability to help SMEs based on ten years of research and experience, allowing them to move fast with confidence and make informed bets on what works.
