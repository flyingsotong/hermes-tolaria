**May 15, 2024**

According to the transcript, the main thing the coachee Amruta wanted awareness around was her career progression and the impact of her recent job change from being the "Inclusive Economies Editor" to the "Asia Editor" at Thomson Reuters Foundation.

She voiced concerns about whether taking this regional role was a step back in her goals of attaining a more global journalism position. She wondered if being pigeonholed as the "Asia person" would limit her future opportunities. She was also grappling with a sense of having to start over after relocating to London for a job that no longer exists in its original form.

Through the conversation, some of the key awarenesses created for Amruta were:

1. Titles and roles are transient, and should not solely define her career journey or potential. What matters more are the skills, reputation and experience she builds.
2. Restructurings and job changes are common, especially in large media organizations. This is likely the first of many she will face, so she needs to reframe her perspective on them.
3. She has more control over her personal narrative and skills development than the internal changes at her company.
4. Her current role, though a "side-step", is an opportunity to broaden her experience in new verticals like climate and tech reporting.
5. As an editor, developing strong people management and leadership abilities is crucial for career growth, beyond just journalism skills.

Overall, the conversation helped Amruta see this job change as a small phase in her larger career, gave her a more philosophical outlook on titles/roles, and oriented her towards proactively building the skills and reputation she wants.

Some better questions Alan could have asked:

1. What specific skills or experiences is she hoping to gain in this Asia Editor role that could aid her future goals?
2. How can she continue scratching her itch for substantive reporting and bylines while in an editing position?
3. What would her dream "global" role look like and what kind of reputation does she want to build towards that?
4. Does she see value in specializing more regionally for a period of time before going global again?
5. How can she seek out mentors or sponsors, perhaps women editors, who have made similar transitions?

Overall, Alan did a good job of reframing Amruta's perspective, but some more probing questions on her specific goals and actionable skill-building could have been useful.
