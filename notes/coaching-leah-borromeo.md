# Coaching - Leah Borromeo

---

**February 12, 2025**

**What Leah needed to work on**
- She was feeling "stuck" after a major professional setback where a collaborator ran away with their project
- She was struggling with grief, self-doubt, and lost confidence in her abilities
- She needed to regain her "moxie" — her previous ability to confidently pitch and secure funding and opportunities
- She was fearful about her future direction and mistrustful after being burned

**What she needed to hear**
- Permission to acknowledge and process her grief over the lost project while still moving forward
- Recognition that her skills and capabilities remain valuable, even if the industry has changed
- That her fears and mistrust are natural responses but shouldn't define her future choices
- That she doesn't have to see herself as "the risky option" — she can reframe her experience as an asset

**What she got out of the conversation**
- A metaphorical framework (harbor vs anchor) that helped her see she needs a base to return to, not just a fixed point
- Recognition that the industry has fundamentally changed — it's more outcome-focused vs process-focused now
- Understanding that her grief and lost trust were holding her back more than actual lack of ability
- "Resolve to leave the harbor" with more confidence that it's not crazy to take risks

**Areas for coaching improvement**
1. Could have established clearer goals and structure for the conversation upfront — it meandered quite a bit
2. Could have done more to help her develop concrete next steps or action items
3. Could have challenged some of her self-limiting beliefs more directly
4. Could have helped her identify specific strategies to rebuild confidence in pitching and funding situations
5. Could have explored more deeply what success looks like for her now vs trying to recapture past approaches

The conversation seemed therapeutic but could have been more focused on forward-looking solutions while still acknowledging her emotional journey.
