**January 27, 2025**

Catherine expressed concerns about the future of journalism given industry changes like layoffs at legacy media organizations, the rise of AI content generation, and peers leaving the profession.

**Key insights**

The conversation helped Catherine understand that traditional definitions of "capital J journalism" may be limiting her career prospects. She realized that holding onto idealistic notions of journalism might prevent her from seeing other valuable opportunities to have impact.

She gained clarity on the challenges of news business models — specifically that people don't pay for general news because it lacks clear tangible benefits, unlike financial news or specialized content that helps readers make money or better decisions.

She learned about the potential of building a personal brand and monetizing expertise directly, using examples like Kara Swisher who negotiates opportunities based on her individual brand value rather than institutional affiliation.

**Areas where I could have gone deeper**

1. Career identity exploration — when Catherine mentioned feeling torn between idealism and pragmatism, I could have asked more probing questions about what specific aspects of journalism work give her the most satisfaction and energy. This could have helped identify transferable elements she could carry forward into new roles.
2. Skills assessment — I could have explored more deeply what unique skills and expertise Catherine has developed at Forbes and SCMP that could differentiate her, especially given her experience with data and lists.
3. Concrete next steps — while we discussed broad directions like developing a personal brand, I could have helped Catherine break down more specific, actionable next steps — perhaps creating a 30–60–90 day plan for exploring new opportunities while maintaining her current role.
4. Revenue model deep dive — when discussing potential paths forward, I could have spent more time examining different revenue models for individual journalists and creators. This could have helped make the possibilities feel more concrete and achievable.
5. Network leverage — I could have asked more about her existing professional network and how she might leverage those relationships as she explores new directions. This could have revealed immediate opportunities or potential mentors.

The session succeeded in helping Catherine question some limiting beliefs about journalism and see new possibilities. However, going deeper in these areas could have provided more tactical guidance for her next steps.
