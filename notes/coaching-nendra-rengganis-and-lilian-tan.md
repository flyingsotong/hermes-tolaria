# Coaching - Nendra Rengganis and Lilian Tan

**Tuesday, April 30, 2024**

- 10:00am met with Nendra Rengganis for introductory coaching call
  - The main problem Nendra started with was figuring out her next steps and potential business ideas after having to shut down the media company (Hipwee) she had worked at since its early days. She mentioned feeling a bit lost after that closure and was exploring ideas around creating content and building a community/services for Muslim women, particularly those in their late 20s to early motherhood stage.
- 11:00am met with Lilian Tan for introductory coaching call
  - The main problem Lilian started with was that she was considering joining a media startup company run by Alan's former colleague Miro. However, the job scope and working arrangements had recently changed from what was initially discussed, making Lilian hesitant about taking the role.
- 3:00pm met with Bryan Goh, Adrian Lim for Group Mentor Coaching Session 4
  - Some of the key lessons learned:
  1. The importance of asking concise, simple questions rather than overexplaining or providing too much context before getting to the question. One coach pointed out that when they start explaining the context too much before asking the question, they may be imposing their own viewpoint or making assumptions.
  2. Learning to "hold space" for the client and allowing them to explore their thoughts at their own pace, without rushing or interrupting unnecessarily. One coach commented that by creating open space, the client was able to go deeper into exploring their thoughts.
  3. Coaching the person, not the problem or situation. One coach highlighted a book titled "Coach the Person, Not the Problem" which resonated with the idea of focusing on the individual rather than getting too caught up in the context or details of their situation.
  4. Recognizing that coaching is not just about formal coaching sessions, but can be integrated into daily conversations and interactions as a way of being present with others.
  5. One coach reflected on the need to build more confidence in simply asking questions without overexplaining, recognizing this as an area for personal growth.
