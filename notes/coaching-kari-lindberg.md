**April 15, 2025**

**What Kari wanted to achieve:**

Kari wanted to explore future career possibilities as a "launching pad" because she felt "in a bit of a rut." She expressed disillusionment with her current company (Bloomberg) and uncertainty about whether her feelings stemmed from the company or the broader industry. She wanted to understand what continuing in journalism might look like versus pivoting to something else. She specifically mentioned having a "nagging sense" that her ideal future job might be in information curation, possibly in a role or platform she wasn't yet aware of.

**Kari's awareness and discoveries:**

- Clarity on dissatisfaction: she confirmed her desire to move on from her current role, stating clearly, "I don't want to do what I'm doing now."
- Valued skills identification: Kari articulated the aspects of her work she enjoys and wants to carry forward — the investigative part, talking to people, and connecting the dots.
- Exploration of transferable skills: she identified a potential new area of interest by considering corporate investigation firms like Kroll, recognizing the similarity in "connecting the dots," even if the output isn't public.
- Acknowledgement of uncertainty: Kari verbalized her struggle in defining her future career path and identifying what achievement would make her proud, noting she felt less certain now compared to five years prior.
- Perspective shift on opportunities: towards the end, Kari seemed to internalize the point about new possibilities existing today that weren't available years ago, calling it "a good point" and "maybe that's the better way to look at it."

**What worked:**

- Confirmed confidentiality and asked Kari what she wanted to focus on, aligning the session's start with her preferences
- Created a safe environment by validating Kari's perspective, confirming confidentiality, and showing empathy
- Effectively reflected Kari's initial statements
- Encouraged forward momentum, helped Kari identify valuable skills, and offered ongoing support

**Areas for improvement:**

- Maintained Presence and Active Listening: when Kari expressed uncertainty, sometimes moved quickly to offer external ideas (like the creator economy) or reframes. A potential improvement would be to linger more in Kari's "not knowing," using questions to explore the uncertainty itself before introducing solutions or new concepts.
- Evoking Awareness: introducing concepts like the creator economy might have slightly shifted from evoking awareness from Kari to providing external information. Allowing Kari more space to explore her own "nagging sense" and generate her initial thoughts before layering in external trends could further empower her discovery process.
- Checking back against Kari's broader goals for coaching throughout the conversation could strengthen the partnership in managing progress towards the desired outcomes.
