# School of Splice

Operations

Management gets harder as a company grows, too. When you’re four people, you can make everyone happy most of the time.

At 200, that's just not possible.

So by the time you get to 200, you'll need to make sure that your management skills keep up with the growth of your team.

In the early days, it’s good to have your hands in lots of things, because it helps you intimately understand the business, which lets you make better decisions. Later on, that same instinct will turn you into a blocker.

There are no people problems, only process problems.

So go back to your principles. Write them down.

Go back to your mission. Develop a deep, understanding of your customer/user/subscriber/member and what they care about. Remember, there are no cheat codes; you just have to spend time with them. You have to ask them what matters to them, and then internalize those with your team.

That becomes your first principle: Solving a specific need of your customer.

Given that this is the number one principle, decide: How much time do you want to spend on this?

Once you have that first principle, create your second: How do you know that you're doing this right? What numbers will tell you that?

Test quickly. Don't bother with too many drawn-out debates internally. Whenever possible, get the product out there so that your users can make that decision for you. Learn to outsource decisions that way.
