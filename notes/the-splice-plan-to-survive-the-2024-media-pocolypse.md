1. Boldly re-define the meaning of journalism by focusing on the principles and process of this work, not the content

2. Double down learning and meeting our users’ needs — default to the problems to solve and the value that the work of journalism can create for them

3. Mentor and coach media professionals ready for this transition — create new case studies of what’s possible; build a new brain trust of allies to drive this pivot

**What’s happening in media this year (what we can say that you probably can’t)**

- Elections around the world are determined by creators whether we like it or not. “Mainstream” media is irrelevant and obsolete in this conversation despite years and money spent in propping up old newsrooms

- Gen AI will flood the content space with more content — all vying for fragments of your attention. Responsible journalistic content, in the way it’s currently created (serving mass, undefined audiences and their needs), is buried

- Content consumption will increasingly be created and delivered on-demand, real-time — all for the audience of one, but at scale

- Social platforms, already disincentivized to differentiate news content (commercial or governmental benefits), will ignore newsrooms completely

- Traditional media donors, unable to shift to evolve their theories of change to address the above, will continue to double down on old and irrelevant news formats
