# Lift equation

**L = CL × ½ × ρ × V² × S**
**"Clever Pilots Value Skills."**
**CL = Coefficient of Lift**
- Changes with angle of attack
- Varies by airfoil design
- Increases with flap deployment
- Has a maximum value before stall
**ρ (rho) = Air Density**
- Decreases with altitude
- Affected by temperature
- Affected by humidity
- Given in kg/m³ or slugs/ft³
**V = Velocity (True Airspeed)**
- Squared in the equation (V²)
- Most significant factor
- Double speed = quadruple lift
- Must be in consistent units
**S = Wing Area**
- Total lifting surface
- Includes area affected by flaps
- Measured in square feet/meters
- Fixed for a given aircraft
**½ = Constant in the equation**
**Key Points:**
- Lift must equal weight for level flight
- Changes in one factor can be compensated by others
- Used to calculate required speeds
- Helps understand performance changes with conditions
**Practical Applications:**
- Understanding stall speeds
- Performance planning
- Weight and balance effects
- Altitude planning
- Temperature effects on performance
