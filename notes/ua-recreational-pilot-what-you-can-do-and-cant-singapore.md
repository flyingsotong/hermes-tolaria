# UA recreational pilot — what you can do and can’t (Singapore)

As a recreational pilot with a drone that's less than 250 grams, you are in the simplest category for drone flying in Singapore. You are exempt from the most burdensome requirements, but you must still operate responsibly and are subject to all general aviation safety rules. Here is a clear breakdown of what you can and cannot do.

**What You CAN Do**

- **Fly Without Registration or a License:** Your drone's low weight exempts you from the mandatory UA (Unmanned Aircraft) registration and the requirement to get a UA Basic Training Certificate or Pilot Licence.

- **Fly for Fun:** You are free to fly for recreational and hobbyist purposes.

- **Fly in Permitted Areas:** You can fly in open spaces where drone activity is not restricted. This includes the two designated Unmanned Aircraft Flying Areas (UAFAs) at Pandan Reservoir and Dover/One-North.

- **Keep it Simple:** Your primary responsibilities are to fly safely, be aware of your surroundings, and respect the rules outlined below.

**What You Absolutely CANNOT Do**

Even with a sub-250g drone, the following rules are strict and apply to you. Violations can lead to significant fines and even jail time.

- **DO NOT Fly in No-Fly Zones:** This is the most critical rule. You are**prohibited** from flying:

 - Within 5km of any civil airport (like Changi) or military airbase.2

 - Within any**Restricted Areas**,**Danger Areas**,**Prohibited Areas**, or active**Temporary Restricted Areas** (e.g., for the National Day Parade).

 - **Actionable Advice:** Before every flight, you must check the**OneMap** app or the [FlyWhere.sg](https://flywhere.sg/) portal. These are the official sources showing all no-fly zones.

- **DO NOT Fly Higher Than 200 feet (60 meters):** You must keep your drone below this altitude limit to stay well clear of other air traffic.

- **DO NOT Fly in Protected Areas:** You cannot fly your drone in areas designated as "Protected Areas" (e.g., government buildings, military camps, Istana) without a specific permit from the Singapore Police Force (SPF).3

- **DO NOT Fly Over People or Crowds:** You must not fly over any group of people, at public events, or over large crowds at any time.4

- **DO NOT Fly Over Moving Vehicles:** It is forbidden to fly over moving vehicles on expressways and other roads, as this can distract drivers and is a safety hazard.

- **DO NOT Interfere with Emergency Services:** Never fly your drone in a way that could interfere with the operations of police, medical, or fire services.

- **DO NOT Fly Beyond Your Sight:** You must always maintain a direct, unaided visual line of sight with your drone. This means not flying it so far away that you can no longer see it, or flying it behind buildings.5

- **DO NOT Violate Privacy:** You are not allowed to use your drone to take photos or videos of people or their private property without their explicit permission. Flying your drone close to residential buildings and looking into windows is a serious breach of privacy.

In short, while you are free from licensing and registration, you share the same airspace and public safety responsibilities as all other pilots. The best practice is always:**"When in doubt, don't fly out."** Always check the OneMap app before your flight.
