**June 3, 2025**

---

**Saroj's intent**

Saroj came into the coaching call with a clear, albeit somewhat undefined, sense of dissatisfaction and a desire for direction. Her primary intent was to:

1. Understand and address a feeling of being "stuck" — despite a successful career and a leadership role she enjoys, she expressed feeling "stuck," not utilized to her "optimum potential," and that her current situation "is not where I wanted to be"
2. Explore "what next?" — she was grappling with a sense of "emptiness" and looking for what the next phase of her career should be, questioning if this was a "mid-career problem"
3. Evaluate specific options like fellowships — she mentioned considering fellowships as a way to "reinvent herself" but was hesitant about returning to the same situation afterward
4. Find fulfillment — underlying her search was a desire for something "fulfilling" beyond her current achievements, which she acknowledged met her previous aspirations ("whatever I wanted I have achieved I hadn't thought of anything bigger than this")

---

**Saroj's awareness**

Through questioning, Saroj had several key moments of awareness:

1. The 3-year contract as a major anxiety source — when asked if she'd feel the same way at another company without a fixed-term contract, she realized "probably not... that's a good question that I should have provoked myself." She acknowledged the fear she had never previously acknowledged — that the contract uncertainty ("what next if it is not renewed") was a core, previously less-defined stressor
2. Reluctance to "market herself" — a reframe of fellowships as a form of marketing resonated deeply. It connected to her admission of not using her "Dr." title due to a fear of "showing off." She realized this self-marketing reluctance is a "biggest problem" and declared, "I will run a course now for myself how to market yourself"
3. The need for proactive upskilling (especially AI) — the conversation solidified the importance of actively engaging with AI; she recognized she was "directionless" in how to approach this and saw the potential to "become the champion of it in the organization"
4. Taking action on fellowships (regardless of outcome) — she acknowledged the value in applying for fellowships simply to explore the option and remove the "what if": "I should definitely start applying for fellowships... Whether it'll market me or not, that's another question but I should apply"
5. Shifting from passive to active stance — her concluding takeaways about applying for fellowships, upskilling in AI, and addressing her fear about the contract suggest a move towards more active agency

---

**Coaching improvement areas**

**ICF 3 — Establishes and maintains agreements**

A more explicit agreement at the outset on what Saroj wanted to achieve by the end of this specific session could have provided a sharper focus. For example: "Saroj, you've mentioned feeling stuck and exploring fellowships. What would be a really valuable outcome for you from our conversation today?"

**ICF 8 — Facilitates client growth**

While Saroj identified takeaways, more time could have been spent partnering to help her design concrete, SMART actions and explore accountability:

- When she decided to apply for fellowships: "What's one fellowship you'll research this week?" / "By when would you like to have your first application ready?"
- Regarding AI: "What's one specific way you could integrate AI into your workflow tomorrow?" / "How will you track your progress or learning with AI?"
- On marketing herself: "What's the very first small step you could take to 'market yourself' in a way that feels authentic to you, perhaps even this week?"

**ICF 5 & 6 — Maintains presence / listens actively**

At times, personal experiences and perspectives (on fellowships, AI usage, the state of journalism) became quite prominent. The final 10 minutes veered into a broader discussion on the media landscape. While it connected to her work, it might have diluted the focus on her specific actions and next steps. Checking in with "How is this discussion useful for you in relation to your initial goal of feeling less stuck?" could have kept it more client-centric.

**ICF 7 — Evokes awareness**

When Saroj expressed feeling "helpless" despite foreseeing risks, there was an opportunity to explore her past instances of overcoming helplessness: "You've successfully navigated a 22-year career with many challenges, including starting a new company. What strengths did you draw upon then that might be useful now?"
