**May 31, 2023**

Looking forward to chatting about how I am thinking about all of this. Just more background to make the most of our discussion: the majority of the time, I try to shove the questions to the back of my head and just put my blinders on and do the work. Sometimes I feel anxious, and other times I try to figure out from a practical standpoint what I can strengthen in areas of weakness, and who else I can have on my team to compensate for areas where I am lacking skills.

Jan Schaffer is a consultant for my project. She sometimes offers tough love, other times is a cheerleader. She says "plenty of other women have started successful news organizations and you can too. You have already been wildly successful with Planet Princeton."

**But I think it is important to work through the doubts and questions, and what success or failure would look like. If the startup has been in existence more than three years and is not gaining traction in terms of reader support, for example, I would consider it unsuccessful.**

I know lots of news nonprofits hanging on by a thread with budgets of under $100,000 a year. Their solution is to try to get foundations to fund them forever. There are exceptions such as poor urban communities, but I feel if you have been around for five years or more and have a budget that size and have not gotten buy-in for support from readers through donations, events, or other means, maybe you are not meeting the information needs of your community.

I had recruited a co-founder and that didn't work out. **Jan thinks I recruited someone I didn't need partially because of my insecurities.** It just wasn't a good fit and I ended up spending my time managing that person.

I recruited him to handle the business side because of his experience in pharma communications and marketing. After cutting ties with him and talking to journalists I asked to work with me, I've embraced the fact that I need to handle the business side. It's easy to find journalists to handle the editorial side. Also, donors want to connect with the founder.
