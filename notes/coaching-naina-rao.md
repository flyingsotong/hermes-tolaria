# Naina Rao

- Email: npbrao@gmail.com
- Location: Guam

**Monday, April 8, 2024**

Naina Rao initially felt overwhelmed and unsure in her new role as news director for a public radio station in Guam. She explained that she was in a challenging situation — the station's content was not very relevant for the local audience, she had limited resources and funding, and her manager was often absent.

However, by the end of the call, Naina seemed to have gained more clarity and confidence. A few key things she learned:

1. She recognized that being thrust into a management role, especially as her first time in that kind of position, was a common experience that many journalists go through. The transition from doing the day-to-day reporting work to managing a team can be quite daunting.
2. She realized that while the situation presented challenges, it also presented an opportunity for her to develop valuable skills and potentially carve out a unique path for herself, rather than just pursuing the traditional "foreign correspondent" role she had initially envisioned.
3. Alan encouraged Naina to build a network of mentors and advisors she can turn to for guidance, rather than trying to navigate everything on her own. He helped her reframe the questions from "what do I do?" to "how do I think about this?"
4. By the end, Naina seemed reinvigorated and excited about the possibilities ahead, rather than just feeling overwhelmed. She recognized that she had the capabilities to succeed, even without a very hands-on manager or mentor physically present.

In summary, Naina started off feeling unsure and stressed, but by the end of the call she had gained more clarity, confidence and a plan to proactively build the support systems she needs to thrive in this new leadership role.
