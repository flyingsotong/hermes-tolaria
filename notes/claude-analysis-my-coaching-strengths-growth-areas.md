# Claude Analysis My Coaching Strengths & Growth Areas

*Generated: January 30, 2026, Berlin*

**Key coaching attributes**

**Deep Listener & Observer**
I consistently pick up on the language people use, their underlying assumptions, and contradictions in their thinking. I notice what's said and what's not said.

**Reframer & Perspective Shifter**
I excel at helping people see their situations through new lenses. Examples:
- Challenged Sharada to think about demand vs. supply
- Helped Franz see himself as a business leader not just a journalist
- Guided Nikita toward entrepreneurship rather than freelancing

**Bold & Direct**
I'm willing to confront limiting beliefs head-on. I challenge people's narratives about themselves, call out self-sabotaging patterns, and aren't afraid to say what I'm seeing.

**Industry-Savvy Guide**
I bring real-world knowledge about media trends, AI's impact, and business realities. I help people understand the systemic changes happening, not just their personal situations.

**Question Architect**
I use powerful questions strategically, particularly around exploring beliefs, assumptions, and future scenarios. Examples:
- "What's the hardest question you're not asking yourself?"
- "How would you feel about this decision three years from now?"

**Connector & Resource Provider**
I actively make introductions, suggest tools (like AI for content creation), and provide concrete next steps. I don't just coach in the abstract.

**Empathetic & Safe**
I create psychological safety where people can be vulnerable. Multiple coachees mentioned feeling able to open up without judgment.

**Areas for growth**

**Structure & contracting**

- Setting clear outcomes upfront — I could do better establishing what success looks like for the session at the beginning. Sometimes let conversations flow organically without explicitly agreeing on desired outcomes.
- Time management within sessions — conversations sometimes shift significantly, taking time away from the coachee's immediate needs. Example: Grace session when discussing Splice's business model.

**Coaching vs. consulting balance**

- Slipping into advice-giving mode — Maya: "this call was a lot about 'advice' — and I need to steer away from that." Cheena: shifted into consulting/advisory mode rather than coaching.
- Talking time balance — with Nikita: contributions became extended in the middle-to-latter part. Could draw out more solutions through Socratic questioning rather than providing them directly.
- Providing solutions rather than evoking them — sometimes tell people what I see or what they should do, rather than helping them discover it themselves.

**Depth of exploration**

- Not following certain threads: Nikita's creative passion for making short films; Franz's specific leadership challenges, achievements, what energizes him; Cheena's spirituality and service orientation, her software engineering background.
- Going deeper on values and long-term vision — sometimes stay at tactical level rather than exploring the deeper "why" behind goals.

**Action planning & accountability**

- Creating concrete next steps — conversations sometimes end with new perspectives but without concrete next steps.
- Establishing accountability measures — need to help people establish specific accountability for implementing insights.
- Checking for overwhelm — with Nikita: introduced several significant ideas rapidly. Could check in more explicitly to ensure processing rather than just agreeing.

**Self-awareness points**

From my own notes, I've identified that I:
- Can **second-guess people's desire for change** (and therefore hold back)
- Can **hold back on confronting someone** when needed
- May not always recognize the signals that I'm holding back

---

**The pattern: over-functioning**

My weakness isn't about lack of skill — it's about **over-functioning**. I'm so knowledgeable, insightful, and genuinely helpful that I sometimes do the work for my coachees rather than letting them struggle toward their own insights.

The irony is that my greatest strengths (industry expertise, strategic thinking, pattern recognition) are also what pull me toward advice-giving.

**My challenge:** Trust the coaching process even when I can clearly see the answer.

---

**Summary**

My coaching style combines strategic thinking with emotional intelligence, industry expertise with personal development, and bold directness with genuine care.

I'm particularly effective with media professionals navigating industry disruption and career transitions.

The work ahead is not about learning new skills, but about restraining my expertise to create more space for coachees to find their own answers.
