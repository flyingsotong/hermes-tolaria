# America Against China Against America

[Read the full article on Jasmi.news](https://jasmi.news/p/china-2025?utm_campaign=post\&utm_medium=email\&triedRedirect=true)

**Author:** Jasmine Sun

**Published:** Aug 28, 2025


_Jasmine Sun, author_

**Summary**

A personal travelogue and analysis of modern China, exploring the contrasts and connections between China and America through technology, economics, culture, and daily life. The essay weaves family history, interviews, and on-the-ground observations from Shenzhen, Hangzhou, Yuyao, and Shanghai, reflecting on themes of progress, competition, governance, and identity.

**Key Themes & Observations**

-**Family & History:** The author's family journey from Indonesia to China, through political upheaval and reform, sets the stage for understanding generational change.

-**Tech & Governance:** China’s approach to technology is driven by state ambition and social control, while the US relies on private investment and individualism.

-**Competition & Involution:** The concept of "involution" (内卷) describes relentless, sometimes purposeless competition—seen in both China and America.

-**Cities & Infrastructure:** Shenzhen’s rapid growth, Hangzhou’s AI boom, and Shanghai’s consumer abundance highlight China’s modernization, but also its challenges.

-**Work & Mindset:** Chinese technologists and founders are pragmatic, resilient, and focused on building, often less ideological than their Western counterparts.

-**Consumer Experience:** Everyday conveniences, infrastructure, and innovation in China often surpass those in the US, shaping perceptions of quality of life.

-**Returnees & Identity:** Many overseas Chinese are returning, drawn by opportunity and convenience, as America’s immigration climate shifts.

-**Governance & Outcomes:** China’s state-driven targets deliver visible results, while US institutions struggle with follow-through and public trust.

-**Personal Reflection:** The author grapples with identity, belonging, and the pace of change, ultimately valuing optimism and agency in the face of uncertainty.

**Notable Quotes**

> "Who wins in technology, wins the world."

> "Involution is the experience of being locked in competition that one ultimately knows is meaningless."

> "The only way to survive was to believe—stupidly, irrationally, delusionally—that you might beat the odds."
