# Coaching - Julianna Barcela

---

**February 7, 2025**

**What Juliana wanted**

Juliana reached out feeling discouraged after nearly a year of unsuccessful job hunting in journalism. As a recent graduate with a journalism degree, she was seeking direction and advice about her career prospects. She had been particularly frustrated because unlike many of her classmates who chose different paths, she genuinely wanted to pursue journalism but found very few entry-level opportunities.

**Key insights Juliana gained**

First, she recognized that she had been defining her career prospects too narrowly through the lens of traditional journalism. She began to understand that her skills — writing, communication, storytelling, and perspective-taking — were transferable beyond newsrooms.

Second, she gained clarity about the broader industry context — that journalism as an industry is undergoing significant disruption and contraction. This helped her see that her struggles weren't personal failures but rather systemic challenges.

Third, she discovered alternative paths to creating impact, particularly through consulting work with social enterprises and NGOs. She realized she didn't need to be in a newsroom to tell important stories or make a difference.

Finally, she understood that she needed to adapt her approach from seeking traditional employment to potentially creating opportunities for herself as a consultant or independent professional.

**Areas where the coaching could have been stronger**

1. Goal setting — while the conversation helped Juliana expand her thinking, it could have benefited from establishing more concrete next steps or action items. The coaching could have helped her develop a more structured plan for moving forward.
2. Deeper exploration of feelings — when Juliana mentioned feeling like she was "lied to" by her professors, this could have been an opportunity to explore her emotions about this realization more deeply.
3. More questions, less advice — at times, the conversation shifted from coaching to mentoring and advising. While this wasn't necessarily bad given the context, there could have been more opportunities to ask powerful questions that would help her discover answers herself rather than receiving direct advice.
4. Alternative perspective exploration — while the conversation effectively challenged her assumptions about journalism, it could have spent more time exploring what other industries or roles might align with her core values and interests.
5. Resource connection — the coaching could have provided more concrete resources or connections to help her begin her transition.

That said, the coaching was largely effective in achieving its primary goal — helping Juliana shift her perspective and begin thinking differently about her career options. The coach demonstrated strong empathy while still being direct about industry realities, and successfully helped Juliana reframe her situation from one of personal failure to one of opportunity for reinvention.

The success is evident in Juliana's own words near the end of the conversation, where she articulates her shift from seeing journalism as a micro-focus to understanding her transferable skills as the macro opportunity.

**Questions that would have helped**

To help her develop immediate action steps:
- "What one small step could you take this week to start exploring these alternative paths we've discussed?"
- "How would you like to begin approaching social enterprises? What would make you feel prepared to have those conversations?"
- "When you think about the next three months, what would meaningful progress look like to you?"

To help her think through building a consulting practice:
- "What would you need to feel confident presenting yourself as a consultant to these organizations?"
- "How might you test this approach with one organization to learn what works?"
- "What support systems or resources would help you make this transition?"

To help her define success beyond traditional employment:
- "How would you define success for yourself outside of having a full-time journalism job?"
- "What would make you feel proud and fulfilled in your work, regardless of the job title?"

A particularly valuable sequence would have been: acknowledge her new insight about thinking beyond traditional journalism → ask what possibilities this opens up for her → help her identify one or two directions that feel most promising → support her in developing specific first steps → work with her to create accountability and success metrics that make sense for her.
