Wed, February 7th, 2024

- 11:00am met with Grace Thu, Tessa Piper for MDIF's Myanmar insights

 - We reached out for a chat around how to spend the money from OSF in the programs that we could possibly run for Myanmar. The quick summary to all of this is that there is already a lot of support that's out there. It sounds like the foundations are stepping over each other's toes and trying to be useful in this space, and yet no one seems to know. So on the one hand, the NGOs have been involved in this space for a while now. NBIA, for example, is probably the longest and most patient of the lot, having done their Myanmar program for about eight years now. So that's a lot of experience. That space is also being competed by and maybe filled by IMS and other small organizations like Intenews, for example.

 - The challenge here is finding our value add in that space. The addressable market here is probably around 50 newsrooms, but the question again is how many of these are actually available to do the work that needs to be done as part of this training, whatever that might be in the end. Very often, there's a lot of suspicion around what other media companies are doing. So Grace was reminding us that in a lot of these workshops, not many people are willing to open up about the details of the work that they do. For some reason, there's a lot of competition and suspicion among media companies. So the short of it is we don't know what our place is in this case, and we are still trying to figure out how to make the most of that OSF re-grant.
