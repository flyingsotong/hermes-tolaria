**January 22, 2025**

**Key insights Aniket gained**

1. Redefining success and journalism — he realized he was defining journalism too narrowly (full-time newsroom job + writing in English). He understood that his existing data journalism work already makes him a journalist and recognized that he needs to detach his identity from traditional journalism roles.
2. Skills and value recognition — he discovered that his data analysis skills are actually quite valuable and rare in journalism. He realized he doesn't need more certifications or validation to be "ready" and began seeing how his engineering background + data skills + policy interest create a unique value proposition.
3. Industry reality check — he gained clarity about the shrinking traditional journalism industry, understood that the challenges he faces aren't just personal but systemic, and recognized that many journalists want to leave the industry due to low pay and limited growth.
4. Practical next steps — he decided to limit job hunting to specific days to free up time for skill-building, started considering alternative paths like teaching data journalism or policy analysis, and began thinking about how to monetize his skills beyond traditional journalism roles.

**Questions that could have been explored further**

Personal values and motivation:
- "What specifically draws you to policy reporting? How else might you serve that interest?"
- "What does 'making an impact' mean to you personally?"

Skills and strengths:
- "What specific data analysis tools or methods are you most confident with?"
- "What unique perspectives do you bring from your engineering background?"

Alternative paths:
- "What would an ideal workweek look like for you?"
- "How might you combine policy work, data analysis, and journalism in a unique way?"

Financial planning:
- "What's your minimum viable income need?"
- "How long can you sustain your current approach financially?"

**Questions to structure a future session**

Current reality:
- "What makes you say the industry is in turmoil? What specific impacts are you seeing?"
- "When you say you're struggling to stay financially afloat, what does that mean specifically?"

Strategy development:
- "What would 'stability in journalism' look like for you?"
- "How are you currently leveraging your unique background in policy and data?"
- "What aspects of the business side of media interest you most?"

Decision making:
- "What would need to happen in the next 6 months for you to feel confident about staying in journalism?"
- "What would be your criteria for deciding to pivot?"
