# FlyBest PPL program

**10 Jan 2025 at 4:19 PM**

Let me summarize the key points from this conversation about the flight training program at FlyBest in Batam, Indonesia:

**Program Overview:**

- The program provides Private Pilot License (PPL) training

- Main locations are in Batam and Tanjung Pinang, Indonesia

- Total cost is approximately $18,000-20,000 USD (recently updated price)

- Includes 70 hours of ground school and 45-46 hours of flight time

- Does not include accommodation, food, or transportation costs

**Application Process & Prerequisites:**

1. Submit application to FlyBest

2. Apply for student visa (requires DGCA approval first)

3. Complete medical examination in Jakarta

 - Must be done at specific facility in Kamayoran

 - Takes about 3 days total (travel + examination)

 - Includes thorough testing (EKG, x-rays, treadmill test, etc.)

4. Obtain security clearances for airport access

5. Begin ground school training

**Timeline and Structure:**

- Total visa/approval process takes about 2-4 weeks with their agent

- Full-time students can complete the program in 2.5-3 months

- Part-time (weekend) students typically take 4-6 months

- Payment can be divided into 4 installments

- Ground school can begin while waiting for some approvals

- Students can progress at their own pace

- Weather can impact training schedule (especially during monsoon season)

**Flight Training Components:**

- Training is divided into 4 stages

- First solo flight typically occurs after 10-15 hours of training

- Includes cross-country flights between Batam, Tanjung Pinang, and Karimun

- Practice areas include locations near Mapur Island and Bridge 5

- Students must pass both written exams and flight check rides

**Additional Notes:**

- Accommodation in Batam ranges from budget (1.5M rupiah/month for basic apartment) to hotels

- Food costs are reasonable if eating local cuisine

- Discovery flights available for approximately 10M rupiah to try before committing

- Basic insurance is included but additional coverage recommended

- Students can choose full-time or part-time study options
