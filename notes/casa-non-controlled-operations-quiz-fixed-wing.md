# CASA Non-controlled operations quiz (fixed-wing)

**1. What is the definition of 'in the vicinity' of a non-controlled aerodrome?**
- within 5 nm of the aerodrome, and 3,000 ft
- **within 10 nm of the aerodrome, and at a height above the aerodrome that could result in conflict with operations at the aerodrome**
- within 10 nm of the aerodrome, and 5,000 ft
- within 15 nm of the aerodrome and 5,000 ft
**Source:** Part 91 Plain English Guide: Meaning of in the vicinity of a non-controlled aerodrome (91.360), page 114.
**2. When two aircraft are on converging headings at approximately the same altitude:**
- **the aircraft that has the other aircraft on its right must give way**
- the aircraft that has the other aircraft on its left must give way
- both aircraft must turn to their right
- the aircraft that has the other on its left must climb
**Source:** Part 91 Plain English Guide: Right-of-way rules (91.330), page 48, and VFRG page 54.
**3. What does ‘reading you three’ mean with regard to the readability of a radio transmission?**
- perfectly readable
- readable now and then
- readable
- **readable but with difficultySource:** AIP GEN 3.4 para 6.1.1, and VFRG page 356.
**4. As far as practicable, you must land and take-off into wind unless:**
- you make a call to the contrary
- you are an aircraft with a MTOW over 1,500 kg
- **the flight manual allows you to take off downwind or crosswind**
- other aircraft are already established in the circuit
**Source:** Part 91 Plain English Guide: Operating on manoeuvring area, or in the vicinity, of a non-controlled aerodrome – landing and taking off into the wind (91.380), page 115, and VFRG page 242.
**5. What calls are recommended in all circumstances when operating at a non-controlled aerodrome:**
- taxi and inbound
- taxi and base
- **taxi, inbound and base**
- taxi, inbound, base and departure
**Source:** AIP ENR 1.1 para 9.1.14 and Be heard, be seen, be safe radio procedures booklet, page 6.
**6. Right hand circuits can only be made at a non-controlled aerodrome if:**
- a radio call is made to announce the intention
- training is being conducted under instruction
- joining from that side of the circuit
- **procedures are given in the AIPSource:** Part 91 Plain English Guide: Operating on manoeuvring area, or in the vicinity, of non-controlled aerodrome – requirements that apply after joining the circuit pattern (91.385), page 115, and VFRG page 243.
**7. You must not continue an approach to land beyond the threshold until:**
- a departing aircraft using the same runway is airborne
- a departing aircraft using the same runway has reached 500 ft
- a preceding landing aircraft has rolled to a stop
- **a preceding landing aircraft using the same runway has vacated it, or is taxiing away from the runwaySource:** AIP ENR 1.1 para 9.16, VFRG page 241, and Part 91 Plain English Guide: Rules for landing, page 113.
**8. When operating VFR in Class G airspace above 3000 ft AMSL, the VFR specified cruising level for a track of 080 is:**
- **odd thousands plus 500 ft**
- even thousands plus 500 ft
- odd thousands
- even thousands
**Source:** Part 91 Manual of Standards 2.08, page 24, and Part 91 Plain English Guide: Cruising levels and minimum heights (91.2750, page 99.
**9. A pilot conducting a straight in approach must give way to other traffic in the circuit and:**
- give a radio call and be established on final by 3 nm
- determine the wind direction and be established on final by 5 nm
- **determine the wind and runway in use at the aerodrome, and be established on final by 3 nm**
- be established on final by 5 nm
**Source:** Part 91 Plain English Guide: Straight-in approaches at non-controlled aerodromes (91.395), page 116, and AIP ENR 1.1 para 9.13.
**10. What radio calls are recommended when other traffic is in the vicinity:**
- **turning downwind, turning base, turning finals**
- entering the runway, joining the circuit, conducting a straight in approach, clear of runway
- turning downwind, turning base
- entering runway, turning base
**Source:** AIP ENR 1.1 para 9.1.14 and Part 91 Plain English Guide: Non-controlled aerodromes – prescribed broadcasts, page 51.
