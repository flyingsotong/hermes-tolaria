**May 2, 2024**

Session with Sherry Wong at CreatorWeek 2024. From Jim Louderback: the session should touch on what Sherry is doing, but lean into how creators are building teams, with the creator as CEO. Every creator is essentially a startup Direct to Community CEO, and team composition is essential — along with experience in the creator space. Look at what happened with Watcher last week, as they tried to cut off their YouTube channel and go 100% paid. Easy to call it a poor strategic decision, but these sorts of decisions and issues are popping up all over.

Sherry is the CEO of Roster — the go-to hiring platform for creators and brands. Roster's recommendation engine gives creators and brands personalized and curated lists of creative talent to hire for behind-the-camera roles (eg. video editors, scriptwriters, thumbnail designers). Their platform is home to talent who have worked for the biggest creators like MrBeast, Dude Perfect, Nas Daily, and more. Sherry is also a YouTuber herself — she's been filming videos for over 10 years and has 9+ million views.

**Interview questions**

1. Team building strategies: Can you describe the process and criteria you use to build a team around a creator, considering the creator as the CEO of their own startup?
2. How much of this was informed by your own experience as a YouTuber?
3. Role importance: From your experience, what is the most under-appreciated job function in a new creator's team, and why do you think it is often overlooked?
4. Hiring challenges: What are some common mistakes that creators make in their first year regarding hiring, and how can they avoid them?
5. First hire: In your opinion, what should be the first hire for a new creator and why?
6. Strategic decisions: Reflecting on incidents like Watcher's attempt to transition to a 100% paid model, what strategic advice would you give to creators considering similar significant changes?
7. Insight development: How did you come across the idea for Roster, and what gap did you see in the market that it addresses?
8. Success DNA: What common traits have you identified in successful creator organizations?
9. Market saturation: How do you navigate the challenges of hiring elite talent in a market that is flooded with candidates?
10. Future trends: What emerging trends do you foresee in the creator economy, and how should creators prepare to adapt to these changes?
11. Personal insights: What has been the most surprising or unexpected lesson you've learned while working in the creator space?
