# Coaching - Cheena Kapoor

**February 10, 2025**

Cheena came to the conversation feeling lost and seeking direction, with several key concerns:

- The instability of freelance income, especially after having a baby
- The shrinking opportunities as publications cut budgets and change editors
- The tension between loving her work (especially ground reporting) but needing more stability
- Uncertainty about whether to pursue a full-time job vs continuing freelance work
- A desire to keep doing meaningful journalism while ensuring financial security

**Key insights she gained:**

1. Recognition that her definition of "journalism" may be too narrow — she began seeing her work more broadly as "service" to communities.
2. Understanding of industry trends: newsrooms shrinking, rise of creators/influencers, impact of AI on journalism, reality that current business models aren't sustainable.
3. Practical solutions she hadn't considered: using AI tools like Claude as an editor, exploring retainer arrangements with publications, thinking about impact beyond just writing articles.

**Areas for improvement:**

1. Structure: The conversation could have started with more clearly establishing what success would look like for the session.
2. Balance: You provided valuable industry insights but sometimes shifted into consulting/advisory mode rather than coaching. More questions could have helped Cheena discover her own answers.
3. Exploration: Several interesting threads weren't fully explored — her mention of spirituality and service, her software engineering background, her photography work.
4. Action planning: The conversation ended with new perspectives but without concrete next steps for Cheena to pursue.

**What worked:**

- Created psychological safety for honest dialogue
- Helped challenge limiting beliefs
- Provided practical suggestions while showing empathy
- Used probing questions about what journalism means to her
- Helped her see her work through a different lens (service vs just journalism)

The conversation gave Cheena valuable new perspectives and starting points for reimagining her career path, even if it could have gone deeper in some areas.
