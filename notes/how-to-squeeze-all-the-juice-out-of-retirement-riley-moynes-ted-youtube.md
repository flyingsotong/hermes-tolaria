1. **Phase One - The Vacation Phase**
- This is when retirement feels like a permanent vacation.
- You wake up when you want, do what you want, and have no set routine.
- Most people view this as an ideal retirement initially.
- This phase typically lasts for about a year before it starts to lose its luster.

2. **Phase Two - The Lost Phase**
- You start to feel lost and experience significant losses associated with retirement.
- The "big five" losses: routine, identity, relationships, purpose, and power.
- You also face the three D's: divorce, depression, and decline (physical and mental).
- This phase can lead to fear, anxiety, and depression.

3. **Phase Three - The Trial and Error Phase**
- You start asking how to make life meaningful again and how to contribute.
- You experiment with different activities that you love and do well.
- This phase can involve disappointments and failures as you try out various things.
- The goal is to find activities that make you want to get up in the morning.

4. **Phase Four - The Reinvention and Rewiring Phase**
- You answer tough questions about your purpose and mission in life.
- You find meaningful activities that give you a sense of accomplishment, often involving service to others.
- The losses experienced in Phase Two (routine, identity, relationships, purpose, and power) are recovered.
- This phase is described as the most gratifying and satisfying, where you can "squeeze all the juice out of retirement."
- The speaker encourages enjoying Phase One, being prepared for Phase Two, experimenting in Phase Three, and ultimately reaching Phase Four to experience a fulfilling retirement.
