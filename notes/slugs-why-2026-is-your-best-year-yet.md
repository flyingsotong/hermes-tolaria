Last week, I told you that the "this seems overblown" phase of AI is over. Today, I want to tell you why that is the best news we’ve had in a decade.

Chris Loy recently wrote about the [Rise of Industrial Software](https://chrisloy.dev/post/2025/12/30/the-rise-of-industrial-software), making the case that coding has reached its "steam engine" moment. It’s moving from a slow, expensive craft to an automated industrial process.

Journalism is hitting the exact same inflection point. For years, we have been trapped in the "factory" phase of media—spending 80% of our time on the mechanics of production (transcribing, drafting, formatting, SEO-hacking) and only 20% on the actual journalism.

**Industrialization is about to flip that ratio.** When the cost of production drops to zero, the value of the "craft" doesn't disappear—it just moves upstream.

**From "Factory Worker" to "System Architect"**
In the industrial era of software, the "junk" (or "AI slop") will be everywhere. But as Loy points out, industrialization also accelerates innovation. Because the "boring" parts are now automated, we are finally free to solve harder, more interesting problems.

In my recent projects, I’ve stopped thinking about myself as a "writer" or a "coder." Instead, I’m an**architect of context.**

When I built that MacOS weather app, I didn't write the logic; I designed the *instructions* and provided the *data*. I was the editor-in-chief of a machine. This is the "Golden Opportunity": AI is taking the "drudgery" of the factory off your plate so you can focus on the**Signal**.

**The "Minimum Viable Experiment": 3 Steps for Monday Morning**
The biggest mistake you can make right now is waiting for a "strategy" from the top. Innovation in 2026 is bottom-up. You don't need a dev team; you just need curiosity.

Here is how you start being a "beginner" again, with the smallest steps possible:
1.**Stop Prompting, Start Documenting:** Don't just ask ChatGPT to "write a headline." Instead, create a "Style Guide" document. List your tone, your audience, and 10 examples of headlines you love. Upload that file to a Custom GPT or a Claude Project.**You have just built your first piece of infrastructure.**
2.**The "Interview Shadow":** Take your next raw interview transcript. Don't ask for a summary. Ask: *"What are the 3 things this person avoided saying?"* or *"Compare this transcript to the city's budget report—where are the contradictions?"* Use the machine for**pattern recognition**, not just text generation.
3.**Build a "Decision Scaffold":** Pick one repetitive task (like Sunday planning). Write down the 5 steps you usually take. Feed that list to an AI and say: *"I want you to be my Chief of Staff. Every Sunday, I will give you my notes, and you will apply these 5 steps to create my briefing."*

**Why Trust is the Premium Product**

Loy mentions the "Organic Software" movement—the demand for handcrafted, durable goods in an era of fast-fashion code.

In our world,**Trust is the new Organic.**

As the internet fills with "Industrial Slop"—perfectly formatted but hollow content—the value of a verified, human-vetted signal will skyrocket. The machine can simulate a "vibe," but it cannot build a relationship with a community. It cannot walk into a courtroom. It cannot look a source in the eye.

The goal isn't to compete with the factory; it’s to**own the factory.** Use the industrial tools to handle the volume, so you can spend your human energy on the one thing the machine can't replicate:**Judgment.**

The wall is gone. The tools are here. The only thing left to do is start.
