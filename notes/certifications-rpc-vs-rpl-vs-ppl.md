# Certifications RPC vs RPL vs PPL

|**Feature** |**RPC (Recreational Pilot Certificate)** |**RPL (Recreational Pilot Licence)** |**PPL (Private Pilot Licence)** |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
|**Issuing Authority** | Recreational Aviation Australia (RAAus) | Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA) | Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA) |
|**Aircraft Type** | Light Sport Aircraft (typically up to 600kg MTOW) | Larger Aircraft (typically up to 1,500kg MTOW) | Wide range of aircraft |
|**Passenger Limit** | Usually 1 | More than 1 (typically up to 3) | More than 1 |
|**Operational Area** | Limited distance from departure point | Greater flexibility, but still some limitations | Fewer restrictions |
|**Training Requirements** | Generally less extensive | More extensive than RPC | Most extensive |
|**Medical Requirements** | Less stringent | More stringent than RPC | Most stringent |
|**Purpose** | Recreational flying, light sport aircraft | Recreational flying, broader range of aircraft | Recreational flying, greater flexibility |
|**Cost** | Generally lower | Moderate | Higher |
|**Progression** | Can be a stepping stone to RPL/PPL | Can be a stepping stone to PPL | Highest level of recreational pilot licensing |
