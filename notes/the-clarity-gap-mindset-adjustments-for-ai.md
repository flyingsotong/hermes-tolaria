# The Clarity Gap Mindset adjustments for AI

**The 3 Big Changes**

**1. The cost of doing has collapsed.**
Writing code, drafting content, analyzing data — the marginal cost of *execution* is approaching zero. What's expensive now is knowing what to do. The Clarity Gap is the new risk, not low output.

**2. The org structures we built are now friction.**
Stand-ups, sprint planning, code review, QA — all of it was designed around human limitations. When AI handles implementation, the coordination layer that consumes 60% of a manager's time stops serving a purpose. The org is running a new engine on an old transmission.

**3. The gap between early adopters and everyone else is accelerating, not closing.**
This isn't a bell curve where laggards eventually catch up. The feedback loops have closed — AI is building better AI — and the distance between frontier teams and average teams is widening faster than most people realize. The "I'll figure it out when I have to" window is shorter than it looks.

---

**The 3 Mindset Shifts**

**1. From producer to architect.**
Stop defining yourself by what you make. Start defining yourself by what you specify, direct, and evaluate. The value is upstream now.

**2. From stability to tolerance for contact.**
Most people stop the moment their taste outruns their ability. AI makes that gap more visible, not less. The shift is learning to stay in the discomfort — shipping imperfect things, running experiments, closing the gap through volume rather than waiting for readiness.

**3. From expertise as identity to judgment as practice.**
The most dangerous position right now is "my field is different." Expertise built over decades doesn't protect you — it can actually make you slower to adapt because there's more identity at stake.

---

**The Coaching Questions**

**Change 1 → Shift 1: Producer to Architect**
- What are you still doing that you should be directing?
- If execution became free tomorrow, what would your job actually be?
- Where are you the bottleneck because you haven't articulated what you want clearly enough?

**Change 2 → Shift 2: Stability to Tolerance for Contact**
- What structure in your organization exists to manage a human limitation that's no longer the constraint?
- What would you stop doing if you trusted the output?
- What's the smallest experiment you could run this week that would give you real information?

**Change 3 → Shift 3: Expertise as Identity to Judgment as Practice**
- What are you protecting by waiting?
- Where is your expertise actually your blind spot right now?
- If someone earlier than you is already ahead — what did they let go of that you haven't?
