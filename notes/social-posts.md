What business are you in?

It’s not what you’re selling. It’s what you’re solving.

We want to show value, provide solutions, and keep the conversation moving. But I'm learning that sometimes, the most valuable thing I can offer is a moment of genuine, quiet attention.

When I intentionally pause and focus on understanding _why_ someone is saying what they're saying – what their underlying need, challenge, or hope is – the entire dynamic shifts. It moves beyond a simple exchange of information or a sales pitch.

It becomes an opportunity for real connection. This isn't just a 'soft skill'; it's fundamental. Whether we're in sales trying to understand a client, a leader guiding a team, or a colleague collaborating, seeking that deeper 'why' helps us contribute far more effectively. It stops feeling like _pushing_ our agenda and starts feeling like building _together_.

It's a practice, and I don't always get it right, but the effort feels worthwhile. It leads to better understanding, stronger relationships, and, frankly, simpler and kinder interactions.

What helps _you_ stay present and listen deeply?

In my coaching work with media professionals, I've noticed two distinct approaches to professional growth: the Seeker and the Finder.

The Seeker is perpetually in pursuit of validation. They believe that one more certification, one more course, or one more degree will finally make them "ready”. The challenge isn't their pursuit of knowledge—it's the underlying belief that they aren't enough without these external validations.

Finders, in contrast, recognize that growth happens through application, not accumulation. They take what they learn and apply it immediately, treating each experience as a laboratory for growth. They don't wait for permission to begin.

The key difference isn't in the amount of learning — both value education. It's in their relationship with action. Seekers use learning as a shield, protecting themselves from their fears. Finders use learning as a tool, immediately putting new knowledge to work.

The world doesn't need you to be perfect; it needs you to grow and use your current capabilities now.


Success isn't about grand gestures – it's about the power of compound habits. Just like how money grows through compound interest, your daily actions multiply over time. Want to write a book? 300 words daily becomes 109,500 words in a year. Want to transform your health? A 10-minute walk compounds into 60+ hours of movement. Want to learn a new skill? 15 minutes of practice snowballs into 91 hours of expertise. The math is simple, but the magic lies in consistency. Each small action builds upon the last, creating momentum that transforms tiny habits into extraordinary results. As we begin 2025, remember: You don't need to do everything today. You just need to do something small that your future self will thank you for.
