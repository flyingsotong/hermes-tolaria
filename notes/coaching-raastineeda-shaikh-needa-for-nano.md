1. Her broadcast skills may not be directly transferable to other industries — this realization helps create a clearer framework for skill-building.
2. The photojournalism industry has changed dramatically, requiring a different approach than in the past.
3. There are practical steps she can take while maintaining her current job:
   - Explore NGO photography opportunities
   - Attend the Angkor Photo Festival
   - Build a portfolio through personal projects

5. Her resistance to certain changes (like working with influencers) might be limiting her opportunities.

**What you (Alan) did well:**

1. Established rapport through shared experience.
2. Provided practical, actionable suggestions.
3. Balanced honesty about industry realities with encouragement.
4. Shared personal experiences to illustrate points.
5. Offered specific resources (Angkor Photo Festival).
6. Identified underlying resistance to change and encouraged exploration of those barriers.

**Areas for improvement:**

1. The conversation sometimes meandered through shared experiences — while this built rapport, it occasionally took focus away from Needa's specific situation.
2. Could have spent more time exploring specific skills Needa needs to develop for her desired transitions.
3. Might have helped to create a more structured short-term action plan, given your concern about the 10-year timeline being too long.
4. **Could have explored more deeply why Needa is resistant to certain changes in media (like the influencer space) to help her overcome these barriers.**
5. The conversation could have benefited from more specific questions about what success looks like for Needa in her next role.

Overall, it was a productive conversation that helped Needa feel understood and gave her some concrete next steps while making her aware of industry realities. A follow-up session focused on specific action steps and timeline would be valuable.

Here are some thoughtful questions to help explore and unpack Needa's resistance to changes in media:

**Values-based questions:**

- "When you say you're principally against influencer culture, what specific aspects conflict with your values?"
- "What does being a 'real journalist' mean to you personally?"
- "What parts of traditional journalism do you feel are most important to preserve?"

**Experience-based questions:**

- "Can you tell me about a time when you saw quality journalism make a real difference?"
- "What's the most meaningful story you've worked on, and what made it special?"
- "Have you encountered any influencers or new media creators whose work you actually respect? What sets them apart?"

**Future-focused questions:**

- "How do you imagine quality journalism could evolve while maintaining its core purpose?"
- "What if you could help new media creators adopt some of the journalistic standards you value?"
- "What would need to change about digital/influencer content for you to feel comfortable contributing to it?"

**To help her overcome these barriers:**

- **Validate and reframe:** Acknowledge her concerns about journalistic integrity. Help her see that her values and skills could actually improve the quality of new media content. Frame it as evolution rather than abandonment of principles.
- **Bridge the gap:** Explore examples of high-quality digital journalism that successfully bridges traditional and new media. Discuss how her skills could help maintain journalistic standards in new formats. Look for opportunities where she could apply traditional journalistic rigor to new media platforms.
- **Start small:** Suggest experimenting with one small project that aligns with both her values and new media formats. Help her identify specific creators or platforms that might align better with her standards. Encourage her to define her own terms for engaging with new media.
- **Focus on impact:** Discuss how her expertise could help combat misinformation in new media spaces. Explore how she might reach and influence younger audiences who don't consume traditional news. Consider how she could help train or mentor digital creators in journalistic principles.
