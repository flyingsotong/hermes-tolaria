# KL Roundtable – Lars, Prem, Ross Settles, Roby

Wed, February 21st, 2024

- 8:30am Roundtable

 - KL Scenario Planning: What is the change we need?

- Rounding up what has been three nights here in Kuala Lumpur and just having all of this time with people like Lars Bestle, Prem, Ross Settles, Roby, and James Pacaba, I now have some different perspectives about how to think about this year ahead. I'm putting this down really so that I can talk to Rishad about this.

- Prem is moving into the deputy CEO position at MDIF, which means he would be in a position to continue to support a lot of the projects and initiatives that we've been doing over the last few years. Having an ally at that level is really quite remarkable.

- Prem suggested a Splice Southeast Asian Media Creators Award. There's an overall disappointment that WAN-IFRA and SOPA are the only award programs in the region. Room to disrupt that space.

- Strangely, no one seemed excited about Kathmandu. If anything, people felt that there was a comfort with Chiang Mai — a place they're all familiar with and know what to expect, and how to plan around, especially for those running side events for their clients and partners. No one said no to Kathmandu, but Chiang Mai seems special for folks.

- James Pacaba and Roby are using Puma to go out and acquire second-tier newspapers that are either distressed or at the end of their lives. They could pull together several newspapers from the provinces, cobble them together, reduce their costs by providing shared infrastructure around finance and bookkeeping, and then using those to start up community events in each of these provinces in the Philippines.

- Ross was saying how we need to take the initiative on doing more of these private roundtables around the region. And he thinks that it would be easy to put together something like that. Something that would run at a budget of about $20k to get, say, 10 of the most senior or interesting statespeople of media around the table a couple of times a year.

- IMS is finally moving into the space of talking about the information ecosystem and what the opportunities are in that ecosystem beyond media. They are engaging with ASEAN at a regional diplomatic level.
