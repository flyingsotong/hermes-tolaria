**Feb 12 2026 (RFTW Session 3)**
**What the coach wanted to cover**
This call had two clear “tracks”:
1.**A people decision under runway pressure**
   * The founder had hired a business development person and now suspected the hire was a miss
   * The real question was not “is it fair” but “when do I act, and how do I do it cleanly”~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
2.**A strategy reframing on monetization**
   * The founder wanted to monetize content via some form of subscription
   * The funder strongly opposed any paywall, and the founder felt stuck between mission and sustainability~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
⠀Then, as the conversation unfolded, the coach steered into a third, deeper theme:
1.**Founder time allocation and identity**
   * The founder was getting trapped in “editor mode” and needed to become the visible, relationship-driven leader of the mission
   * The coach pushed toward carving out daily strategy time and lowering perfectionism in production~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
**What he learned in the process (the founder’s learning)**
The founder articulated (and accepted) a few important learnings:
***Hiring:** trust the “energy and hunger” signal, not just credentials or written communication, especially for roles requiring independence and initiative~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
***Business model:** stop treating “content” as the only product, and separate the needs of funder vs community (two customers, two jobs-to-be-done)~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
***Leadership:** the founder’s highest leverage is “leading from the front” (relationships, presence, charisma, thought leadership), not polishing articles to 100%~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
***Time as strategy infrastructure:** if strategic time is not protected, the business stays trapped in production mode~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
**Your value here as a coach**
You created value in three ways:
***You made the decision emotionally safe, without taking it away**
  * You validated the founder’s clarity (“you are very clear this is not working”) and surfaced the real blocker (hesitation and guilt), which helped move them toward action~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
***You introduced clean frameworks at the right moments**
  * “Consultant vs coach” distinction, which forced the founder to locate what they actually wanted from you~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
  * “Two customers” reframing, which reduced moral/mission noise and turned the subscription conflict into a segmentation problem~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
***You shifted the conversation from tactics to leverage**
  * You repeatedly redirected toward founder leverage: visibility, relationships, and protected thinking time
  * You gave a practical behavioral anchor (15 minutes daily, then 30) rather than vague “be more strategic” advice~[\[1\]](https://www.notion.so/RFTW-coaching-Ankur-Paliwal-3051555a2e1e805c9ae6d4f7256e712d?pvs=21)~
**Areas you could have done better**
A few improvement opportunities, if you want to tighten your coaching craft:
***Clarify the agenda earlier**
  * The founder said “a bunch of things.” You did well to take them one by one, but you could have asked: “What is the one outcome that would make this call successful?” This would sharpen prioritization and pacing
***More explicit contracting on coach vs consultant mode**
  * You asked “as a consultant or as a coach?” which was good
  * You could then have named what would change: “If coach, I will not tell you what to do. I will help you decide.” That sets expectations and reduces “Am I being harsh?” looping
***Push for a clearer decision rule on the hire**
  * You touched on “will they be surprised?” (good)
  * You could have helped the founder define *observable pass/fail metrics* for the “selling phase” test (for example, pipeline created, number of outreach conversations, conversion milestones), so the next conversation is evidence-based rather than vibe-based
***Do a cleaner close**
  * You ended with encouragement and the habit prescription, but you could have summarized in the founder’s words:
    * “What are you committing to do about the hire?”
    * “What is the new belief about the product?”
    * “What is the first protected-time habit?”
  * That kind of recap increases follow-through and makes the coaching impact more concrete
**October 10, 2025 (RFTW Session 2)**
**Intention: What the Coachee Wanted**
Ankur came into the session with a mix of tactical, managerial, and strategic challenges he wanted to discuss:
1.**Hiring Advice:** His most immediate need was advice for an in-person meeting that night with a promising candidate for a business development role. He was concerned about how to handle the salary conversation and how to set the right expectations.
2.**Founder Frustration:** He was struggling with a common founder's dilemma: feeling frustrated that his employees weren't as driven or detail-oriented as he is. He wanted to know if it was possible to find "A+" workers or if he needed to change his perspective.
3.**Financial Model:** He was worried that his budget was unsustainable, with nearly 95% of his funds going directly into editorial content, which he knew wasn't generating revenue. He felt stuck in a cycle of creating content that required more money to sustain.
**New Awareness: What the Coachee Learned**
Ankur experienced a significant breakthrough in his thinking, realizing that his core challenge stemmed from a flawed premise. His key new awareness was:**Content is not the product; it's the cost of making the product.**
He learned to see his journalistic work not as the end goal, but as a marketing tool or a necessary ingredient used to create and deliver the _actual_ products. The true products Queerbeat offers are the outcomes and impacts the content enables:
-**The product is the conversation** a queer student can have with their parents using a Queerbeat story.
-**The product is the curriculum** that allows a teacher to educate their class.
-**The product is the feeling of being seen** and acknowledged.
-**The product is the impact** of changing the media narrative.
This shift reframed his entire business model. The 95% editorial spend is no longer a "cost center" that loses money, but a "top-of-funnel" investment required to build the valuable, monetizable products that people are actually buying. As Alan put it, "What you're selling and what they're buying, not the same thing."
**Future: A Potential Follow-up Conversation**
A follow-up session would naturally focus on how Ankur is implementing this powerful new mindset across his organization. The conversation could cover:
-**The Business Hire:** How did the conversation with the candidate go? Was Ankur able to frame the role around selling "impact" and "conversations" rather than just "content"?
-**Product Definition:** Moving from the abstract to the concrete. What steps has he taken to formally define his products? For example, what is included in the "educational resources" package, and what is its price point?
-**Content Strategy Pivot:** A crucial next step. A follow-up would explore the question: "If these are our products, what kind of content should we be creating to support them?" This could lead to a strategic shift in their editorial planning.
-**Team Alignment:** How is he communicating this new vision to his team? Helping them understand that the stories they produce are the foundation for the organization's real products could help bridge the motivational gap he was feeling.
**August 26, 2025 (RFTW session 1)**
**Intention: What the Coachee Wanted**
At the start of the meeting, Ankur wanted to figure out a clear path to**make his organization, Queerbeat, profitable**. His primary goal was to generate enough revenue so he could focus solely on his work there without needing side jobs. He came to the conversation with two specific ideas he wanted to discuss:
1. Hiring a**business development person** to help him lead revenue generation.
2. Relaunching and refining his**"Queer Beat Club" membership plan** with a suite of offerings for paying members.1
**New Awareness: What the Coachee Learned**
Ankur's main realization was that his entire business thinking was trapped in a limiting framework:**"content equals value."** He believed the stories and articles were the product, which made it hard to see how to generate revenue since people are reluctant to pay for content.
Through the conversation, his coach Alan helped him reframe his perspective. Ankur learned that Queerbeat wasn't just creating content; it was providing four distinct and valuable**services**:
1.**Identity & Communication:** Helping queer individuals feel "seen" and giving them resources (stories) to communicate their identity to family and friends.
2.**Educational Resources:** Providing schools and teachers with fresh, India-centric materials to discuss queerness.
3.**Research & Insights:** Offering data, trend analysis, and research on the queer community to organizations like NGOs and universities (e.g., the "Queer Data Lab" concept).
4.**Media Training:** Teaching newsrooms and other organizations how to report on LGBTQ+ issues accurately and sensitively.
This shift in perspective was a breakthrough. Ankur realized that while the journalism itself might not make money directly, it's the engine that powers these monetizable services. The goal isn't to sell the content, but to**use the content's impact to build and sell valuable services** to specific customer groups.
**Future: A Potential Follow-up Conversation**
A follow-up conversation would likely focus on the**execution and initial results** of this new service-oriented strategy. Key topics could include:
-**Hiring Progress:** Discussing the process of hiring the business development person, particularly how Ankur structured the incentive-based compensation that Alan suggested.
-**Service Development:** Reviewing which of the four services Ankur has prioritized. For example, have they created a package for educational resources or a proposal for the "Queer Data Lab"?
-**Website as a "Menu":** Checking on the progress of revamping the Queerbeat website to clearly showcase the four services, making it easy for potential clients to understand how they can work with and hire the organization.
-**Early Client Conversations:** Exploring any initial outreach to potential customers (schools, universities, NGOs) and the feedback they've received.
-**Testing Small Offers:** Following up on Alan's advice to "test all these little bits first" by asking if Ankur experimented with standalone paid workshops instead of committing to the resource-intensive membership club.
