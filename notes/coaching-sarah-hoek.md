# Coaching - Sarah Hoek

---

**March 12, 2025**

**What Sarah was looking to understand**

Sarah was feeling stuck and bored in her career at Daily Maverick, a South African news organization where she's worked for nearly five years since graduating university. As the deputy newsletter editor, she expressed:
- A desire for something new after falling into a monotonous routine
- Uncertainty about what comes next in her career
- A need to recapture the excitement of project-based work, where she can take on new challenges with clear start and end points
- Confusion about whether to pursue a new position at Daily Maverick (community manager) or look elsewhere
- Concern about leaving an organization whose mission (independent journalism) she deeply believes in

**What Sarah learned from the conversation**

- A new professional identity — Alan helped Sarah reframe her role from "deputy editor" (with limited growth potential) to "product person" — someone who creates valuable products for audiences that drive engagement and monetization opportunities.
- Career path flexibility — she realized there are more options beyond the traditional journalism career paths (senior reporter or editor). This new perspective opened possibilities she hadn't considered.
- Practical next steps: creating an ideal job description focused on product development, developing a gradual transition plan (6+ months) to shift responsibilities, communicating strategically with managers about her goals, and exploring product management resources and training.
- Media viability perspective — she gained insights about focusing on utility and specificity in media products rather than traditional "news" frameworks.
- Self-awareness — Sarah recognized that her greatest satisfaction came from building and launching new products (like the syndicated AI newsletter), and that this product development mindset is what truly energizes her.

**Where Alan could have improved**

- More structured follow-up plan — while Alan provided excellent insights and reframing, he could have helped Sarah create a more concrete action plan with specific timelines and milestones.
- More exploration of external options — Alan focused primarily on how Sarah could reshape her role within Daily Maverick. More time could have been spent exploring external opportunities that might align with her product management interests.
- Deeper dive into skill gaps — the conversation could have included more discussion about what specific skills Sarah might need to develop to successfully transition to a product-focused role.
- Work-life balance consideration — the conversation briefly touched on Sarah doing her master's degree simultaneously and her comment that "much of my life is already my job," but Alan didn't explore potential burnout or workload management issues.
- More guidance on handling resistance — while Alan acknowledged that managers wouldn't want Sarah to drop responsibilities, he could have provided more specific strategies for navigating potential resistance to her transition plan.

Alan's most valuable contribution was helping her see herself as a product person rather than being limited by traditional media career trajectories, opening up new possibilities for growth without abandoning her commitment to quality journalism.
