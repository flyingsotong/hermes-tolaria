Tips for Smoother Flying: Advice from Experienced Pilots (r/flying)


Based on the feedback provided to the new pilot (around 15 hours of experience), here's a comprehensive summary of tips to help you become a smoother pilot:

**Key Physical Techniques**

1.**Use a gentle touch** - Multiple pilots suggest flying with just "one or two fingers" rather than gripping the controls tightly. One even humorously suggests "touch it like you're touching a nipple."

2.**Apply pressure, not movement** - Think about applying gentle, even pressure to controls rather than making large movements. You rarely need to move the yoke more than 1/4-1/2 inch.

3.**Proper trimming is essential** - Nearly every experienced pilot emphasized proper trim. Trim the aircraft to relieve pressure from the yoke, allowing it to maintain level flight without constant input from you.

4.**Small corrections early** - Make small adjustments early rather than large corrections later. As one pilot says, "Small changes make for small, predictable results. Big changes make for large, unpredictable results."

5.**Let the plane stabilize** - Allow the aircraft to stabilize after making corrections before making additional adjustments. Avoid "chasing" the instruments.

**Mental Approach**

1.**Stay ahead of the plane** - Anticipate rather than react. Think ahead and be proactive with your control inputs.

2.**Trust the aircraft's stability** - Most aircraft are designed with positive stability and will naturally return to level flight if properly trimmed. Don't fight the plane's natural tendencies.

3.**Relax physically** - Consciously relax your arms, shoulders, hands, and wrists. Tension leads to overcontrolling the aircraft.

4.**Look outside more** - Maintain a good visual reference with the horizon, using the instruments to verify what you're seeing, not the other way around.

5.**Practice patience** - Let the plane do its thing. Don't rush your inputs or procedures.

**Specific Techniques**

1.**For cruising:** Trim properly and use the horizon as your primary reference.

2.**For climbs/descents:** When leveling off, maintain climb power until reaching cruise speed, then reduce power gradually.

3.**For turns:** Use the turn coordinator to match wings to markings, and remember to coordinate with rudder.

4.**For approaches:** Study "stabilized approach" concepts and know your power/configuration/airspeed relationships.

**Words of Encouragement**

Many experienced pilots emphasized that at 15 hours, you're still very early in your training:

1. "You're only at 15 hours, that'll come with time. Remember how badly you sucked at driving 15 hours in?"

2. "The more you fly, the more confident you'll be, the less robotic and more natural the movements will be."

3. "You're an infant in the game. A true mastery of the airplane takes hundreds of hours."

4. "Nobody is perfect, even after thousands of hours."
