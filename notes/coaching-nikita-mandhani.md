# Coaching - Nikita Mandhani

---

**May 28, 2025**

**What Nikita was initially interested in understanding**

Nikita came into the call feeling "a bit lost" and "bored of the freelance life" after about eight months. She was struggling with a lack of career direction. Specifically, she wanted:

1. Career direction — she wasn't sure what path to take next, feeling overwhelmed by various interests (video, short documentaries, making news accessible and inclusive, especially for women and younger audiences) and the tendency to say "yes" to many things.
2. Meaningful impact — she expressed a desire to "make a difference" in journalism, possibly through prestigious fellowships like Nieman or JSK, focusing on problems like reaching underserved audiences, particularly women.
3. Reconciling conflicting desires — she felt a "big jumble" between wanting to do strategic, impact-focused work (like training newsrooms post-fellowship) and her passion for "telling stories myself... making short films and being creative."
4. Sustainability of freelancing — she was also beginning to question the freelance model itself due to the need to constantly chase money, leading to burnout and a lack of time for more meaningful projects.

Essentially, she was looking for clarity on how to navigate her career, make a meaningful impact, and find a sustainable and fulfilling path, possibly involving a fellowship or a different structure than her current freelance setup.

---

**What she actually got out of it that was different**

While Nikita didn't get a definitive "you should do X fellowship" or "take Y job," she received a significant mindset shift and a concrete, entrepreneurial path forward that she hadn't explicitly considered:

1. A challenge to the "freelancer" identity — Alan strongly critiqued the "freelancer" label, suggesting it invites exploitation and lacks power. He encouraged her to think like an entrepreneur and build a brand. This resonated with Nikita.
2. A new perspective on industry trends — Alan provided a stark outlook on the journalism industry ("it is dying"), that news is already accessible via new platforms, and AI's impact. This reframed her problem from finding a place in the existing structure to adapting to a new reality.
3. A concrete niche and business idea — the pivotal shift came when they discussed her experience training journalists in Zambia to be more like content creators. Alan helped her see the opportunity not just in training journalists to be creators, but more significantly, **training content creators to be journalists**. This became a tangible potential direction.
4. An actionable entrepreneurial path — Alan guided her towards recognizing this training as a "product" or "playbook," using this playbook to pitch to organizations like BBC Media Action or create her own content to build her brand, and becoming a creator herself to demonstrate her expertise.
5. Empowerment and structure — by the end, Nikita felt she had a "structure" and a way for things to "work out," moving from feeling "lost" to having "a lot to think about and work towards."

Instead of just clarity on existing options, she gained a framework for a new, proactive, entrepreneurial approach centered on a specific market need she was uniquely positioned to fill.

---

**What Alan could have done better as her coach**

Overall, this was a highly effective coaching session. You successfully shifted Nikita's perspective and helped her identify a new, actionable path. A few potential areas for refinement:

1. Balance of talking time — especially in the latter part of the conversation, your contributions became more extended. While your insights were clearly valuable and transformative, there might have been opportunities to draw out more of the solutions from her with Socratic questioning. For instance, after identifying the "training creators" idea, you could have asked "How might you package that?" before suggesting the playbook.
2. Deeper exploration of her "creative jumble" — Nikita mentioned missing "telling stories myself... making short films and being creative." While becoming a creator to promote her training was touched upon, the call didn't fully explore how her passion for making short films could integrate into this new entrepreneurial path beyond just promotional content.
3. Pacing and checking for overwhelm — you introduced several significant, potentially confronting ideas in rapid succession (industry dying, AI's impact, the problems with freelancing, the need to become an entrepreneur). While Nikita responded positively, briefly checking in more explicitly — "That's a lot to take in — what's your immediate reaction to that?" — at various points might have been beneficial.
4. Exploring the fellowship motivation more deeply early on — Nikita mentioned fellowships as a way to "immerse myself in a problem" and "make a difference." While the conversation moved towards a more entrepreneurial solution, a slightly deeper dive into the core values driving that fellowship desire initially might have further reinforced the eventual path identified.

Despite these minor points, your coaching was impactful. You actively listened, challenged her assumptions constructively, reframed her situation, helped her identify a unique value proposition, and guided her towards concrete next steps.
