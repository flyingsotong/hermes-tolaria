# Coaching - Kanika Sharma

**February 5, 2025**

Key challenges:

- She is trying to find her unique identity and voice as a journalist, particularly as a book journalist
- Feels like an imposter in journalism despite having valuable experience and insights
- Struggling with confidence after taking a 7-8 year break for motherhood
- Financial pressures and irregular/delayed payments from Indian publications
- Questioning if she should pursue content marketing or other more lucrative paths

**Key insights and advice:**

1. Her unique perspective and interests around rituals, culture, and how people make meaning of their lives could be her differentiator, rather than seeing them as limitations.
2. She needs to reframe her view of journalism — stop seeing it just as traditional reporting; recognize her unique lens and expertise in exploring cultural stories and rituals; focus on her strength in helping people understand and navigate their lives through stories.
3. Next steps: break down learning goals into small, manageable steps (eg. 2 hours per week of focused learning); consider starting a newsletter to test ideas and build marketing skills organically; focus on creating value for a small but dedicated audience rather than trying to appeal to everyone.
4. Mindset shifts needed: stop comparing herself to established writers; recognize that her unique point of view is valuable; give herself permission to explore and experiment.

The conversation revealed that Kanika has valuable insights and capabilities but needs to build confidence in her unique perspective rather than trying to fit traditional journalism models.

Key quote: "The USP, you know, the you in there is not unique in that it has to be completely different... It just needs to be your point of view."
