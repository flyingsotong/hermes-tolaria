**September 22, 2025**

**Coachee's learnings & insights**

Erin walked away from this session with several critical shifts in her thinking:

- A fundamental reframe of the problem — she arrived thinking "What is my brand?" and left understanding the more foundational question is "What am I genuinely interested in and can build a niche around?" This shifts her from a marketing problem to a self-discovery mission
- Acceptance of industry reality — she moved past worrying about the industry's decline and started accepting it as a baseline reality; a crucial step from victim mindset ("the industry is failing me") to agent mindset ("given this reality, what will I create?")
- The concept of a "runway" — she recognized that her current contract, while frustrating, is a financial runway that gives her time and space to figure out her next move, not a trap
- The power of niche specialization — concrete examples (Ben Thompson, Grace Sha) helped her grasp that a personal brand is about becoming a known, trusted expert in a narrow, specific domain — not a clever tagline

**Coach's strengths (ICF competencies)**

- Cultivating trust and safety (ICF 4) — excellent rapport from the start; validated her feelings by normalizing her experience as a "mid-career crisis"; created an environment where she felt safe to be candid
- Active listening (ICF 6) — remembered the previous conversation about the Al Jazeera job and skillfully brought it back to highlight a pattern in her thinking
- Evoking awareness (ICF 7) — challenged her to accept the industry's decline, a significant reframe that shifted the entire direction; powerful question: "By this time next year, what would be a safe comfortable spot for you where you can look back and go... I really made the most of that one year?"
- Facilitating client growth (ICF 8) — successfully partnered with her to move focus from perpetuating her current situation to exploring a "right turn"; promoted her autonomy by putting responsibility of discovering her interests back on her

**Areas for improvement**

- Distinction between coaching and consulting (ICF 1) — a significant portion of the session was spent sharing analysis and perspective on the media industry; this positions you as the expert telling her how things are. Instead, use questions to lead her to that conclusion: "What have you observed about the freelance market over the last five years?" / "If you assume this trend won't reverse, what would that mean for you?"
- Maintaining presence and exploring emotion (ICF 5 & 6) — when Erin expressed raw frustration with "I just don't f****** know, man," you immediately moved to a solution-oriented reframe. Pause instead, acknowledge the emotion: "That sounds incredibly frustrating. What's behind that 'I don't know' for you?"
- Asking vs. telling (ICF 7) — offering ESG as a specific example of a niche borders on giving advice. Instead: "What topics do you find yourself reading about when you're not working?" / "What's a topic that's underserved or poorly covered that you feel a pull towards?"

**January 13, 2025**

Key takeaways for Erin:

1. She's experiencing career uncertainty but has more influence and value than she realizes, evidenced by Bloomberg's recruitment interest
2. She's weighing several options: Bloomberg (requiring relocation to Hong Kong), Al Jazeera (reservations about their new direction), or continuing as a freelancer
3. She seems to be realizing she should take her work more seriously and may need to develop a clearer specialization
4. Has concerns about the industry's future, particularly regarding AI and job stability
5. She acknowledges needing a bigger project but is unsure about her focus area
6. She values the entrepreneurial aspects of freelancing despite its challenges

Strengths of coaching:

1. Helped her realize that becoming a generalist editor at Al Jazeera might not be the best move for her career stage
2. Encouraged her to think about specialization and niche expertise
3. Discussed future-proofing skills (like business journalism and OSINT)
4. Maintained a supportive tone while being realistic about industry challenges

Areas for improvement:

1. More structured start — perhaps beginning with a clear assessment of her current situation and goals
2. More concrete next steps beyond attending an upcoming event
3. Explore more deeply what she enjoys most about her current work to identify potential specialization areas
4. The discussion about LinkedIn vs. Twitter could have been redirected toward more strategic career planning
5. Help her break down the "bigger project" idea into more manageable steps
6. More specific questions about what aspects of business journalism interest her, given her concerns about lacking formal business education
