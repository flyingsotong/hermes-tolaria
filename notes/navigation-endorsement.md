*Source: Claude*

The navigation endorsement is an addition to your privileges as an RPC holder. It allows you to fly beyond the 25nm radius from point of origin. Once complete you will be allowed to fly anywhere within Australia.

It isn't internationally recognised but it is recognised by CASA when completing a GA license. So if you choose to gain your RPL you can then fly in the same manner a VH registered aircraft to 1500kg MTOW within Australia's borders. **A Private Pilot License is required to fly internationally.**

The navigation training is approximately 10 flights including the 2 solo flights and flight test. Every person learns at their own pace so some people may complete more flights than this. Each navigation exercise typically takes between 2–3hrs plus ground time. The applicable Bob Tait text book should be read alongside your study. It could even be read and understood before your first lesson booking. The exam must be completed before your first solo navigation flight.

**Booking number 1** — over the course of a double booking slot (4hrs) you will be shown how to plan your navigation flight. Being familiar with how to read aviation weather is essential so you may want to refresh their RPC knowledge of how to do this. The instructor takes you step by step through the process as if you were flying on the day. Much of this plan can be transposed onto a fresh navigation plan for the day of the flight. Your instructor will ask that you plan & prepare what you can from what you have learnt today before you meet again for your flight booking.

**Navigation 1** — You and your instructor will have 2 hours together before hopping in the aircraft. The instructor will carefully take you through the planning for the day and fill in any gaps in your knowledge and your planning. This flight will be focused on the general navigation exercise of going from A to B, knowing where you are and what time you will be expected to arrive at your next location, using the flight computer (Whizzy Wheel), making the appropriate radio calls (CTAF and CTA), entering and exiting unfamiliar airfields. The flight can be overwhelming but remember so was circuits when you first began!

**Navigation 2** — This flight is focussed on the same as Nav 1 however the instructor will expect that most of the planning is done prior to the booking. Students can request an extended booking slot if they feel they need more coaching in the planning of the flight. 1 hour on ground will normally suffice. The instructor introduces the new concept of diversions including how to plan and execute. You will also submit an online flight plan to Air Services including a SARTIME. You will also be introduced to the concept of planning for an alternate.

**Navigation 3** — This navigation focusses on busy airspace and CTAF airports. You will carefully plan how to safely enter and exit these busy zones. You may also do a diversion on the way home in order to work on this skill.

**Navigation 4** — This exercise is focussed on what to do if you become lost. You will also attempt a 1 in 60 tracking exercise and a diversion. For the large part you will be away from busy airspace. You will also plan an alternate.

**Navigation 5** — This exercise acts as a pre-solo assessment. You will execute arrivals and departures from a CTAF, become lost at some stage and divert to another location once your position has been found. You'll learn how to use the GPS on your way home and you'll be expected to avoid busting controlled airspace on your way out and back from YBSU. You will also plan an alternate imagining you couldn't make it to one of your waypoints.

**Navigation 6** — Once you have passed your navigation exam, and an instructor has gone through any incorrect answers with you, you will then be able to fly your first solo nav. The route is the reverse of the first nav. Students who have a gap of more than 3 weeks since their last navigation exercise will require a short dual navigation flight before completing this solo. You are expected to use your maps while also double checking your orienteering skills on the GPS. If you become overwhelmed at any stage just use the GPS.

**Navigation 7** — This exercise is much like Nav 5 with the emphasis on improving any problem areas you have identified on your first solo nav. You will also be introduced to the lost procedure at low level (1000–1500ft AGL) which doesn't allow for the same perspective. At some point in your training, you should have had the experience of sub optimal weather conditions. Nav 7 should hopefully allow this experience if it hasn't happened yet. Most pilots don't realise just how bad the VFR minimums are in reality and it's important to see less than 10km visibility with the safety of your instructor by your side. You will also plan an alternate in your pre-flight planning.

**Navigation 8** — Second solo. This flight is the same route as Nav 2 and not in reverse.

**Navigation 9** — Pre-license. This is essentially a dress rehearsal for the license flight. You will do this with an instructor who you have not flown with for a majority of your training. They will ask you questions during the ground theory to assess your knowledge. They then fly with you but are not there to teach you anything. Only to assess. Your instructor will only take over for safety reasons in which case you may not be ready for your license just yet. You are also expected to be able to perform RPC skills such as short field, practise forced landings & precautionary search — to name a few.

**Navigation 10** — This is your flight test. You will be asked theory style questions to assess your RPC and Cross Country Endorsement knowledge. You will then head out on your navigation exercise and the chief flying instructor will not intervene unless they need to for safety reasons. Once you successfully complete this assessment your biannual flight review date will also be reset for the next 2 years.

## Plan to do a touch n' go at the underlined airports
* **NAV 1** — *normal ops, circuit entries* YBSU – YGYM – YMYB – DLP – NOSA – YBSU
* **NAV 2** — *diversions, circuit entries, flight plan submission* YBSU – YBIN – YKRY – YNA – YBSU
* **NAV 3** — *normal ops, busy airspace, circuit entries, CTA avoidance, GPS use* YBSU – YCDR – YCAB – YNA – YBSU
* **NAV 4** — *Lost & diversions, 1 in 60s, airspace avoidance, alternates* YBSU – YKRY – YGAS – YBSU
* **NAV 5** — *Lost & diversions, 1 in 60, GPS use, airspace avoidance* YBSU – YNA – CORO – YWND – YWSG – YBSU
* **NAV 6** — solo NAV 1 in reverse (CX Exam must be complete and KDRs completed)
* **NAV 7** — *Lost & diversions, 1 in 60, GPS use, airspace avoidance, alternates* YBSU – YGAY – YHBA – YBSU
* **Nav 8** — Solo YBSU – YBIN – YKRY – YNA – YBSU
* **NAV 9** — Pre-license
* **NAV 10** — License test
