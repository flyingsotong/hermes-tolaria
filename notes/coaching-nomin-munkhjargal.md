# Coaching - Nomin Munkhjargal

**Friday, August 16, 2024**

- 1:00pm met with Nomin Munkhjargal for introductory coaching
  - This was my first coaching call in a very long time and it's with someone that I don't actually know. So it gave me a chance to start this muscle again. Within about five minutes of the conversation she started breaking down. There's a lot of trauma in her past and perhaps this is the first time where I felt uncomfortable as a coach versus what a counselor would have done. But I think we managed to pull through this one. I come out of this feeling really fulfilled and it's a nice reminder again to myself why I started coaching to begin with and how much joy it gives me.

Based on the coaching conversation, here are the key insights and learnings that emerged:

1. Self-discovery and identity: Nomin learned that she has been suppressing a large part of her identity and creativity, especially since moving back to Mongolia. She realized she has a strong desire to create something of her own and express herself authentically.
2. Permission and self-doubt: Nomin recognized that she's been waiting for permission from others to pursue her ideas and passions. She learned that she needs to give herself permission to create and pursue her goals, rather than seeking external validation.
3. The podcast project: Nomin gained clarity on why she wants to create a podcast — it's primarily for herself as a form of self-expression and connection. She learned that the success of the podcast doesn't have to be measured by external metrics, but by how it fulfills her personal needs.
4. Dealing with past trauma: Nomin gained insight into how her relationship with her father and past experiences have influenced her self-doubt and tendency to over-analyze ideas. She learned the importance of acknowledging these influences while not letting them hold her back.
5. Self-perception vs. others' perceptions: Nomin discovered that her self-image differs significantly from how others perceive her (as funny, creative, and capable). She learned the value of considering positive feedback from others to build a more balanced self-perception.
6. Embracing vulnerability: Nomin realized that her fear of being vulnerable has been holding her back, especially in Mongolia. She learned that embracing vulnerability could be key to connecting with others and expressing herself authentically.
7. The value of creative expression: Nomin gained a deeper understanding of why creative projects like the podcast are important for her personal growth and well-being. She learned that creative pursuits don't always need to have a grand purpose or outcome to be worthwhile.
8. Overcoming perfectionism: Nomin realized that her tendency to over-analyze and dissect ideas (learned from her father) has been preventing her from taking action. She learned the importance of starting projects without expecting perfection.
9. Cultural identity and belonging: Nomin gained insight into how her multicultural background has shaped her experiences and struggles with belonging. She learned that her unique perspective could be valuable to others with similar experiences.
10. Self-compassion and acceptance: Nomin learned the importance of being kinder to herself and accepting her desires and needs. She gained permission to pursue her interests without feeling guilty or ashamed.

Alan did a good job guiding the conversation and uncovering many insights, but here are some additional questions he could have asked to add more depth:

1. "What does success look like for you beyond the podcast? How does this project fit into your larger life goals?"
2. "If you were to fully embrace your authentic self in Mongolia, what would that look like? What's stopping you from doing that now?"
3. "How has your multicultural background shaped your strengths? What unique perspectives do you bring because of your experiences?"
4. "What would your ideal relationship with your father look like? What small steps could you take towards that?"
5. "If fear wasn't a factor, what would you do differently in your life right now?"
6. "How can you create a support system that encourages your creativity and authenticity?"
7. "What are some past successes or accomplishments that you're proud of? How can you apply those strengths to your current situation?"
8. "How do you define 'being useful'? Are there ways you're already being useful that you might be overlooking?"
9. "What would it look like to fully accept and embrace all parts of your identity — Mongolian, American, global citizen?"
10. "If you were to mentor someone with a similar background to yours, what advice would you give them?"

These questions could have potentially added more layers to the conversation, helping Nomin gain even deeper insights into her situation, goals, and potential paths forward.
