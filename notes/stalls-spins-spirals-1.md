# Stalls, Spins & Spirals 1 (RAAus Practice Exam)

**Source:** Pilot Practice Exams - RAAus BAK
**URL:** https://www.pilotpracticeexams.com/exams/free/raa-stalls-spins-spirals-pilot-practice-exam-1/
**Type:** Multiple choice, 12 questions
**License:** Free practice exam

---

1. **An identifying factor to help you determine a spin from a spiral is:** **airspeed is low in a spin and high in a spiral** ✓ / airspeed is high in a spin and low in a spiral / spins are upside down and spirals are right wayup / none of these

2. **A stall can occur during any manoeuvre where the angle of attack increases too far.** False / **True** ✓

3. **If an aircraft is flown lower than MTOW, the stall speed will be:** higher / **lower** ✓ / the same / none of these

4. **During a stall, the centre of pressure:** **moves forward** ✓ / none of these / moves aft / stays the same

5. **Above the stall angle of attack, lift and drag do the following:** Lift increases and drag increases / Lift increases and drag decreases / Lift decreases and drag decreases / **Lift decreases and drag increases** ✓

6. **Note you should refer to the POH for each aircraft. A typical spin recovery is usually achieved using:** **throttle closed ailerons neutral, and opposite rudder and neutral or slightly forward elevator** ✓ / throttle wide open ailerons neutral / throttle closed ailerons opposite and opposite rudder / none of these

7. **During a stall, the control stick has a position called the _________.** stalled stick elevator / back stick position / **stalled stick position** ✓ / stalled stick limit

8. **An aircraft is said to be stalled when:** the aircraft drops from the sky / **smooth air separates from the top of the wing and becomes turbulent** ✓ / none of these / the plane balloons upward and slows dramatically

9. **Using flaps _____________ the stall speed of an aircraft.** is not affected / increases / **decreases** ✓

10. **Always refer to the POH. Spiral recovery usually involves:** none of these / opening the throttle and pull out, but not suddenly / **closing the throttle, stop the roll and pull out, but not suddenly** ✓ / closing the throttle, stop the roll and pull out suddenly

11. **During a stall, the upper aerofoil is __________.** effectively smoothed / none of these / **effectively roughened** ✓

12. **The main factor causing a stall is:** weight / centre of pressure / **angle of attack** ✓ / centre of gravity
