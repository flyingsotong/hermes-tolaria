**Key Differences**
- **METAR:** Observation of current conditions, hourly — unless SPECI for significant changes
- **TAF:** Forecast of future conditions, usually 4x a day
**METAR (Meteorological Aerodrome Report)**
- **What it is:**
 - A METAR is a real-time observation of the current weather conditions at an airport or weather station. It provides a snapshot of what is happening right now.
- **Purpose:**
 - It's used to give pilots and other aviation professionals up-to-the-minute information about the actual weather conditions they will encounter.
- **Update frequency:**
 - Typically, METARs are issued hourly.
 - However, special reports (SPECI) are generated whenever significant changes in weather occur between those hourly observations. This means that if the weather is changing rapidly, there could be multiple updates within an hour.
**TAF (Terminal Aerodrome Forecast)**
- **What it is:**
 - A TAF is a forecast of the expected weather conditions within a five-statute-mile radius of an airport. It predicts what the weather will be like over a future period.
- **Purpose:**
 - It's used for flight planning, allowing pilots to anticipate weather conditions at their destination or along their route.
- **Update frequency:**
 - TAFs are typically issued four times a day, usually valid for a 24 or 30-hour period. The standard issue times are 00Z, 06Z, 12Z, and 18Z.
