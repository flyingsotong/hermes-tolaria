**April 1, 2025**

**What Shadma wanted to achieve**

At the start of the call, Shadma was exploring how to adapt her journalism career in the face of changing media consumption patterns. Her main concerns:

1. Finding effective formats beyond long-form journalism, which she excels at but feels is becoming less engaging
2. Understanding how to create value when "information is so cheap" and readily available
3. Addressing the challenge that her paywalled content faces in attracting readers
4. Preventing others from repackaging her in-depth work into more viral formats before she can
5. Finding ways to maintain audience engagement that she noticed had decreased since returning from maternity leave

She had recently started experimenting with video content on Instagram but felt she was in a discovery phase, trying to understand what would work best.

---

**What Shadma discovered**

1. She had been focusing primarily on format (long-form articles vs. videos) without clearly defining her target audience or their needs
2. She needed to clarify her "why" before determining her audience and then choosing appropriate formats
3. The value of thinking about specific individuals (making a list of 10 people) whose engagement would excite her, rather than chasing abstract metrics
4. LinkedIn might be more strategic than Instagram for her professional goals, as it allows her to see exactly who is engaging with her content
5. She should focus on the utility and value her content provides rather than just formats

---

**What Alan missed as a coach**

1. Not exploring Shadma's specific expertise deeply enough — he mentioned domain expertise but didn't dig into what exact topics or niches she covers that might differentiate her work
2. Limited follow-up on her early career insights — she mentioned her experience covering Chinese apps in India and how geopolitics affected her beat, but this potentially valuable perspective wasn't explored further
3. Not addressing the work-life balance aspect — she mentioned having a young son and taking a break, but Alan didn't explore how this might affect her content strategy or time constraints
4. Limited practical next steps — while he gave conceptual guidance, more specific action items beyond "make a list of 10 people" would have been helpful
5. Not acknowledging her current successes — could have explored what's working well for her currently at Mint to build on those strengths
6. Not exploring collaborative opportunities — she mentioned trying to bring freelance journalists together, but this community-building aspect wasn't explored as a potential differentiator or growth avenue

Alan guided Shadma to a crucial insight about focusing on audience and value rather than format, but could have provided more personalized guidance based on her specific expertise and circumstances.
