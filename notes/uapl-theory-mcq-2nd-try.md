| Topic | Key Concepts to Review | Action Plan |
|**Stalls** |**Critical Angle of Attack (AoA):** A stall occurs when the AoA is**exceeded**, causing airflow to separate from the**top** (upper surface) of the wing, leading to a sudden loss of lift. | Watch a short video on the "Angle of Attack" and "How a Wing Stalls." |
|**Four Forces & Steady Flight** | In**straight and level, unaccelerated flight** (steady flight), the opposing forces must be**equal**:**Thrust = Drag** and**Lift = Weight**. To increase speed, you must first increase**thrust** so it is momentarily _greater_ than drag, accelerating the aircraft until a new, higher speed is reached where the forces are equal again. | Draw the four forces and label the condition for unaccelerated flight and acceleration. |
|**Drag** |**Induced Drag** (drag created as a by-product of lift) is highest at**low speeds and high AoA** because the wing needs to be at a higher AoA to generate enough lift at low airspeeds. | Review the relationship between the two main types of drag (Induced and Parasite) and airspeed. |
|**Density Altitude** |**High Density Altitude** (hot, high, humid) means less dense air, which**reduces performance** (less lift, less engine power). Therefore,**low density altitude** (cool, low, dry) means high-density air, which**increases performance** (more lift). If an area is "high density," it simply means the air is dense, leading to _more_ lift. | Use the mnemonic:**"High Density Altitude is Bad Performance."** |
