# Building your media startup

Talk to smart(er) people about your plan. Ask: What are the risks to failure? What do I need to do to de-risk it?

There is no such thing as "launch day". Don't think of it as getting the product out to your user -- think about this as the day you get feedback coming in.

Don't wait for perfect. Perfect costs time, money, energy. Good enough is good enough to get feedback.

Be married to the problem. Not the tech. The latter changes. And is a pain to build if you're not from tech. Deeply understand the problem because you'll spend the next few years wrestling with it.

Momentum is oxygen. If you lose it, fear sets in.

But if you have fear, mitigate by talking to your users. If you're uncertain, get that prototype out there.

Find out how to make 10 people happy. Then 100.

The best predictor of success: how often you talk to users. Figure out how to operationalize that early so it's in your DNA. Do things daily. Do things weekly. Do things monthly.

If you're the founder, 80% of your head needs to be on "listening" mode. The rest is acting on what you've heard.
