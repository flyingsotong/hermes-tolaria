# Meeting Nic Hopkins – Google Support and Bryan Goh Coaching

Tue, April 2nd, 2024

- The days are long, but the years are short. Especially with kids.

- 2:00pm met with Nic Hopkins

 - Big regional budgets are up in the air

 - Google has decided it was no longer useful to stay tool agnostic when people are in fact using their tools and want to use those better

 - Doesn't know if there's a clear budget for Beta this year (Faisal's budget) but will look

 - In the meantime, wants a menu of workshops/training/coaching that we can do

 - Sympathetic — asked if we would be economically in trouble without Google's support this year

- 3:15pm met with Bryan Goh, Adrian Lim for Group Mentor Coaching Session 2

 - We started off with Adrian coaching me in this session. I was slightly unsure whether I wanted to talk about how I feel about Splice, the lingering question of what an end to my time at Splice would look like. That's been on my mind for a very long time now, and I think increasingly so. What was really useful from this session with Adrian was that he got me to articulate exactly what I was most afraid of about this question.

 - There are two things that have come up. One was, how do I explain all this to Rishad? How do I handle this breakup with him in a way that isn't too disruptive for him? That's one aspect. The good thing about that problem is that it's something I can act on.

 - But the real, bigger, deeper problem that I've thought about, or maybe articulated for the first time, is that I am truly worried that this could be the end of my productive years — or that this could be me pulling back. I'm concerned about losing my motivation and excitement, or the drive to do something out of sheer passion and love.**There's a side of me that worries/fears that by closing the door on Splice, it would be an acknowledgment that those days of motivation and energy are over.**

 -**And with that, the real fear is that here I am in my 50s and beyond, not being excited about anything and not finding meaning in anything else. That's the true fear.** I'm glad that Adrian was able to bring it out.

 - The session was also useful because it allowed me to reflect on Adrian's coaching technique. There were several good aspects I observed.**One was his deliberate pauses when asking questions.** This had the interesting effect of giving me permission to slow down my responses, which led to a more expressive way of communicating that didn't just repeat what I've discussed with other coaches.**I appreciated this method of slowing down the questions.**

 - Adrian was also adept at asking deeper questions by using my own words and exploring them with me. For instance, I frequently used the word 'worry,' and he was quick to pick up on that and delve deeper. To improve, he could have equated 'worry' with 'fear,' especially since I often used 'worry' when I actually meant 'fear.' This would have enhanced our conversation if I had introduced the term 'fear.'

 - Additionally, I liked that he allowed me to turn off the camera, which made me more comfortable and thus more open in our discussion. With the camera off, he had to rely solely on his hearing, and he noticed when I sighed. He seized the opportunity to ask about my feelings at that moment, which I found quite insightful. The outcome was that my comments following the sigh were a reflection of my acceptance of my feelings, allowing for that. It was a good catch on his part.
