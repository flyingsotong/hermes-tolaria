Mon, May 6th, 2024

- I got to spend Saturday with some of the smartest, curious and energetic journalism students from Hong Kong at the Foreign Correspondents Club. I was on a panel with Jing Yang and Aaron B — masterfully moderated by Joe Pan — about alternative careers in media.

If you're in your twenties, you owe it to yourself to take a little risk and try a path that doesn't involve a traditional route to joining a big legacy newsroom — those jobs are few and these companies continue to struggle with their business models. And that means fewer opportunities, less growth, and a higher chance of you eventually getting the axe.

As I was telling the young journalists in the room, make the most of your youth — you have the elasticity of cost — you can make changes to your consumption habits, lower your costs, and take bigger bets on trying different paths as a media startup founder or a media creator. But try. Spend a year building something and see if that works for you.

It's harsh, but no one owes you a career in journalism just because you're a journalist. If you have time on your side, make the most of it.
