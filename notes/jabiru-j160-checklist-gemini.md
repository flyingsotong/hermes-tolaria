# Jabiru J160 Checklist (Gemini)

Via Gemini. This information is for general educational and informational purposes only.**It is not a substitute for the official Pilot's Operating Handbook (POH) for the specific aircraft you are flying.** Procedures can vary based on the aircraft's serial number, installed equipment (e.g., choke vs. primer), and local operating conditions.
ALWAYS refer to the approved POH and your flight school's or organization's specific checklists before operating any aircraft.*
The startup procedure can be broken down into four distinct phases:
1.**Pre-Flight Inspection (Walk-Around)**
2.**Cockpit Pre-Start Checks (Internal)**
3.**Engine Start**
4.**After Start Checks & Warm-Up*
**Phase 1: Pre-Flight Inspection (Walk-Around)**
Before you even get in the cockpit, a thorough walk-around is essential. While the full walk-around is detailed in the POH, key check items include:
-**Documentation:** Ensure AROW (Airworthiness, Registration, Operating Handbook, Weight & Balance) are on board.
-**Cockpit:** Master switch OFF, ignition keys removed.
-**Fuselage:** Check for skin damage, loose rivets, or fluid leaks.
-**Empennage (Tail):** Check rudder and elevator for freedom of movement and security.
-**Wings:** Check ailerons and flaps for freedom of movement. Inspect the wing surfaces for damage.
-**Fuel:**
 - Visually check the fuel level in each tank.
 - Drain a sample from each wing tank sump and the main gascolator sump (usually under the fuselage). Check for water and contaminants.
-**Landing Gear:** Check tires for proper inflation, brakes for leaks, and fairings for security.
-**Engine & Propeller:**
 - Open the cowl inspection hatches. Check oil level with the dipstick (aim for the upper mark).
 - Check for any fluid leaks (oil or fuel).
 - Inspect the propeller for nicks, cracks, and security.
 - Ensure the air intake is clear.
 - Securely fasten the cowlings.
**Phase 2: Cockpit Pre-Start Checks (Internal)**
Once you are seated and comfortable in the cockpit:
1.**Parking Brake**.....................**SET**
2.**Harnesses & Seatbelts**.......**SECURE**
3.**Circuit Breakers**..................**ALL IN**
4.**Avionics Master Switch**......**OFF**
5.**Fuel Selector**.......................**ON** (select the fullest tank)
6.**Mixture Control**...................**IDLE CUT-OFF** (Full Lean)
7.**Carburetor Heat**.................**COLD** (Off)
8.**Throttle**................................**CLOSED** (Idle)
9.**Flight Controls**....................**CHECK** (Full and free movement)
**Phase 3: Engine Start**
This procedure can change depending on whether the engine is cold, warm, or flooded.
**A) Standard Cold Start**
This is for the first flight of the day.
1.**Master Switch**.....................**ON**
2.**Fuel Pump**...........................**ON** (You should hear it running. Check for fuel pressure if a gauge is installed). After a few seconds, turn**OFF**. This primes the carburetor bowl.
3.**Choke/Primer**....................**APPLY**
-**If Choke:** Pull the choke lever to the full ON position.
-**If Primer:** Pump 2-3 times. (Do not over-prime).
1.**Throttle**................................**CRACKED OPEN** (About 1/4 inch or 1 cm)
2.**Mixture Control**...................**RICH** (Full Forward)
3.**Area Clear**...........................**VERIFY CLEAR**, then shout**"CLEAR PROP!"**
4.**Ignition Key**.........................**TURN TO START**
5.**As Engine Fires:**
- Release the key immediately.
- Slowly push the**Choke** to the**OFF** position as the engine smooths out.
-**CRITICALLY, CHECK OIL PRESSURE.** It must be rising into the green arc within 10-15 seconds. If not,**SHUT DOWN THE ENGINE IMMEDIATELY** to prevent damage.
**B) Warm Start**
If the engine has been run in the last hour.
1. Follow steps 1-2 and 4-8 from the cold start procedure.
2.**DO NOT USE CHOKE OR PRIMER.** A warm engine does not need the extra fuel and is likely to flood if you use it.
**C) Flooded Start**
If you smell a strong odor of fuel or the engine fails to start after a few attempts.
1.**Mixture Control**...................**IDLE CUT-OFF** (Full Lean)
2.**Throttle**................................**FULL OPEN**
3.**Choke/Primer**....................**OFF / IN**
4.**Area Clear**...........................**VERIFY CLEAR**, shout**"CLEAR PROP!"**
5.**Ignition Key**.........................**TURN TO START** and crank the engine. This will pump air through the cylinders to clear the excess fuel.
6.**As Engine Sputters to Life:** Simultaneously and smoothly, bring the**Throttle to IDLE** and the**Mixture to RICH**.
**Phase 4: After Start Checks & Warm-Up**
Once the engine is running stably:
1.**Throttle**................................**ADJUST** to 1000-1200 RPM for warm-up.
2.**Oil Pressure**.......................**VERIFY IN GREEN**
3.**Ammeter/Voltmeter**...........**CHECK** for positive charge, indicating the alternator is working.
4.**Avionics Master Switch**......**ON**
5.**Radios / Transponder**........**SET** for taxi and departure.
6.**Engine Warm-Up:** Monitor the engine instruments. Do not begin your engine run-up or take-off until the**Cylinder Head Temperature (CHT)** and**Oil Temperature** are in the green arc or have reached the minimum temperature specified in the POH. This is crucial for the health of the air-cooled Jabiru engine.
Once the engine is warm, you can proceed with the taxi and pre-takeoff run-up checks as per the POH.
**Common Jabiru Tips**
-**The "Jabiru Rattle":** The Jabiru 2200 engine has a distinctive rattle at very low RPMs due to its gearbox design. This is normal and usually smooths out above 1000 RPM.
-**Carburetor Icing:** The J160 is susceptible to carb icing. Be vigilant in conditions of high humidity and temperatures below 20C (70F). Know how and when to use your carb heat.
-**Engine Temps:** These are air-cooled engines. Avoid rapid temperature changes. Allow for a proper warm-up before applying high power and a cool-down period at low RPM before shutdown to prevent shock cooling.
