Session title: To survive, It doesn't matter if journalism needs a broader definition. Here's how journalists can work with creators — and drag queens.

For the past half-century, our working definition of journalism has been an American one — one that holds power to account as it serves the public's interest. We've created institutions, newsrooms, job titles, products along these lines.

We don't think that's enough.

Viable media is user-focused, demand-driven, and interest-based. It doesn't matter whether journalism serves the public interest if audiences ultimately detach from it. The label "news avoidance" obscures an important point: The content and products of newsrooms — stuff we call journalism and news — aren't working in a time when content consumption is defined by its users.

We need a broader definition of journalism if we want to reach audiences in new ways.

At Splice Beta 2023, we featured Ryan Pronstoller, a creator from the Philippines who's best known for his drag queen persona, Your Tita Baby. Ryan works alongside Probe, a storied investigative journalism archive accredited by IFCN, to counter disinformation in the Philippine information space.

"There is a lack or a gap in truth storytellers telling the truth. If people ask me, why do you do it in drag? My drag is just an art. If Picasso's medium was painting, Mariah Carey's medium is music. To tell stories, the way I tell my story is through my medium, which is drag," Ryan told the crowd.

We would love to put together a panel discussion with Ryan anPronstoller and Jen Aquino (who runs Probe) at IJF24 about exploring new approaches toward working with creators to reach different audiences. The conversation would be moderated by Rishad Patel and Alan Soon from Splice.
