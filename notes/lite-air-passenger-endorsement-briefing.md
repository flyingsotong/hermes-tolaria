How we manage and treat passengers is a vital skill for ALL pilots. The experience you provide will ensure a great outcome for the passengers' flight and display excellent "pilotage" on behalf of the aviation industry.

1. Discuss the privileges that are yours to use with a passenger
2. Ensure you have at least 10 hours solo PIC time before applying for the endorsement test.
3. Use the IMSAFE acronym for both passenger and pilot — (I)llness, (M)edication, (S)tress, (A)lcohol, (F)atigue, (E)ating (and hydration)
4. Weather — good idea to test the air before taking passenger (calm conditions)
5. Pre‑flight inspection — do not allow passengers to be part of the aircraft pre‑flight inspection. Distractions.
6. Any questions and an approximate plan of the flight. Also ask the pre‑boarding toilet question.
7. Assist to enter and exit aircraft; indicate seatbelt use, door access and hatch lock.
8. Feet to remain off pedals and never touch controls (PIC only). No loose objects in the front of the aircraft — all stowed behind seats including water bottles.
9. Do the pre‑take‑off safety brief mentally; doing so out loud can cause unnecessary anxiety with the passenger.
10. Sick bag access and discuss not feeling well — look for symptoms of pending sickness.
11. Radio operations vs intercom.
12. Sterile cockpit for take‑off and from downwind.
13. Passenger to keep a lookout for any aircraft — use the clock method to identify possible threat.
14. Do a weight and balance check on PC before flight and don't trust passenger estimates.
15. Questions — speak up.
