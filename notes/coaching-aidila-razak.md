**January 15, 2025**

---

**Background**

Aidila is a senior journalist at Malaysia Kini, leading their investigative/special reports team (News Lab). Key context:

- 40 years old, concerned about industry transitions
- Has a 3-year-old child and is pursuing IVF
- Feels stuck with limited growth opportunities and can't relocate due to family commitments
- Leads a small, pioneering team of 3
- Struggles with founder's syndrome despite being an employee
- Deals with constant survival mode operations and grant applications (which she doesn't enjoy)

---

**What she gained from the session**

1. Space to articulate and process her thoughts
2. Recognition of her progress in being able to interview for other positions
3. Permission to prioritize her needs over organizational loyalty
4. Perspective that succession planning is the organization's responsibility, not hers
5. Acknowledgment of her conflicts about family vs. career

---

**Coaching improvement areas**

- Use more direct coaching questions to help her explore options
- Challenge some of her assumptions about career penalties
- Help create a more structured action plan
- Explore her definition of "growth" and what it means to her beyond positional advancement
- Dig deeper into her fear of becoming a "statistic" and how that's influencing decisions
- Help her map out concrete next steps or a decision-making framework

The session was therapeutic in allowing her to voice concerns, but could have been more structured toward actionable outcomes.

---

**Effective questions from this session**

- "Do I really want to be on the cutting edge?" — prompted deep self-reflection about her true career motivations vs. what she feels she "should" want
- Questions about motivations for leaving — "Why am I so desperate to leave?" — helped her examine her true reasons beyond surface-level frustrations, leading to revelations about ownership stakes and emotional investment
- "What I need to figure out is whether my expectations are realistic" — helped her step back and look at her situation more objectively

**Questions that could have deepened the conversation**

- "What would 'growth' look like for you if location wasn't a constraint?"
- "How do you define success in your career at this stage of your life?"
- "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable stepping back?"
- "What assumptions are you making about taking time for family?"
- "How might your perspective on growth change if you weren't comparing yourself to others in the industry?"
