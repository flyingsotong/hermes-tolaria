# Definitions Parasitic vs Induced Drag

**Parasitic Drag (Increases with airspeed)**
1. **Form Drag**
    - Caused by the aircraft's shape disrupting airflow
    - Affected by streamlining and frontal area
    - Examples: Landing gear, struts, antennas
    - Can be reduced through aerodynamic design
2. **Skin Friction Drag**
    - Caused by air molecules interacting with aircraft surface
    - Affected by surface smoothness and area
    - Increases with wetted area
    - Reduced by smooth surfaces and polishing
3. **Interference Drag**
    - Created where different aircraft parts meet
    - Common at wing/fuselage junction
    - Can be reduced with fairings
    - Significant in areas of turbulent airflow

**Induced Drag (Decreases with airspeed)**
- Created as byproduct of lift
- Caused by wingtip vortices
- Highest at low speeds/high angles of attack
- Factors affecting it:
    - Weight
    - Wing design (aspect ratio)
    - Air density
    - Lift coefficient
    - Can be reduced with winglets

**Total Drag Relationship**
- Total Drag = Parasitic Drag + Induced Drag
- Each type dominates at different speeds:
    - Low speed: Induced drag is primary
    - High speed: Parasitic drag is primary
- Point where they're equal is roughly at best Lift-Drag speed

**Understanding these helps pilots**
- Choose efficient cruise speeds
- Maximize aircraft performance
- Plan fuel consumption
- Configure aircraft for different phases of flight
