# Coaching - Anubha Bhonsle

---

**January 20, 2025**

Anubha primarily wanted to discuss her deep internal conflict about her media business, Newsworthy Studios:

1. Her studio business (client/revenue side) is doing well, but she feels conflicted about her news product (Instagram news content with 60–64k followers) which isn't growing as she'd like and is actually costing her money.
2. She's considering shutting down the news side of the business — something she hadn't voiced externally before this conversation. Her accountant suggested this would make her more profitable.
3. She's struggling with her identity transition from being a high-profile CNN journalist to her current role as a media entrepreneur. While she's succeeded in building a profitable business, she feels she may have diluted her "brand value" and journalistic identity.
4. She's questioning whether her news product on Instagram is truly adding value or if she's "just an aggregator."
5. She wants to understand why, after 5 years of running this business successfully, she's feeling this particular type of dissatisfaction now — especially since she previously thought she had "unlearned" her attachment to traditional media roles.

The underlying theme was really about reconciling her past identity as a traditional journalist with her current role as a media entrepreneur. **This culminated in her important realization that she might be confusing influence with purpose.**

**Key learnings for Anubha**

1. She discovered that she's conflating influence with purpose — a crucial insight that helps explain her dissatisfaction despite having built a successful business.
2. She realized she has two seemingly contradictory views of her business: the studio side (revenue-generating, client work) which is doing well, and the news side (Instagram, editorial) which she feels isn't meeting her standard.
3. She recognized that her definition of "news" is still heavily influenced by her traditional media background, which may be limiting her ability to see value in her current work.
4. She learned that her dissatisfaction isn't necessarily about growth metrics but about how she views herself and her work's impact.
5. She gained awareness that she had previously unlearned some of these traditional media mindsets but they're resurfacing now.

**Areas where the coaching could have been improved**
- Could have explored more concrete examples of what "influence" means to her versus what "purpose" means — this distinction emerged late in the conversation.
- Might have spent more time unpacking why the documentary work felt so fulfilling — there seemed to be important insights there that weren't fully explored.
- Could have helped her articulate more specific next steps beyond general reflection.
