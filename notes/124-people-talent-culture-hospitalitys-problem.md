# 124 - People, Talent & Culture - Hospitality's problem

[Read on Substack](https://martinsoler.substack.com/p/124-people-talent-and-culture-hospitalitys)

**Author:** Martin Soler -**Published:** 2025-09-25


**Summary**

This newsletter explores the top concerns of hospitality leaders, including people, talent, culture, robotics, automation, B2B marketing, luxury hotel investment, major events, loyalty programs, AI, and industry challenges. The author emphasizes the importance of technology that enhances human connection rather than replacing it.

**Key Sections**

**Robotics and Automation and Labor**

Labor and HR costs are a major problem for hotels. Robots may help budget hotels, while automation supports staff in higher-end hotels. The best tech is invisible, helping staff focus on guests.

**B2B Marketing and Brand**

B2B marketing is often lead-focused, but strong brand assets are underutilized. Building a distinctive brand is as important as driving leads.

**Luxury Boutique Hotels and Capital**

Luxury boutique hotels attract capital from private investors, fostering creativity and new concepts in the industry.

**Major Events and Revenue Management**

Major events disrupt standard revenue management. Gut feeling, FOMO, and ego can become liabilities during these times.

**Marriott’s “Member’s Day”**

Loyalty programs should focus on paid memberships to change dynamics. Giving loyal members a preview of new ideas is valuable, but most join for status and rewards.

**AI for Changement**

AI can redesign workflows and flag risks, acting as an enabler for smoother hotel operations.

**Understanding the Fees in Travel**

Opaque costs and policies from intermediaries frustrate customers. Transparency is needed in travel payments and booking flows.

**Top 10 Hotel Industry Concerns - Fall 2025**

1. People, Talent & Culture: Hard to find/keep staff; need better morale and training.

2. Guest Experience & Service: Guests expect personalized, memorable stays.

3. Operational Costs & Efficiency: Rising costs require automation and control.

4. Technology & Digital Systems: Modernize systems to improve service/productivity.

5. Growth & Market Positioning: Strategic expansion and differentiation.

6. Distribution & OTA Dependence: Reduce OTA reliance, boost direct bookings.

7. Owner/Franchisee Value & Alignment: Keep owners profitable and engaged.

8. Sustainability & Climate Change: Meet ESG targets and appeal to eco-guests.

9. Financing & Development Capital: High costs make funding tough.

10. Macroeconomic & Geopolitical Volatility: Prepare for instability and crises.

> Here is a radical thought: hotels should adopt a single north star metric for tech. Every hour a staff member spends looking at a screen instead of making eye contact with a guest is money lost. That may sound unscientific (it is), but think about it. Hospitality thrives on human connection. The moment technology pulls employees away from guests, it is working against its purpose. The best hotel tech is invisible, running in the background, removing friction, freeing staff to do what no AI can: make guests feel welcome.
