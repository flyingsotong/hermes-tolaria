Carburetor heat is needed during descent (and other low power settings) because the engine isn't producing as much heat, and the venturi effect in the carburetor can cause significant cooling. This cooling can drop the temperature enough to cause ice to form if the conditions are right (high humidity). During climb, the engine is at a higher power setting, producing more heat, making icing less likely.
