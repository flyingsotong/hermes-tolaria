# Coaching - Caecilia Mediana

---

**January 17, 2025**

**Key learnings for Cecilia**

1. She realized she has already achieved her teenage dream of working at Kompas for over 10 years — it's okay to move on and not feel guilty about it.
2. She understood that her industry (media) is shrinking and staying at Kompas hoping for growth opportunities may be futile.
3. She recognized that her worth isn't determined by her editor's opinion of her.
4. She learned that short-term contract roles could be valuable opportunities if they offer better pay and working conditions.
5. She gained perspective that her parents' approval and comparisons to her siblings shouldn't dictate her career choices.
6. She understood that the "bad economy" shouldn't be a barrier to making positive changes in her life.
7. She recognized her own strength in reaching out for help, showing she has more confidence than she gives herself credit for.

**Areas where you could have provided additional support**

1. Earlier exploration of mental health — given her mentions of burnout and depression (2018–2021), you could have gently explored whether she has access to mental health support or counseling.
2. More concrete action planning — while you encouraged her to reach out to Tech in Asia and other companies, you could have helped her develop a more structured action plan with specific next steps.
3. Skill development discussion — though she mentioned improving her English and Japanese, you could have explored more specific skill development opportunities that would make her more marketable.
4. Network building — you could have discussed strategies for building professional networks outside of Kompas.
5. Financial planning — given her concerns about stability, you could have briefly discussed financial planning or saving strategies to help her feel more secure about making a transition.

You did many things exceptionally well: showed genuine empathy and created a safe space for her to be vulnerable, validated her feelings while challenging limiting beliefs, provided perspective from your own experience in the industry, maintained a good balance between pushing her to consider changes while being supportive, and helped her reframe her "failure" at Kompas as a successful achievement of her original goal.
