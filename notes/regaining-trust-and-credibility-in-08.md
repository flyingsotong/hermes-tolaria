Making the Press, Politics and PR Personal Again By Stephen M.R. Covey, CEO, CoveyLink Worldwide; Author, "The Speed of Trust"

Created March 27, 2009

1.**Integrity**. This is what most people think about when they think of trust. How can you (not to mention, members of the media or politicians) show integrity? The answer is to "Talk Straight." This is honesty in action. Be honest. Tell the truth. Use simple language, and call things what they are. Demonstrate integrity. Don't manipulate people or distort the facts. Don't spin the truth or leave false impressions.

2.**Intent**. This refers our motive, our agenda. If people do not trust our motive, they will not trust us. Motive needs to be open (as opposed to hidden) and one of mutual benefit where we care not just for ourselves, but also for the people we lead or serve—and where we seek to act in their best interests. How do you show your intent? The answer is to "Create Transparency." Tell the truth in a way people can verify. Get real and genuine. Be open and authentic. Err on the side of disclosure. Don't have hidden agendas or hide information.

3.**Capabilities**. These refer to our ability to inspire confidence—the means we use to produce results. This refers to our talents, attitudes, skills and knowledge—in short, our competence. How can you display your capabilities? The answer is, "Get Better." Continuously improve. Increase your capabilities. Develop feedback systems, both formal and informal. Act upon the feedback you receive. Don't assume your knowledge and skills will be sufficient for tomorrow's challenges.

4.**Results**. This element refers to our track record, our performance, our ability to get the right things done. Results matter to our credibility. As Jack Welch said, having results is like having "performance chits" on the table. They give you clout. They classify you as a producer, as a performer. I maintain that results are what convert the cynics. How can you show and establish a track record? The answer is to get the right things done, the right information out there.  The upshot of everything here is to simply tell the truth, and leave the right impression. Each of these four cores—integrity, intent, capabilities and results—is vital to personal credibility and maintaining a trusted rapport with your audiences.
