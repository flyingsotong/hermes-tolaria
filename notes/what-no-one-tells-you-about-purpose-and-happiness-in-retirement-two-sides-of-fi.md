# What No One Tells You About Purpose and Happiness in Retirement (Two Sides of FI)

January 7, 2025 [What No One Tells You About Purpose and Happiness in Retirement](https://twosidesoffi.com/purposecode/)


Key Distinction Between Big P and Little P Purpose:

1. Big P Purpose:

- Goal-oriented and often difficult to achieve

- "All or nothing" mindset where you either succeed or fail

- Often follows a scarcity mindset (e.g., only one person can be president)

- Frequently tied to societal expectations and external validation

- Can lead to anxiety and frustration when goals aren't met

2. Little P Purpose:

- Process-oriented, focused on activities that bring joy and fulfillment

-**Success isn't tied to specific outcomes**

- Follows an abundance mindset - many ways to find purpose

- Based on personal satisfaction rather than external validation

- More sustainable and linked to long-term happiness

Finding Purpose - Practical Approaches:

1. Identifying Purpose Anchors:

- Examine potential future regrets

- Look back at childhood joys and interests

- Use the "art of subtraction" - identify what you enjoy in current activities

- Try new things (the "spaghetti method")

2. Common Misconceptions:

-**Purpose doesn't need to be found; it can be created**

- Money and financial independence are tools, not purposes

- Family/providing shouldn't be your sole purpose

-**You don't need to wait for retirement to pursue purpose**

Key Insights About Purpose and Retirement:

1. Time Management:

- Average Americans have about 5 hours of free time daily

- Focus on filling time slots with purposeful activities

- Start pursuing purpose before retirement

2. Connection to Happiness:

- The Harvard Developmental Adult Health Study showed interpersonal connections, not money or achievement, were most strongly linked to happiness

- Little p purpose often leads to community and connections

- Purpose anxiety is common due to societal pressure but can be managed through proper planning

3. Retirement Transitions:

- Some people need time to decompress before finding purpose

- Others benefit from planning purposeful activities before retiring

- Problems arise when people mistake the tools (money, retirement) for the purpose itself

The interview emphasizes that finding purpose is not about achieving grand goals but rather about engaging in activities that bring personal fulfillment and connection with others, regardless of financial status or career stage.
