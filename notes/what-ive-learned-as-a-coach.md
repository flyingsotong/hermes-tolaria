1.**There is always more going on than the client suggests.** The stuff that people struggle with is almost never what they say it is -- sometimes they're looking at the wrong thing, and other times they're trying to bury it. Don't expect your coachee to know what they're looking for at the start of the session. Look past their story.

2.**Watch for smokescreens.**If they’re asking for advice, sometimes they’re avoiding the real questions. Don’t take the bait.

1) What is the one truth the client is avoiding?

1.**Emotional language is taught though upbringing.** Don't assume we have the same emotional language.

2.**Compassionate observation without judgement is a skill and it will change your life.** Social media teaches us to form emotional responses to what's around us, but that can often rob us from truly understanding it.

3.**Get people into a position where they can have their own insight.** Safety and trust matter. Help your coachee define their own success.

4.**Know what success for myself looks like — to build my own confidence.** What does a successful session look like? What do I want to hear them say?

5. You can never want more for a client than they do.

6. Hospitality is the coach's superpower — the ability to create a space so that the coachee knows she's being seen and heard, and in doing so, creates an opportunity for change.

7.**Shifting from "What I Can Do" to "What Clients Need (Deeply)" — too many professionals only look at their skills and experience, not what is truly needed**
