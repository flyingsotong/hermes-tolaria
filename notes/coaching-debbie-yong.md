**5-session coaching program: Building your media leadership platform**

Overall goal: Help Debbie establish a strong, sustainable, and impactful media platform that aligns with her expertise, serves a specific audience, and supports her business objectives.

**Session 1: Defining your "who" and "what"**

Objective: Gain clarity on Debbie's target audience and the core problem she solves for them.

- Deep dive into her ideal client/audience: demographics, psychographics, professional roles, biggest challenges and pain points, aspirations and needs
- Refine her value proposition: what specific problem does she solve, what unique value does she offer, how does she transform her clients' lives or businesses
- Homework: conduct research or interviews with potential audience members; draft a refined "who we work with" statement for her website

**Session 2: From content to solutions**

Objective: Shift focus from content creation for its own sake to developing solutions that address the target audience's needs, with content as a supporting element.

- Brainstorm solutions beyond content: services, products, experiences; consulting, advisory work, or coaching; community or connection facilitation
- Map out a customer journey: how will she attract her ideal client, what touchpoints will they have with her brand, how will she deliver lasting value
- Homework: outline potential services or offerings; research successful business models of individuals who have done this

**Session 3: Personal branding and thought leadership**

Objective: Develop Debbie's personal brand and thought leadership strategy around helping Asian leaders articulate their value and build their own platforms.

- Define Debbie's brand voice and messaging: core values, unique perspective on Asian leadership, how to authentically connect with target audience
- Develop a content strategy: topics and themes, formats (written, audio, video, speaking), how to showcase expertise and build credibility
- Homework: refine brand story and key messages; create a content calendar for the next month

**Session 4: Podcast strategy and implementation**

Objective: Strategically plan the launch or relaunch of Debbie's podcast, ensuring it aligns with overall business goals and effectively reaches her target audience.

- Refine the podcast concept: how it serves the target audience, how it supports other business objectives (e.g. lead generation), format, tone, and style
- Podcast production and marketing: resources needed, how to attract and grow listenership, how to leverage the podcast for business development
- Homework: develop a detailed podcast production and launch plan; outline the first 5–10 episodes

**Session 5: Business model and monetization**

Objective: Develop a sustainable business model for Debbie's media platform, exploring monetization strategies that align with her values and goals.

- Explore monetization options: sponsorships, subscriptions, services, events; how to diversify revenue streams; pricing strategies
- Develop a financial plan: revenue goals for the next year, startup and operating costs, how to track and measure financial performance
- Homework: create a detailed business plan outlining the revenue model and financial projections; develop a launch and marketing plan for services or offerings

**What Debbie was seeking**

1. More information about coaching services and how they're structured
2. A structured way to think about her media business after leaving traditional journalism
3. Guidance on making her content initiatives (newsletter, podcast) profitable and sustainable
4. Help figuring out her post-journalist professional identity, particularly around personal branding and thought leadership

She was at a career crossroads after leaving several roles (including as editorial director at Michelin Guide), and wanted to avoid more job changes that could damage her reputation. She had already started a newsletter and was planning a podcast on Asian leadership.

---

**Insights Debbie gained**

1. The importance of being highly specific rather than broad — "niche" doesn't mean "small" but rather "highly specific and useful for a specific segment"
2. Content as a means to an end — she should determine what her audience truly needs before assuming content is the answer
3. Shifting from journalist to creator mindset — the need to "show up" more in her content rather than disappearing as journalists traditionally do
4. Audience-first thinking — think deeply about who exactly needs what from her, rather than focusing on credentials
5. Financial realism — she doesn't need a massive audience to be financially successful, just the right audience who would pay for specific value

---

**What I might have missed as a coach**

- Deeper exploration of her ADHD mention — when Debbie mentioned being "high functioning, somewhere on the spectrum" with ADHD, this wasn't explored further; this could have been relevant to what structures might work best for her
- More concrete market validation strategies — while the emphasis on knowing her audience was right, specific tactics for validating assumptions weren't provided
- Timeline and milestone planning — given her history of changing directions, establishing clear milestones might have helped with accountability
- Her personal financial runway — she mentioned having financial support from her husband "for a while," but what this meant in terms of time wasn't explored
- Her event moderation experience — she mentioned moderating events and seeing a lack of Asian representation on panels, but this wasn't explored as a potential service offering or differentiator

---

**Sales approach improvement opportunities**

- More structured package presentation — rather than "I would put together a five-part package," describe a ready-made structure that shows it's been done successfully before
- Clearer pricing — at least a price range during the call would set expectations
- Stronger social proof — brief success stories of other clients would strengthen credibility
- More definitive next steps — specific exercise with clear deliverables rather than open-ended homework
- Urgency or incentive — provide a reason to make a decision soon (limited availability or an introductory rate)
- Value articulation — summarize the specific benefits she would receive from the coaching package more explicitly when transitioning to the sale
