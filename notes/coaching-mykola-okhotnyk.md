# Coaching - Mykola Okhotnyk

**June 4 2025**

**Client's Intent (What Mykola wanted to achieve at the start)**

Mykola explicitly stated his primary aim for the session was centered on**personal development**. He expressed feeling "a bit lost" in his profession and was unsure if he was "doing the right thing." He wanted to explore these feelings and his career direction with Alan. He was looking for clarity and a path forward, particularly concerning two main factors: anxiety about the future of the media industry and personal uncertainty about his career choices and passions.

**Client's Awareness (What Mykola discovered in the coaching call)**

Throughout the conversation, Mykola gained several key insights and shifts in perspective:

1.**Reframing Industry Challenges as Opportunities:** He became aware that the very industry disruptions causing him anxiety (publisher failures, mergers, rise of creators, AI impact) could be reframed as significant opportunities for his consulting work. He acknowledged the need to "position ourselves as the solutions and as a helper for media."

2.**The Value of Providing Confidence:** Mykola recognized that a key role he could play for struggling publishers was not just offering solutions, but providing the *confidence* they lack to implement necessary changes.

3.**Embracing His Predictive Nature:** He began to see his tendency to "look around corners" and feel anxious about the future not just as a burden, but as a potential strength that could be "embraced" for strategic foresight and positioning.

4.**Normalizing a Multi-Career Path:** Mykola realized that his underlying belief in needing "one career for the whole life" was a source of his "what if" anxieties. He gained awareness that having multiple, evolving careers is a modern norm and that his diverse experiences are an asset, not a sign of being lost.

5.**Shifting from "Sunk Costs" to Future Runway:** He acknowledged the "fallacy when the cost of switching" influenced him but was guided to focus on the "runway that's in front of you, not the one that's behind you."

6.**Understanding the Role of Coaching:** Mykola explicitly stated he gained "a couple of nice ideas that I haven't thought of before" and valued the dedicated space for reflection that coaching provides, distinct from conversations with friends or managers.

7.**Actionable Perspective on Age and Change:** He was able to see that while age brings biological realities, it also opens up different types of opportunities, rather than simply closing doors.

**Your Lesson as a Coach (What Alan missed or could have done better, referencing ICF Competencies and providing specific examples/alternative questions)**

Reflecting on the session with Mykola, here are areas where I (as Alan, the coach) could have potentially enhanced the coaching:

1.**Deeper Exploration of Underlying Values and Passions (ICF Competency 7: Evokes Awareness):**

-**Observation:** When Mykola mentioned his original major in International Relations (IR) and pondered "what if," I quickly moved to the impracticality of dwelling on the past and the "sunk cost fallacy." While valid, I might have missed an opportunity to explore what *values* or *interests* from IR still resonate with him and how they might inform his current or future path, or bring more fulfillment.

***Missed Opportunity:** I didn't fully explore the *source* of his feeling that he might not "like the most" what he is currently doing (05:30).

-**Alternative Approach/Questions:**

- "Mykola, when you think back to your time studying International Relations, what was it about that field that particularly energized or fascinated you?"
- "Are there elements of that fascination—perhaps a particular skill, type of impact, or environment—that you feel are missing now, or that you'd like to integrate more into your professional life?"
- "If you could bring just one core value or interest from your IR studies into your current consulting work, what would that be and what might that look like?"

1.**Co-creating More Concrete Action Steps (ICF Competency 8: Facilitates Client Growth):**

-**Observation:** Mykola ended the session stating he had "a couple of really important things that I need to reflect on" and would email with ideas. This is positive, but the session concluded without collaboratively defining specific, actionable next steps he could take based on his new awareness.

***Missed Opportunity:** While the reflection is valuable, facilitating the design of clear actions can help the client bridge awareness to tangible progress.

-**Alternative Approach/Questions (towards the end):**

- "Mykola, you've mentioned several new perspectives today. To help solidify these insights, what's one initial action, however small, you might take this week to explore one of these further?"
- "As you reflect on our conversation, what support or resources might you need to move forward with these ideas?"
- "How will you ensure you dedicate time for the reflection you mentioned, and what would you like to achieve through that reflection before we potentially connect again?"

1.**Further Exploring the "Anxious Nature" (ICF Competency 7: Evokes Awareness & Competency 4: Cultivates Trust and Safety):**

-**Observation:** Mykola mentioned his "general anxious probably nature" (22:15) as a contributing factor to his feelings. While I reframed his forward-looking tendency positively, I didn't deeply explore the *impact* of this stated "anxious nature" on his decision-making or well-being beyond the career context.

-**Missed Opportunity:** A brief exploration could have helped Mykola understand this aspect of himself better and how it interacts with his professional life, ensuring he felt fully heard on this point.

-**Alternative Approach/Questions:**

- "You mentioned a 'general anxious nature.' In what ways do you find this trait serves you, and in what ways might it hinder you, particularly when considering career changes or navigating uncertainty?"
- "What strategies have you found effective in the past for managing this anxiety so you can make clear, confident decisions?"

By focusing more on these areas, I could have potentially helped Mykola connect more deeply with his intrinsic motivations and leave with an even clearer sense of actionable direction, further enhancing the impact of the coaching.
