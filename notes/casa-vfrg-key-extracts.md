## VMC Criteria (VFR weather minima)

**Class A, C, E, and G airspace (aeroplanes):**

| Height | Flight visibility | Distance from cloud | Operational reqs |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| At or above 10,000 ft AMSL | 8,000 m (8 km) | 1,500 m horizontal, 1,000 ft vertical | — |
| Below 10,000 ft AMSL | 5,000 m (5 km) | 1,500 m horizontal, 1,000 ft vertical | — |
| Class G at or below 3,000 ft AMSL or 1,000 ft AGL (whichever higher) | 5,000 m (5 km) | Clear of cloud, in sight of ground or water | — |

**Class D controlled airspace (aeroplanes):**

| Height | Flight visibility | Distance from cloud |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------|
| All heights | 5,000 m (5 km) | 600 m horizontal; 1,000 ft vertical above, 500 ft vertical below |

**Radio requirement**: In Class C, D, and E, radio must be carried and used on the appropriate frequency. Class G — no radio required.

## Fuel requirements (CASR 91.455 / MOS 19.02)

| Aircraft | Flight rules | Final reserve | Contingency |
|----------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Piston ≤ 5,700 kg | **VFR day** | **30 min** | nil |
| Piston ≤ 5,700 kg | Night VFR | 45 min | nil |
| Piston ≤ 5,700 kg | IFR | 45 min | nil |
| Turbojet / turboprop > 5,700 kg | VFR or IFR | 30 min | 5% of trip fuel |

## VFR semicircular cruising levels

Above 3,000 ft AMSL and below transition altitude (10,000 ft):

| Magnetic track | Cruising level (altitude) |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| 000° to 179° (easterly) | Odd thousands + 500 ft (e.g. 3,500, 5,500, 7,500, 9,500) |
| 180° to 359° (westerly) | Even thousands + 500 ft (e.g. 4,500, 6,500, 8,500) |

Below 3,000 ft AMSL — random levels (no semicircular rule applies).

## Transition altitude

- Transition altitude: **10,000 ft** (below = QNH, above = 1013.2 hPa)
- Transition level: FL110 when area QNH ≥ 1013.2 hPa; varies when QNH < 1013.2 hPa
- You must not cruise within the transition layer
