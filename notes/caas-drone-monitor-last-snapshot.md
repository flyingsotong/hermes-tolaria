## Page: UA Regulatory Requirements
**URL:** https://www.caas.gov.sg/public-passengers/unmanned-aircraft/ua-regulatory-requirements
**Last Updated on CAAS site:** 01 November 2024

### Registration
- All UA >250g must be registered, regardless of purpose (recreational, educational, business)

### Recreational use — pilot requirements
- Indoors AND outdoors: UABT or UAPL required for 1.5–7kg if flying in publicly accessible place or at event with >50 attendees
- UAPL required for >7kg in publicly accessible place or >50-person event
- Outdoors (additional): UABT or UAPL for 1.5–7kg; UAPL for >7kg

### Recreational use — permit requirements
- **Indoors:** Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit for events with >50 attendees
- **Outdoors:** Class 2 Activity Permit required if UA ≤25kg AND flying >200ft ASML, within restricted/danger area, OR within 5km of airport/military airbase
- Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit required if: UA >25kg (regardless of location/height), OR flying BVLOS (regardless of weight)
- SPF permit also required for protected areas
- UA operations not allowed in prohibited areas at any time

### Educational use — pilot requirements
- Same as recreational: UABT or UAPL for 1.5–7kg in public/50+ events; UAPL for >7kg

### Educational use — permit requirements
- Indoors: Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit for >50-person events
- Outdoors: Class 2 Activity Permit for UA ≤7kg (note: lower threshold than recreational) flying >200ft ASML, in restricted/danger area, or within 5km of airport/airbase
- Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit if: UA >7kg (regardless of location), OR BVLOS

### Business/Non-Recreational/Non-Educational use — pilot requirements
- Indoors: UAPL required if flying in publicly accessible place, sporting activity, or event with >50 attendees
- Outdoors: UAPL required regardless of weight, location, or height

### Business use — permit requirements
- Indoors: Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit for organised sporting activity/competition/race/tournament, or event with >50 attendees
- Outdoors: Operator Permit + Class 1 Activity Permit required regardless of weight, location, or height

## Page: No-Fly Zones and Unmanned Aircraft Flying Area
**URL:** https://www.caas.gov.sg/public-passengers/unmanned-aircraft/no-fly-zones-and-unmanned-aircraft-flying-area
**Last Updated on CAAS site:** 20 January 2026

### No-fly zones
General no-fly zones (permit required to operate in these areas):
- Areas within 5km of aerodromes
- Areas within danger areas
- Protected areas under Air Navigation Act
- Areas within prohibited areas
- Areas within restricted areas
- National parks, nature reserves, and private property may have additional local restrictions

### Temporary Restricted Areas (TRAs)
- Established by CAAS for specific durations during aerial displays or large-scale events (e.g., National Day Parade)
- All aerial activities prohibited during TRAs (kite-flying, captive balloons, drones)
- Check OneMap before flying for current TRAs

### Unmanned Aircraft Flying Areas (UAFAs)
No entry fees for UAFAs. Current regulations and safety guidelines apply.

**UAFA @ Pandan Reservoir:**
- 16 hectares over reservoir waters
- Operating hours: 7am–7pm
- No fuel-based UA; no fixed-wing UA
- Boundaries marked by standing flags and yellow water buoys
- No retrieval service if UA falls into water
- No operations when fogging is in progress

**UAFA @ Dover Road:**
- 0.6 hectares over land and greenery
- Operating hours: 7am–7pm
- Area demarcated by standing flags
- VLOS required; exercise caution near trees

**Commercial UA Flying Areas:**
- Fees and different operating hours may apply
- See respective company websites published on OneMap

### Contact and updates
- Enquiries: caas_utep@caas.gov.sg
- Telegram channel available for site updates (UA Flying Areas)
- OneMap app recommended for pre-flight zone checks
