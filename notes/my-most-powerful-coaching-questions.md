The question that created the most awareness for Alan's clients was one that challenged their core assumptions about their identity and the nature of their work.

Across coaching calls, the most impactful questions were those that helped clients reframe their professional identity from a **"journalist"** or **"content creator"** to a **"problem-solver"** or **"strategic partner,"** often by asking them to define their value in terms of **demand** rather than **supply**.

**1. Challenging the "journalist" identity**

This question helped clients separate their self-worth from a shrinking industry and see their skills as transferable assets:

> **"What would your work look like if you focused on impact rather than format?"**

- **Ankur Paliwal** realized his organization's core challenge was a flawed premise: *"Content is not the product; it's the cost of making the product."* This led him to redefine Queerbeat's value as providing services and impact, not just articles.
- **Bhanupriya Rao** shifted her organization's identity from a passive journalistic witness to an active **"agent of change and a 'mobilizer of care.'"**
- **Venkatesh HR + Jency Jacob (Boom)** moved beyond thinking of themselves as a media company to reframing their identity around their core skill: the **methodology and process of fact-checking.**
- **Golda Arthur** realized her podcast was no longer the entire business, but **"just one part of a larger ecosystem"** — a mission-driven brand that could also offer workshops and events.

**2. Focusing on demand over supply**

This line of questioning forced clients to stop creating content and start identifying who specifically needs their information and why they would pay for it:

> **"Who needs this information, what will they do with it, and how will they benefit from it?"**

- **Sharada Balasubramanian** realized she needed to shift from a purely supply-driven (creating journalism content) to a **demand-driven approach** — identifying who specifically needs her information and why.
- **Chatwan Mongkol** was guided away from thinking about "news" as the primary product and instead emphasized an **audience-first, problem-solving approach.**
- **Vignesh Vellore** was challenged to deconstruct his entire narrative and focus on the fundamental question: **"Why are people paying us?"**

**3. Addressing internal blocks**

For clients struggling with self-doubt and inaction, the most powerful question was one that challenged their internal resistance:

> **"What is the one truth I am avoiding?"**

- **Gosia Klimowicz's** coaching centered on confronting her **"uncomfortable truth"** — the self-doubt and fear of being a "fake" that was preventing her from marketing herself effectively.
- **Erin Hale's** pivotal moment was accepting the industry's decline as a baseline reality, shifting her from a victim mindset to an agent mindset: *"Given this reality, what will I create?"*
- **Saroj Singh** realized her feeling of being "stuck" was rooted in a core, unacknowledged fear about her 3-year fixed-term contract.
