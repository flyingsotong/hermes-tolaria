**What worked well in the session**

1. Contracting around coaching timing (15 minutes).
2. Great demonstration of trust and safety throughout. You tried to understand the client within their context while demonstrating curiosity. For example: *"I would love to know what is on your mind." "How are you feeling about…?"*
3. Beautiful demonstration of maintaining presence. Your body language and tone were inviting and helped hold the space for thought and vulnerability for the coachee. You remained calm while the coachee was winding up, creating space for silence, pause and reflection.
4. Clear summaries throughout, including normalizing, clarifying and breaking it down to the essence. *"It does not sound like you have problems to articulate. I hear you said…"* You went on to acknowledge the strength of the coachee and brought it back to the realm of feelings.
5. You were able to redirect the conversation to the person away from the box. Example: *"What assumptions are you making about your boss and what he might say?"*

---

**What was missing / could be improved upon**

1. The goal was unclear. You took the topic to be the goal. Practice to ask at least three goal questions going forward: *"What do you want to achieve during the coaching session?" "Why is this important to you?" "How do you know you will have achieved it?"*
2. At the end of the session the coachee still uses words like **"must, have to, should."** Those words are indications that no real buy-in for any option has been created yet. There is room to explore further with more time.

Well done in demonstrating coaching competencies 4 and 5. You were able to co-create the relationship and maintain presence. It was a joy to listen to this coaching session.
