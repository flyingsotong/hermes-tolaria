# What makes me unique as a coach

Based on an analysis of your extensive coaching notes and self-reflections, your uniqueness as a coach stems from a specific intersection of**deep media industry expertise**,**psychological excavation**, and a**"compassionate challenge"** style.
Here is what makes you unique as a coach:
**1. The "Uncomfortable Truth" Framework**
You have developed a signature methodology around identifying the uncomfortable truth that keeps professionals stuck. Unlike coaches who focus solely on forward-looking tactics, you believe that "you cannot build a new future on a foundation of denial." You are uniquely skilled at listening past the story to find the "handbrake" a client doesn't realize is on.
**2. Reframing "Capital J" Journalism**
You serve as a specialized bridge for journalists in crisis. You help them divorce their identity from their job title, reframing their skills from "content creation" (which is being commoditized) to "problem-solving" and "high-value insights."
-**Example:** Helping Ankur Paliwal realize that "content is not the product; it's the cost of making the product."
-**Example:** Guiding Bhanupriya Rao to shift from a "Needs Assessment" to a "Value Assessment."
**3. The "Compassionate Challenge"**
Your style is characterized by a high degree of empathy mixed with radical directness. You often use self-disclosure—sharing your own struggles as a TV producer or your fears about aging—to create a safe container for your clients to face their own vulnerabilities. You don't just cheerlead; you provoke critical thinking about their thoughts.
**4. Business Strategy as a Coaching Tool**
While you strive to maintain ICF coaching principles, you uniquely lean into your consultant persona when it adds value. You help media entrepreneurs build "instant companies" and "launching muscles," moving them from Seekers (who want more certifications) to Finders (who apply knowledge immediately).
**5. Focus on "Personhood" and Safety**
You have a deep interest in how a client's upbringing and childhood narratives influence their professional paralysis. You view coaching as hospitality—the ability to create a space where a coachee feels seen and heard, which you call your "superpower."
**6. Your Core Strengths**
Your CliftonStrengths profile (Relator, Strategic, Restorative, Learner, Belief) manifests in your coaching as an ability to:
-**Relator:** Build high-trust, vulnerable relationships quickly.
-**Strategic:** See solutions before others know there is a problem.
-**Restorative:** Identify exactly what is "wrong" in a business model or career path and help resolve it.
In summary, you are not a generalist life coach; you are a**strategic thought partner for the "mid-career crisis" in media**, helping people navigate industry death by finding new life in their own "personhood" and specialized expertise.
