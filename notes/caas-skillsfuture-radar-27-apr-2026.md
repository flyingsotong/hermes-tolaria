## CAAS schemes

**Aviation Outreach Fund (AOF)**
Relevance: Medium

Previously called the Youth Outreach Seed Fund, the AOF provides up to S$40,000 per initiative for schools, industry associations, societies, or student groups to develop aviation activities that ignite youth interest. Past-funded activities include curriculum development, enrichment programmes, competitions, and development of aviation teaching facilities. The primary constraint for Alan is that the fund targets institutions and associations — not individual media ventures — and the beneficiary must be youth. However, Dronefly.sg's mission (demystifying Singapore drone law, promoting safety culture) aligns tightly with the fund's goals. A partnership application with an aero club, polytechnic, or school group could unlock this. Magenta Debrief is harder to fit unless positioned as an educational resource for youth aviation outreach.

Status: Ongoing (page last updated March 2024 — confirm current round with CAAS directly)

URL: https://www.caas.gov.sg/public-passengers/learn-about-singapore-aviation/aviation-outreach-fund

## SkillsFuture opportunities

**Current credit quantum:** S$500 opening credit for all Singapore Citizens aged 25 and above (base tier). Additional top-ups available for those aged 40+.

**SFEC status for SMEs:** The SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit page (skillsfuture.gobusiness.gov.sg) returned a 404 — the URL appears to have moved. Cannot confirm current SFEC quantum this week. Historically S$10,000 per enterprise; check https://skillsfuture.gobusiness.gov.sg directly.

**SkillsFuture Credit (Opening Credit)**
Relevance: High

S$500 usable against any SSG-approved course listed on the MySkillsFuture portal, covering aviation, drone, digital, AI, media, and content skills. Alan can apply this to UABT refreshers, drone operations courses, flight simulation evaluation training aligned to Magenta Debrief's review work, or AI workflow courses relevant to Splice Nano. Immediate, no application required beyond logging in.

---

**SkillsFuture Level-Up Programme + Mid-Career Enhanced Subsidy**
Relevance: High

For Singapore Citizens aged 40 and above. The Level-Up Programme provides an additional top-up credit on top of the S$500 base (quantum to confirm at myskillsfuture.gov.sg/level-up). The Mid-Career Enhanced Subsidy increases course fee subsidies to up to 90% for this cohort. This is the most financially impactful SkillsFuture lever for Alan — it can dramatically reduce the cost of longer, more substantive courses (e.g., aviation-related diplomas, AI and data skills certifications, content publishing programmes). Mid-Career Training Allowance also provides income support for full-time or part-time long-form training.

Low lift to complete — worth scanning the catalogue for anything applicable to Splice or Splice Nano's AI workflow content. No financial commitment required.

**TechSkills Accelerator (TeSA) — IMDA**
Relevance: Medium

IMDA's TeSA programme trains ICT and digital media professionals for tech-adjacent roles. Historically includes tracks for digital content, media technology, and infocomm. Given Splice Nano's focus on AI-augmented solo creator workflows, there may be alignment with IMDA's "digital content" or "emerging tech" pathways — either as a training recipient or as a training provider delivering IMDA-recognised content. Worth a direct enquiry to IMDA.

URL: https://www.imda.gov.sg/how-we-can-help/techskills-accelerator-tesa

**SkillsFuture Series**
Relevance: Medium

Short, industry-relevant training sprints in Digital, Green, Care, and Industry 4.0 pillars. The Digital pillar includes AI tools, data analytics, and digital marketing — all directly applicable to Splice and Magenta Debrief workflows. These are typically subsidised and fast to complete. ## My picks

**1. SkillsFuture Level-Up + Mid-Career Enhanced Subsidy (personal)**
Immediate action. Log into MySkillsFuture, check your current credit balance, and verify Level-Up Programme top-up eligibility. Use the credit for one high-value course — aviation skills (flight sim evaluation methodology, instrument rating ground school), or AI/content creation (applicable to both Splice Nano and Magenta Debrief). The 90% subsidy means your out-of-pocket cost on a S$1,500 course drops to S$150.

**2. Aviation Outreach Fund — Dronefly.sg partnership angle**
Medium-term. The AOF's S$40,000 ceiling is meaningful. Dronefly.sg is already doing exactly what CAAS funds — demystifying drone law for the public. The missing piece is institutional sponsorship. Identify one Singapore polytechnic or aero association (e.g., Singapore Aircrew Association, Singapore Flying Club) to co-apply with. You bring the content platform and editorial credibility; they provide the institutional eligibility. This is a 2–3 month conversation, not a cold submission.

**3. IMDA TeSA — Splice Nano training provider angle**
Speculative but high upside. Splice Nano's proposition — AI-augmented solo creator training — maps to what IMDA wants to fund under digital media skills development. A direct enquiry to IMDA about whether Splice Nano content could qualify as TeSA-aligned training (or whether IMDA would co-fund a programme) is worth a one-email test. The Splice client roster (Google, Meta, ABC, Deutsche Welle) provides credibility.
