# Coaching - Sharada Balasubramanian

**April 23, 2025**

**What Sharada wanted to get out of the conversation**

- **Direction and Clarity:** Sharada, with 23 years in media, felt "lost" and at a "crossroads." She'd seen the industry evolve from newspapers to digital and social media and felt traditional journalism, especially pitching to publications, no longer resonated with her. She stated, *"I'm no longer interested in pitching stories to a publication... I'm like done to death with all that."*
- **Leverage Her Experience:** She wanted to find ways to utilize her extensive experience in environmental and development journalism, her skills in reporting, interviewing, investigations, audio storytelling/podcasting, and photojournalism.
- **Find a New Path/Platform:** While she didn't want to work for anyone else, she was unsure what form her independent work should take. She mentioned a past idea of a niche website focusing on development or climate economics, similar to how science journalism translates complex papers.
- **Understand the Changing Media Dynamics:** She was grappling with how the "business model" of media was impacting journalism and wanted to find a sustainable way forward.

**What Sharada arrived at (key takeaways/decisions)**

- **Focus on Demand, Not Just Supply:** A major realization was the need to shift from a purely supply-driven (creating journalism content) to a demand-driven approach — identifying who specifically needs her information and why.
- **Niche Down is Crucial:** She understood the importance of identifying a "super niche" and "thinly sliced" area of focus rather than trying to appeal broadly.
- **Potential in ESG and Specialized Data:** The idea of focusing on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investment analysis or providing specialized data/information for specific industries (like renewable energy B2B) resonated with her.
- **Explore New Formats (Video Podcasts):** She was open to the idea of video podcasts, recognizing the trend and monetization potential.
- **Leverage AI for Ideation and Research:** She was introduced to the idea of using AI tools like Gemini to analyze her past work, identify niche themes, and even plan content, which she found to be a "great idea."
- **Need for Prep Work and Research:** Sharada acknowledged the need to do "prep work, do some research, do some analysis" on the demand side before launching anything. This involves asking potential users what information they need and how it would help them.
- **Own Her Content and Expertise:** The conversation reinforced her desire to own her content and not be dictated to by traditional editorial structures.

**How Alan helped as a coach**

- **Challenged Existing Beliefs:** Questioned her underlying assumption that "doing journalism" must equate to "being a journalist" in a traditional sense. *"How do you get to this point where journalism equates to you being a journalist?"* and later, *"Doing journalism or being a journalist?"*
- **Introduced the Demand vs. Supply Framework:** A pivotal contribution. Used her economics background to explain how media is often overly focused on creating supply without clearly identified demand. *"Media is the only one that is so crazy about creating supply, right?"* Guided her to ask, *"Where is the demand? And then figure out what the supply could be."*
- **Highlighted the Importance of "Usefulness" and "Value":** Stressed that for information to be monetizable, it must be useful and valuable to a specific audience who would then be willing to pay. *"If it's useful and if it's valuable, someone will pay for it."*
- **Pointed to Emerging Trends and AI:** Brought her attention to the rise of AI-generated content as a competitor on the supply side and, conversely, as a tool she could leverage. Also highlighted the growth of video podcasts.
- **Provided Concrete Examples of Niches:** Offered specific examples of how she could "thin slice" her expertise:
  - Explaining economics (e.g., interest rates) to young adults
  - Focusing on ESG products for investors
  - Interviewing ESG analysts or stock exchange representatives
- **Suggested Actionable Research Steps:** Advised her to talk to potential users (e.g., ESG analysts) about their information needs: *"What is the hardest information for you to get that you hate hunting for?"* Also suggested analyzing existing creators in potential niches and using AI to analyze her own work.
- **Offered a Framework for Defining Her Audience and Value:** Guided her through questions to identify her target audience and the value she could provide: *"Who needs this information, what will they do with it, how will they benefit from it, and how will you benefit from it subsequently."*
- **Validated Her Desire for Independence:** Affirmed her decision not to work for anyone else and encouraged her to build something on her own terms.
- **Emphasized the Irrelevance of Format (Initially):** Suggested that the specific format (article, podcast, etc.) is less important at the outset than identifying the problem she's solving and for whom. *"I would even go as far as to say that the format doesn't even matter... at this point."*
- **Reassured Her About Timing and Tools:** Pointed out that *"this is a good time to get in front of [current trends]"* and that tools like AI make starting easier now than it would have been five years ago.
- **Recognized Her Pivot Point:** Acknowledged she was at a "really great pivot of your career" and commended her willingness to embrace change.

Your coaching helped Sharada move from a feeling of being "lost" to having a clearer framework for identifying a viable and fulfilling path forward — one that leverages her unique skills and experience by focusing on specific audience needs and market demands.
