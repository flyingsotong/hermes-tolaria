# Key Points on Media Freedom in Hong Kong

5/4/2024

FCC Journalism Conference

Tom Grundy

Bao Choy

1.**Government Control and Legal Challenges:**

 - The government's attitude towards media is increasingly controlling, not relaxing.

 - Legal frameworks and court rulings are used to test and potentially limit journalistic practices.

 - High-profile cases and trials, such as the Stand News case, are significant for press freedom and are closely watched for their implications.

2.**Journalistic Practices and Challenges:**

 - There is a discrepancy between how judges understand media practices and the actual practices themselves.

 - Journalists face challenges such as censorship, avoidance of sensitive topics, and pressures to align with government narratives.

 - Public attitude towards media is polarized, with diminishing trust and support for journalists.

3.**Impact of National Security Law:**

 - The introduction of the National Security Law has led to increased scrutiny and pressure on journalists, with some facing legal actions and others altering their practices to avoid crossing red lines set by the government.

4.**Media Landscape Changes:**

 - Several media outlets have shut down or downsized, while new ones have emerged, showing a dynamic but challenging media environment.

 - Journalists are adapting by using digital platforms and social media to continue reporting despite the constraints.

5.**Role of International Media:**

 - International media outlets continue to operate but face their own sets of challenges and scrutiny under the new legal frameworks.

6.**Advice for Journalists:**

 - Experienced journalists emphasize the importance of understanding legal risks, staying professional, and adhering to rigorous journalistic standards.

 - Despite the challenges, there is a sense of mission and dedication among journalists to continue providing accurate and fair reporting.

These points reflect a media environment in Hong Kong that is under significant pressure with legal, political, and social challenges, impacting how journalists operate and the overall state of press freedom.
