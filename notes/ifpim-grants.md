# IFPIM grants

**My thoughts**

The one thing that's come across in all of these applications is really a question of what this money is meant to do. If the goal is to keep the lights on and to function for the next two years, I think that's one.

But let's be absolutely clear that two years from now, the market situation will worsen further because there have been no incentives for these newsrooms to pivot. I don't think I've seen any of these applications address new opportunities that are in front of them — something that takes them away from the work that they've always done.

So in many ways, these ground applications are basically asking for money to do exactly what they've always done and to do what has brought them to this situation begin with.

A $250K grant is lifechanging for smaller newsrooms — but they should demonstrate that this money will indeed change their business strategy instead of merely keeping the lights on.

**Questions for all**

- What have you learned about the media space the past two years?

- What are your best prediction of where things will go two years from now?

- How has this informed your plans?

- What is the business problem you want to solve with grants?

- What is the grant meant to do? How will it change your trajectory two years from now?

- What do you really need to focus on to build a viable business before this grant runs out?
