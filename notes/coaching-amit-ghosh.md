**February 6, 2025**

**Analysis of coaching session (ICF Core Competencies)**

**Foundation**

Strong ethical practice throughout — established confidentiality upfront and got explicit permission regarding the recording. Maintained appropriate coaching boundaries, staying within coaching topics rather than straying into consulting or therapy.

**Relationship building**

Strongest area was maintaining presence and creating trust. Demonstrated deep listening and allowed Amit to explore his thoughts without rushing or judgment. When he said "Just give me a second" to think, that space was honored, showing respect for his process. This created psychological safety that allowed him to explore vulnerable topics like his fears and childhood programming.

Area for growth: establishing more explicit agreements upfront. While the time boundary (45 minutes) was set, more time could have been spent establishing specific outcomes Amit wanted from the session. Questions like "What would make this conversation most valuable for you?" or "How will you know this conversation has been helpful?" could have created clearer direction.

**Communication skills**

Active listening was evident throughout — picked up on key words like "intentional" and "selfish" that Amit used repeatedly, and helped him explore the deeper meaning behind these terms. Noticed important contradictions, like how he believed he was "asking for help" when reaching out to people, yet acknowledged he never actually asks for help in those conversations.

Questioning showed both strengths and areas for development. Some powerful questions led to insights (like exploring why he views networking as "asking for help"). However, some questions could have been more open-ended rather than yes/no format — for example, instead of "Is that correct?", ask "What are your thoughts about that?"

**Facilitating learning and growth**

Amit achieved several meaningful insights:

1. He recognized how his childhood programming about "selfishness" affects his business development
2. He gained a new perspective on giving/receiving in professional relationships
3. He understood how fear of being perceived as "asking for help" was holding him back
4. He reframed "selfish" networking as "giving opportunity for connection"

Main area for development: helping Amit create more concrete action steps from these insights. While the awareness was valuable, more time near the end exploring how he might apply these realizations in practical ways would have helped.

**What Amit gained**

A significant shift in perspective about networking and business development. He moved from seeing outreach as potentially selfish or burdensome to understanding it as an opportunity for mutual connection and giving. He also gained awareness of how his childhood programming about selfishness was creating unnecessary barriers in his business growth.

---

**Recommendations for improvement**

1. Spend more time at the beginning establishing clear desired outcomes for the session
2. Use more consistently open-ended questions that invite deeper exploration
3. Check in periodically about whether the conversation direction continues to serve him
4. Help create specific, actionable steps near the end of the session
5. Establish accountability measures for implementing his insights
