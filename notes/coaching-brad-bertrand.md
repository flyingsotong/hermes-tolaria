# Coaching - Brad Bertrand

---

**Friday, March 28, 2025**

**Brad's initial problem statement**

Brad was seeking help with evangelizing and gaining buy-in for content design within Standard Chartered, specifically: helping stakeholders understand the value of content design, overcoming resistance from legacy bankers who don't understand product design or content design, and finding ways to educate and demonstrate the impact of his work.

**Key awareness created**

Prioritization vs bandwidth — Brad realized his challenges were more about prioritization than lack of time. He identified specific actions he could take, such as assigning the SharePoint site update to his deputy, creating quarterly workshops to educate junior product owners, and setting clear boundaries for team projects.

Approach to stakeholder education — shift from seeing it as a massive undertaking to creating recurring, manageable touchpoints. Using visual before-and-after examples, creating a standardized UX writing standards guide, and developing mini-workshops with practical examples.

Recruitment and team building — reconceptualizing content design hiring: look for problem-solvers, not just content experts. Focus on finding people who can understand user research, identify and remove friction points, and think like product managers.

**Potential coaching improvements**

1. Earlier probing — could have asked more direct questions about specific barriers earlier in the conversation; might have pushed Brad to articulate his challenges more precisely from the start.
2. More structured goal setting — while the conversation was organic and helpful, a more structured goal-setting framework could have been beneficial. Could have used a SMART approach to define Brad's objectives.
3. Follow-up mechanism — the conversation didn't establish a clear follow-up plan. Could have suggested a specific date to review progress on the identified actions.
4. Deeper exploration of emotional barriers — while the conversation touched on Brad's organizational challenges, it could have delved deeper into his personal hesitations and motivations, including why he procrastinates on certain types of projects.

**Strengths of the coaching**

- Organic, conversational approach — allowed Brad to discover solutions through dialogue and helped him articulate his own path forward.
- Practical, actionable suggestions — provided concrete, implementable ideas and helped Brad see his challenges from different perspectives.
- Active listening — reflected back Brad's own thoughts and asked clarifying questions that led to deeper insights.

Overall, the coaching session was successful in helping Brad gain clarity, develop a more strategic approach to his work, and identify specific actions to address his challenges.
