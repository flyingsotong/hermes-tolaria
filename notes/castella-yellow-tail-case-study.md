# Castella Yellow Tail case study

- Takes place in 2001 in the US

- Problem to solve:

- US winemakers weren't seeing much growth at this stage.

- Wine appreciation was still relatively new and people didn't like having to make their way through difficult language around wine drinking.

- US winemakers were after the same template -- talk about the mystery of the grape, the legacy of the vineyard, and with pricing to undercut the competition. All of which didn't help the growth of an overcrowded industry.

- The complicated nature of wine selection also made it hard for retail staff to recommend

- Castella launched Yellow Tail in July 2001 and kept things simple

- Two grapes -- Chardonnay and Shiraz

- No technical jargon, just a simple non-traditional label with a kangaroo in orange and yellow -- eyecatching, unintimidating

- Red and white used the same bottles -- minimizing stocking and manufacturing

- No marketing for the first few years

- The expected to sell 25,000 cases in their first year. They sold 9x that amount

- By the end of 2005, total sales were at 25 million cases.
