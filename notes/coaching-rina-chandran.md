# Coaching - Rina Chandran

---

**February 16, 2024**

The first call was interesting. It's a conversation many late-career journalists have. Rina, who has been in media her entire career and has built an impressive resume at Reuters, Thomson Reuters Foundation, and Bloomberg, was surprisingly taken aback by her third layoff. As someone who covers industry trends and sees where things are heading, her reaction was surprising to me.

Hearing her discuss her skills and experiences was enlightening, but it was also apparent she was hesitant about branching into new areas. Journalists often confine their abilities to a traditional news delivery format and that becomes learned helplessness. I emphasized repeatedly that she possesses many transferable skills, particularly as she transitions from old media to a domain increasingly influenced by AI. There are numerous opportunities at the intersection of AI and society, and her experience with a non-profit organization is valuable.

Although she may not have been entirely persuaded by my advice, I believe she recognizes that the traditional paths are no longer viable. She needs a multi-year plan to achieve her goals for the next five years (8 years at TRF).

Questions used or to use:

- What change do you want to see for yourself a year from now?
- Go deeper on motivation — why is this important to you?
- How would you feel about this decision or indecision 3 months, or 3 years from now?
- If you did nothing, how would you feel about that 3 years from now?
- Which of these paths will lead you to the change that you want to see for yourself?
- Apart from your professional work — what crossroads are you at in your personal life right now?
