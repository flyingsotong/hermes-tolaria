**February 4, 2025**

At the start of the conversation, Candice expressed nostalgia and a desire to return to journalism/media. She talked about missing several aspects of newsroom life:

1. Missing the "rigor" of newsroom work
2. Missing storytelling and creating content that has societal impact (versus selling products)
3. Missing the "rush" of news work and the daily satisfaction of completing stories
4. Feeling unfulfilled by corporate marketing work where she only has "decks and emails to show" for her work week

Her initial framing was very much about wanting to get "back into" journalism in some form. Through the coaching conversation, it became clear that what she actually wanted wasn't to return to being a journalist, but rather to find a way to make meaningful impact in the media industry using her evolved skillset. You helped her recognize this distinction and reshape her thinking from "how do I get back into journalism?" to "how can I serve newsrooms in a new and valuable way?" This was a great example of how the initially stated need isn't always the real underlying need.

**Key insights for Candice:**

1. She realized she didn't actually want to return to journalism as a reporter/producer, but rather wanted to stay connected to the media industry in a different capacity.
2. She discovered she could combine her media background with her digital marketing experience through information design consulting.
3. She recognized she already had valuable skills and expertise that newsrooms could benefit from, particularly around simplifying complex information.
4. She gained clarity on how to package and position her services through a structured approach (free templates, audits, then custom consulting).

**What worked:**

1. Active listening and pattern recognition — you picked up on key phrases (like "nostalgia") and helped her examine their meaning.
2. Reframing — you helped her see her marketing experience as an asset rather than a departure from journalism.
3. Practical guidance — you broke down abstract ideas into concrete, actionable steps (website creation, free audits, etc.).
4. Future-oriented thinking — you identified opportunities like conferences and milestones to work toward.
5. Validation — you affirmed her expertise while pushing her to think bigger about her potential impact.

**Areas for improvement:**

1. Could have explored her fears/hesitations more deeply before moving to solutions.
2. Could have spent more time helping her articulate her unique value proposition — what specifically makes her approach to information design different.
3. The conversation could have benefited from more specific goal-setting or timeline planning beyond the broad milestones mentioned.
4. Could have asked more about her definition of success or how she would measure the impact of her information design work.

The conversation successfully shifted from "I miss journalism" to "Here's how I can add value to newsrooms in a new way."
