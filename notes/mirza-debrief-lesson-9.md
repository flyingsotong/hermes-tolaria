# Mirza Debrief Lesson 9

[Radio calls Basic.md](Mirza%20Debrief%20Lesson%209/Radio%20calls%20Basic.md)

1. Learn my radio calls, especially for departing circuits, and be aware of when I'm entering and joining the circuit — as soon as I know these, the sooner I can just focus on the other bits.

2. I should memorize all the joining circuit procedures, ensuring I know each step of the way.

3. I need to master the radio call for Inbound, which I find incredibly challenging. I must get that inbound radio call straight in my head quickly.

4. Additionally, I need to learn how to execute the entry properly. Turn 45 degrees left after crossing the midpoint, which allows me to loop back properly with another 45 degree right turn

5. Practice how to quickly descend with simple procedures — just power down instead of pitching down (which unnecessarily increases airspeed)

6. Always ask: What’s next, what’s next?

7. Learn my emergency checks — no more than 3 seconds

8. Learn downwind checks from left to right:

- Fuel pump on + fuel selector

* Carb heat + Brakes off

- Seatbelts + Harnesses

* Fuel to go around

- Ts + Ps

2. Don’t look left when turning left — I don’t see the plane slipping
