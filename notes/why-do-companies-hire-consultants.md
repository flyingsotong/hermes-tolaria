# Why do companies hire consultants

<https://twitter.com/SeanODowd15/status/1592888227148750851>
**Smart summary:** Consultants are not hired for their specialized knowledge, but for their ability to work on special projects that business owners don't have time for. This makes them effective in getting things done short term.
I'm super fortunate to have built a highly profitable, one-man consulting firm
A typical project is $2-$2.5K a day
Since it's so much, the question I get asked the most is:
Why do companies hire you and pay you that much?
Here's why:
First, it's NOT the answer you think it is
Most people assume consultants are hired due to some specialized knowledge they have
E.g: the world expert on XYZ thing
In 99% of cases, that is NOT the value consultants provide
Consultants are useful for something else entirely
You've might have heard the phrase "work on your business, not in your business"
The message is simple: if you spend all day trying to keep the lights on for your business, you don't have time to make your business better
This principle is true at ANY size of business
But, as any business owner knows, it's really hard to do this
They probably have an idea of something that could dramatically improve the business
They just don't have the time to do it
So it stays on their to-do list for a long time
For example: consider this story of an SVP of a Fortune 500.
He had a hunch there was a big upside revenue opportunity to make adjustments to an existing product
Only problem: he didn't have a single person in his organization he could ask to work on his hunch
Literally, everyone who worked for him, and everyone who worked for those people, was already working 50+ hours a week
That's a problem.
The options to solve it aren't that great.
He could:
-Forget about his hunch. That would mean forgoing a potentially big opportunity
-Give the project to someone who is already overworked. That would hurt corporate morale
-He could hire temporary 'reinforcements' to look into the opportunity
Option 3 is what he did, and that's what consultants are
Consultants shouldn't be thought of as super specialized skill experts
We're short-term reinforcements to look into special projects that people who are working "in the business" don't have time to do
It's a different framing than how consultants are typically perceived, but it's what we are!
Happy to answer any questions if helpful!
Adding a great point added by @ConsultingZaddy
Consultants are not influenced by office politics or hidden agendas. As temporary reinforcements, those things don't matter to us
It tends to make consultants more effective in getting things done short term
