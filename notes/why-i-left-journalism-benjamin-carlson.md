Here are the bad habits — or peccadilloes, or perhaps even sins — that I perceived in the industry and feared were growing in me as a byproduct of being a journalist:

1. Cynicism
2. Disingenuousness
3. Thirst for glory over truth
4. Indifference to the consequences of a story
5. Rejection of anything not “new” or “relevant”
6. Desire to control what others think and know
7. Contempt for anyone outside media
8. Herdthink
9. Intentional twisting of words
10. Proudly biased
11. Shamelessly inexpert yet swaggeringly confident
12. Certainty that only you know what really matters
