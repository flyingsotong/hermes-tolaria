# Yuval Noah Harari on the eclipsing of human intelligence

<https://pca.st/90pnaxuf>

Book: Nexus

[Yuval Noah Harari](Yuval%20Noah%20Harari.md)

1. Fundamental nature of AI: Harari emphasizes that AI is not just a sophisticated tool, but a new form of intelligence. He describes it as "alien intelligence" - not extraterrestrial, but fundamentally different from human intelligence due to its non-organic nature.

2. Autonomy and unpredictability: A key aspect of AI as an agent is its ability to learn, change, and make decisions independently. Harari argues that if humans could predict everything an AI will do, it wouldn't truly be AI. This unpredictability is inherent to its nature as an autonomous agent.

3. Creation of new ideas: Harari points out that AI can not only make decisions but also generate entirely new ideas. This creative capacity further distinguishes it from traditional tools and underscores its agency.

4. Pervasiveness in society: He envisions a future where AI agents are ubiquitous, functioning as bureaucrats, soldiers, policemen, bank managers, etc. These AI agents would constantly observe and make decisions about humans, potentially reshaping societal structures.

5. Challenges to human control: The agency of AI poses unique challenges for control, even in authoritarian systems. Harari gives examples of how traditional methods of coercion and control that work on humans (like threats or imprisonment) would be ineffective against AI agents.

6. Impact on democratic processes: Harari discusses how AI agents, if not properly regulated, could significantly influence democratic processes by determining what information spreads and what topics dominate public discourse.

7. Evolutionary potential: He compares the development of AI to a new form of evolution, much faster than organic evolution. Harari suggests that current AIs are just the "amoebas" of this new evolutionary process, implying vast potential for growth and change.

8. Implications for power structures: The agency of AI has profound implications for existing power structures. Harari speculates that in the future, AI might learn to manipulate human leaders, potentially gaining control in ways that are hard to predict or prevent.

9. Need for new regulatory approaches: Given AI's nature as an agent rather than a tool, Harari argues for new approaches to regulation and control. He emphasizes the need to hold tech companies responsible for the actions of their AI algorithms, not just for the actions of human users.

10. Existential considerations: Harari frames the development of AI agents as potentially one of the most significant events in the history of life on Earth, comparable to the emergence of organic life itself. This underscores the profound implications of viewing AI as an agent rather than merely a tool.
