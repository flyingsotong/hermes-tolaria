**April 12, 2024**

**Key insight from the session**

Sahina had a breakthrough around how to approach her post-journalism venture with a former colleague — shifting from a content-first mindset to a need-first mindset.

She and her colleague (both culture reporters, one with a master's in culture studies from CEU) had been thinking about starting a culture-focused media venture. But they were approaching it the traditional journalism way: "we care about culture, therefore we'll create culture content." The conversation helped her see that this starts at the wrong end.

The catalyst was a story she shared about a mayor who had called her asking for a promotional video for a local festival. Her instinct was to produce the video herself. But through the coaching conversation, she realized the mayor's actual need was much bigger — he needed a whole media plan, including connections to cultural influencers she already knew.

Key reframe: her value isn't in creating the content — it's in being the bridge between the mayor (who has a promotional need) and the cultural influencers (who can create better content with her research and context behind them). She sits in between both parties and provides the intelligence that makes everything work.

Her insight: "I think all this time, both of us were basically thinking like we need to be the ones doing all the work, but we don't have to be doing all the work."

Next step: use journalism skills to validate the thesis — talk to the mayor and the influencers, understand their actual needs, confirm whether the opportunity is real.
